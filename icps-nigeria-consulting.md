## ICP 1: Tier-1 Commercial Banks & Financial Services

### Firmographics
- **Sector:** Banking, Investment Banking, Asset Management, Microfinance (Tier 1 & 2)
- **Employee count:** 500 - 15,000+
- **HQ location:** Lagos (Victoria Island, Ikoyi, Marina), with regional offices nationwide
- **Revenue:** N100B+ annually
- **Structure:** Publicly listed on NGX or subsidiary of multinational banking group
- **Ownership:** Private / publicly traded

### Example Accounts
| Company | Employees (est.) | HQ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stanbic IBTC | 2,500+ | Lagos (VI) | Standard Bank Group subsidiary; strong L&D culture |
| Access Bank | 10,000+ | Lagos (VI) | Post-merger integration = heavy talent/change mgmt needs |
| Ecobank Nigeria | 5,000+ | Lagos (VI) | Pan-African group; regional talent harmonization |
| FCMB Group | 4,000+ | Lagos (Marina) | Diversified financial holding; multiple HR functions |
| Quest Merchant Bank | 200-500 | Lagos | Boutique investment bank; lean HR, outsources OD work |

### Target Personas

| Role | Title Variations | Seniority | Why They Buy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HR Director** | Chief People Officer, Group Head HR, ED Human Capital | C-1 / C-2 | Signs off on consulting retainers; owns talent strategy budget |
| **L&D Manager** | Head of Learning, Training Manager, Capability Development Lead | Mid-Senior | Owns training calendar, external facilitator procurement |
| **Talent Management Specialist** | Talent & OD Manager, Head of Talent Acquisition & Development | Mid-Senior | Runs succession planning, leadership pipelines, performance frameworks |
| **Strategy Manager** | Head of Corporate Strategy, Business Transformation Lead | Mid-Senior | Sponsors change management and culture transformation projects |
| **Corporate Comms Manager** | Head of Corporate Affairs, Internal Communications Lead | Mid-Senior | Buys employer branding, internal engagement, culture comms |

### Pain Points
- Post-merger talent integration (Access, Ecobank consolidations)
- CBN regulatory pressure on corporate governance and board effectiveness training
- High attrition of mid-level talent to fintechs and international banks
- Succession planning gaps at MD/ED level
- Need for digital upskilling (AI, data analytics) across workforce
- Annual mandatory compliance training (AML, KYC, risk management)

### Buying Triggers
- New MD/CEO appointment (triggers culture reset)
- CBN circular mandating new governance/training requirements
- Annual budget cycle (Q4 planning for next FY)
- Post-merger or acquisition integration
- Strategic plan refresh (typically 3-5 year cycles)
- Poor employee engagement survey results

### Budget Indicators
- Dedicated L&D budget line (typically 1-3% of personnel costs)
- Existing relationships with Big 4 / global consulting firms (Deloitte, KPMG, McKinsey)
- Active on LinkedIn posting about "people strategy" or "talent transformation"
- Attendance at CIPM (Chartered Institute of Personnel Management) events

### Competitive Landscape
- Phillips Consulting, Workforce Group, KPMG Advisory, Deloitte Human Capital
- Global firms (McKinsey, BCG) for strategy-tier engagements
- Freelance facilitators/coaches for one-off training

## ICP 2: Government Agencies & Regulatory Bodies

### Firmographics
- **Sector:** Federal government agencies, regulatory bodies, state-owned enterprises
- **Employee count:** 500 - 30,000+
- **HQ location:** Abuja (primarily), Lagos (some operational HQs)
- **Budget:** Federal appropriation; procurement via BPP (Bureau of Public Procurement) guidelines
- **Structure:** Statutory bodies, parastatals, ministries

### Example Accounts
| Company | Employees (est.) | HQ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| NNPC Ltd | 7,000+ | Abuja | Recently corporatized (2022 PIA); undergoing massive HR transformation |
| Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) | 8,000+ | Abuja | Tax modernization driving digital upskilling + change management |
| Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) | 6,000+ | Abuja | Regulator; invests heavily in staff development and governance training |
| Galaxy Backbone | 500+ | Abuja | Govt IT backbone; needs tech talent development |
| Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC) | 800+ | Abuja | Telecoms regulator; capacity building mandate |

### Target Personas

| Role | Title Variations | Seniority | Why They Buy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HR Director** | Director of Human Resources, Director Admin & HR | Director-level (GL 17+) | Controls HR consulting procurement; signs off on retainers |
| **L&D Manager** | Head Training & Development, Director Capacity Building | Deputy Director+ | Owns staff development programs, coordinates external trainers |
| **Strategy Manager** | Director of Strategy & Planning, Head Reform Coordination | Director-level | Drives institutional reform, change management, strategic plans |
| **Corporate Comms Manager** | Head Public Affairs, Director Information | Deputy Director+ | Manages internal comms, culture change campaigns, branding |

### Pain Points
- Transition from civil service culture to performance-driven culture (especially newly corporatized entities like NNPC)
- Mandatory capacity building requirements under federal directives
- Brain drain of technical talent to private sector and diaspora
- Leadership development for succession (aging senior workforce)
- Digital transformation readiness (most staff lack digital fluency)
- Need for change management during institutional reform programs

### Buying Triggers
- New DG/CEO/Minister appointment (common after elections)
- Corporatization or restructuring (NNPC model spreading to others)
- World Bank / AfDB / donor-funded institutional strengthening projects
- Federal Executive Council directives on capacity building
- Annual training plan procurement (typically Q1-Q2)
- BPP-compliant RFQ/RFP issuance cycles

### Procurement Notes
- Must be registered with BPP and on agency's vendor list
- Procurement often via competitive bidding (3 quotes minimum)
- Payment cycles: 60-120 days (budget delays common)
- Relationships with Permanent Secretary / DG office critical for sole-source
- Tax Clearance Certificate (TCC), CAC registration, PENCOM compliance required
- Donor-funded projects (World Bank, AfDB) may have separate procurement rules with shorter cycles

### Budget Indicators
- Active capacity building line items in annual budget
- Engagement with international development partners (GIZ, DFID/FCDO, USAID)
- Participation in AAPAM, CIPD, CIPM conferences
- Published strategic plans mentioning "human capital development"

## ICP 3: Pension Fund Administrators & Insurance

### Firmographics
- **Sector:** Pension Fund Administrators (PFAs), Pension Fund Custodians, Insurance
- **Employee count:** 200 - 3,000
- **HQ location:** Lagos and Abuja
- **Regulator:** PenCom (National Pension Commission)
- **Structure:** Licensed financial institutions; compliance-heavy

### Example Accounts
| Company | Employees (est.) | HQ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trust Fund Pensions | 500+ | Abuja | One of largest PFAs; government-adjacent client base |
| ARM Pensions | 400+ | Lagos | Part of ARM Group; sophisticated HR function |
| Leadway Pensure | 600+ | Lagos | Large PFA with national branch network |
| AIICO Pension | 300+ | Lagos | Insurance group subsidiary |
| Stanbic IBTC Pension | 400+ | Lagos | Banking group PFA arm |

### Target Personas

| Role | Title Variations | Seniority | Why They Buy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HR Manager** | Head of Human Resources, People & Culture Manager | Mid-Senior | Manages lean HR team; outsources specialized OD work |
| **L&D Manager** | Training Coordinator, Head Learning & Development | Mid | Coordinates PenCom mandatory training + soft skills |
| **Talent Specialist** | Talent Management Officer, HR Business Partner | Mid | Runs performance management, competency frameworks |

### Pain Points
- PenCom mandatory training and certification requirements for all staff
- Branch network staff development (geographically dispersed)
- Customer service excellence programs (reputation-driven industry)
- Leadership pipeline thin at mid-management level
- High compliance burden; need governance and ethics training
- Digital transformation of pension administration

### Buying Triggers
- PenCom circular mandating new training requirements
- Annual training needs assessment (Q4)
- New branch openings requiring onboarding programs
- Merger/acquisition activity among PFAs (industry consolidation ongoing)
- Customer complaint spikes triggering service excellence programs

- IPO preparation or post-IPO governance upgrades
- Board-mandated leadership assessment/succession review

## ICP 5: Fintechs & Tech Companies (Growth Stage)

### Firmographics
- **Sector:** Fintech, Payments, Digital Banking, SaaS, E-commerce
- **Employee count:** 200 - 2,000 (fast-growing)
- **HQ location:** Lagos (Yaba, Lekki, VI), some dual HQ with London/SF
- **Funding:** Series B+ or profitable
- **Structure:** Venture-backed or bootstrapped; scaling rapidly

### Example Accounts
| Company | Employees (est.) | HQ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Flutterwave | 1,000+ | Lagos / SF | Payments; scaling people ops |
| Moniepoint (TeamApt) | 1,500+ | Lagos | Agent banking; massive field force management |
| Interswitch | 1,000+ | Lagos (VI) | Established fintech; mature HR function |
| Paystack (Stripe) | 300+ | Lagos | Acquired by Stripe; needs local OD despite global parent |
| Kuda Bank | 500+ | Lagos / London | Digital bank; culture-first approach |

### Target Personas

| Role | Title Variations | Seniority | Why They Buy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HR Manager** | Head of People, People Operations Lead, VP People | Senior | Building HR function from scratch; needs external expertise |
| **L&D Manager** | People Development Lead, Head of Learning | Mid-Senior | Standing up first formal L&D programs |
| **Talent Specialist** | Talent Partner, People Business Partner | Mid | Scaling hiring + retention in competitive market |

### Pain Points
- Transitioning from startup culture to structured people operations
- Manager readiness (ICs promoted to leads without management training)
- High attrition (6-18 month tenure common)
- Need for performance management frameworks beyond OKRs
- Founder/CEO coaching and executive team alignment
- Remote/hybrid workforce culture across multiple countries

### Buying Triggers
- Series B/C funding (triggers "professionalize HR" mandate from board/investors)
- Headcount crossing 200, 500, or 1,000 thresholds
- First CHRO/VP People hire (they bring in consultants immediately)
- Employee engagement crisis or Glassdoor reputation issues
- International expansion requiring cross-cultural management training

## Composite Scoring Model

Score each prospect 0-100 to prioritize outreach:

| Signal | Points | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Employee count 500+** | 15 | Higher training volume, larger budgets |
| **Employee count 200-499** | 8 | Viable but smaller engagements |
| **HQ in Lagos or Abuja** | 10 | Accessible for in-person delivery |
| **Financial services / banking** | 12 | Highest consulting spend per employee |
| **Government / regulatory body** | 10 | Recurring capacity building mandates |
| **Known existing client** | 15 | Highest conversion; expansion opportunity |
| **Target persona identified by name** | 10 | Named contact = actionable lead |
| **Recent leadership change (CEO/DG/CHRO)** | 10 | Triggers new consulting procurement |
| **Active job posting for HR/L&D roles** | 8 | Signal of HR investment |
| **Published strategic plan mentioning talent/people** | 7 | Budget intent signal |
| **Attended CIPM/SHRM/industry HR event** | 5 | Engaged buyer persona |
| **Donor-funded project active** | 8 | Separate budget, faster procurement |
| **Recent M&A or restructuring** | 10 | Integration = consulting goldmine |

**Tiers:**
- **80-100 (Hot):** Prioritize for direct outreach within 1 week
- **60-79 (Warm):** Nurture via content + event invitations
- **40-59 (Cool):** Add to awareness campaigns, monitor for triggers
- **0-39 (Watch):** Park; revisit quarterly for trigger events

> "PenCom's new training requirements for 2026 are creating urgency. We deliver PenCom-aligned programs that also build real capability — not just check the compliance box."

### Conglomerates & Industrials
> "As [Company] expands into [new sector/geography], the leadership bench needs to scale with it. We've helped similar groups build internal academies that reduce external hiring dependency by 30%."

### Fintechs & Tech
> "Most fintechs hit a people wall between 200 and 500 employees — ICs become managers overnight with no training. We run a 90-day manager acceleration program designed for fast-scaling African tech companies."
