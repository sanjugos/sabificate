## KEY STRUCTURE DECISIONS (Already Proposed by Mark)

### Equity: 30/30/30 + 10% Option Pool
- Equal ownership across all three founders
- 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff, monthly thereafter
- Double-trigger acceleration on sale
- 10% option pool reserved at formation (dilution shared equally)
- Advisor equity capped at 5% aggregate from the pool
- **20% dilution floor** — no founder can be diluted below 20% without their consent

### Entity: Dual Structure
- **US:** Delaware LLC (converts to C-Corp at institutional priced round or DFI requirement)
- **Nigeria:** Operating subsidiary registered with Corporate Affairs Commission
- Nigerian subsidiary wholly owned by US holdco (single cap table)
- Nigerian subsidiary owns learner data and customer contracts
- Platform IP owned by US entity, licensed to Nigerian subsidiary at arm's length

### Revenue: Role Fees Before Profit Distribution
- No fees pre-revenue (confirmed from your meeting)
- Fees activate when revenue/grants cover them
- Fees rank ahead of profit distributions
- Profit distributions follow equal equity (33.3% each after option pool)
- NGN-denominated for Nigerian roles, USD for US roles

### Nigeria-First Covenant (replaces "revenue preference")
- Nigeria is the founding market
- Gbitse holds decision authority over Nigerian government relations, regulatory, cultural adaptation, public representation
- No founder makes commitments affecting Nigerian market without Gbitse's sign-off
- Geographic expansion requires supermajority (2 of 3) approval
- This is governance-based recognition, not economic preference

### Exit Protections
- Good leaver: keeps vested shares, company option to repurchase at fair value
- Bad leaver: forfeits unvested, sells vested at lower of cost or fair value (after 90-day notice + 30-day cure)
- **No damages, no repayment, no liability for leaving** — equity only
- Buyback payable over 12-24 months (no cash crisis)
- Buy-sell via independent appraisal, NOT shotgun (protects the less-wealthy partner)
