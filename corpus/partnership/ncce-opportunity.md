## Question / Scope

Can SABIficate build and deploy a CPD microlearning academy for Nigeria's 200+ colleges of education via a direct relationship with the NCCE head, using their existing Blackboard LMS and a revenue-share model?

## Findings

### The Contact

Gbitse Barrow has a direct, personal relationship with the head of the **National Commission for Colleges of Education (NCCE)**, Nigeria's regulator for all colleges of education. She is the first non-professor and first woman appointed to the position. The NCCE regulates 200+ institutions (federal and state government-owned). They hold a **10-year Blackboard LMS license** with very low adoption.

**Source:** Team meeting 2026-07-08 (Gbitse, Mark, Sanju). Contact made at a Blackboard conference in the US.

### The Proposal

Build the **NCCE Academy** — CPD microlearning courseware for lecturers and administrators, deployed on their existing Blackboard LMS, revenue-shared between NCCE and SABIficate.

- **Target users:** Lecturers and administrative staff across 200+ colleges of education
- **Price points:** Must be affordable for individual lecturers paying out of pocket (not institutional procurement initially)
- **Revenue model:** Individual lecturer purchases for CPD/CEU credits, revenue share with NCCE
- **Long-term funding:** World Bank, Universal Basic Education Commission (UBEC), Nigerian government education funding — approach after 1-2 years of proven commercial viability
- **Content:** AI-powered rapid production, positioned as "proprietary system" — SMEs control curriculum, AI does the heavy lifting behind the scenes, can iterate endlessly without large teams
- **Competitive advantage:** NCCE has the infrastructure (Blackboard, 7-year remaining license), SABIficate provides the missing content

### Accreditation Strategy

**Key question:** Who accredits CPD content when the accrediting body (NCCE) is the client?

**Paths being explored:**
1. **Catholic University of Nigeria** — via Father George (Psycho Spiritual Institute), has deep relationship with the university. Gbitse to approach.
2. **Prior precedent:** Gbitse previously signed an MOU with a monotechnic proprietor (same NCCE contact, 10 years ago) — certificates issued in monotechnic's name, 10% revenue share, worked for 3 years.
3. **NCCE self-accreditation** — their Director of Accreditation could review SABIficate content through existing channels.

**Framing:** "Affiliated with" language, not formal accreditation claims. Build to standards, advisory board of PhDs, honest about being new. Never claim more than evidence supports.
