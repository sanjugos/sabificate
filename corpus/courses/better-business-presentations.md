## 2. PRESENTATION FRAMEWORKS

### 2.1 Minto Pyramid Principle

**Origin:** Developed by Barbara Minto, the first female post-MBA hire at McKinsey, in the 1970s. Published as "The Pyramid Principle: Logic in Writing and Thinking."

**Core Concept:** Answer-first communication. Present the main conclusion first, followed by supporting arguments, then data. Ideas map as a hierarchy with detail increasing downward.

**Why It Works:** Matches how decision-makers process information -- they want the answer first, then evaluate supporting logic. Widely used across consulting, corporate strategy, finance, and executive communication.

**Application to Nigerian Context:** Particularly relevant because Nigerian business communication tends to move from "general overview before gradually moving to specific details" (Commisceo Global). The Pyramid Principle can help Nigerian professionals adapt their natural communication style to boardroom expectations while respecting the cultural norm of context-setting.

**Source:** https://www.betterup.com/blog/minto-pyramid; https://managementconsulted.com/pyramid-principle/

### 2.2 SCQA Framework (Situation-Complication-Question-Answer)

**Origin:** Popularized by Barbara Minto in "The Pyramid Principle" (1987). Used extensively at McKinsey, Bain, and BCG.

**Components:**
- **Situation:** Context everyone agrees on
- **Complication:** The problem creating urgency
- **Question:** What naturally arises from the complication
- **Answer:** The recommendation/solution

**Why It Works:** Respects how people think -- audiences need context before conclusions, tension before answers. Works in presentations, emails, memos, and stakeholder meetings.

**Source:** https://managementconsulted.com/scqa-framework/

### 2.3 Assertion-Evidence Slide Design

**Origin:** Developed by Michael Alley at Penn State University for engineering/science communication, now applied broadly to business presentations.

**Core Concept:** Replace traditional "topic headline + bullet points" slides with two components: (1) a concise assertion sentence as the headline, (2) visual evidence supporting that assertion (photograph, chart, diagram, video clip).

**Research Evidence:**
- Assertion-evidence slides produce statistically significant improvement in comprehension and recall
- Misconceptions are approximately 10x higher with traditional PowerPoint vs. assertion-evidence
- Students using assertion-evidence understand content better during preparation (deeper thinking)
- Lower perceived cognitive workload than traditional PowerPoint (ScienceDirect, 2025)
- Presenters project more confidence using this approach

**Source:** https://www.assertion-evidence.com/; https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2307187725001701; https://writing.engr.psu.edu/research.html

### 2.4 Nancy Duarte's Resonate Framework

**Origin:** Nancy Duarte, CEO of Duarte Design (created Al Gore's "An Inconvenient Truth" presentation). Published 2010.

**Core Concept:** The presenter is the mentor, the audience is the hero. Presentations should create an experience blending information with story. Effective presentations oscillate between "what is" and "what could be."

**Method:** Reverse-engineered structure from successful presentations by Steve Jobs, Martin Luther King Jr., and others. Identifies a repeatable pattern of moving between current state and desired future.

**Key Insight:** A presentation should not just inform -- it should move people to action.

**Source:** https://www.duarte.com/resources/books/resonate/

### 2.5 STAR Method for Business Storytelling

**Components:** Situation, Task, Action, Result

**Application Beyond Interviews:** Now a go-to strategy for presentations, team meetings, performance reviews, and leadership communication. Especially effective for status updates and senior stakeholder meetings.

**Research:** MIT Sloan School of Management research demonstrates structured narratives significantly improve information retention and decision-making quality.

**Source:** https://www.stratechi.com/star-method-framework/

### 2.6 Carmine Gallo's TED Framework (Talk Like TED)

**Structure:** Three parts (emotional, novel, memorable) x three principles each = 9 secrets:
1. Unleash the master within (passion)
2. Master the art of storytelling
3. Have a conversation (delivery)
4. Teach something new (novelty)
5. Deliver jaw-dropping moments
6. Lighten up (humor)
7. Stick to the 18-minute rule
8. Paint a mental picture with multisensory experiences
9. Stay in your lane (authenticity)

**Key Takeaway:** The three-part structure is central. Storytelling and structure of three are the most important "secrets."

**Source:** https://www.carminegallo.com/talk-like-ted/

### 2.7 SUCCESs Framework (Made to Stick)

**Origin:** Chip and Dan Heath, Stanford. Published 2007.

**Six Principles for Sticky Ideas:**
- **S**implicity -- find the core message
- **U**nexpectedness -- create surprise to get attention
- **C**oncreteness -- make ideas tangible
- **C**redibility -- establish trustworthiness
- **E**motions -- make people feel something
- **S**tories -- package messages in narrative

**Application:** Directly applicable to presentation content design -- every slide/section should pass the "stickiness" test.

**Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Made_to_Stick

### 2.8 Cognitive Load and Slide Design Science

**Key Research Findings:**
- Working memory can handle only 4-7 items at once (Miller's Law)
- Slides with 3 or fewer bullet points and 20 or fewer words are more effective
- With a single bullet point, students engage comprehensively; engagement drops with density
- Short bullet points / key words improve critical information absorption
- Albert Mehrabian's research: communication is 55% non-verbal, 38% vocal tone, 7% words

**Source:** https://ojs.library.queensu.ca/index.php/PCEEA/article/view/14872/9726; https://www.researchgate.net/publication/242099673

## 3. NIGERIAN BUSINESS COMMUNICATION NORMS

### 3.1 Hierarchical Structure and Respect for Authority

- Nigerian workplaces have strong hierarchical structures where age, seniority, and position command respect
- Challenging authority directly is generally frowned upon
- Senior leaders hold clear authority; experience-based leadership is valued
- Decision-making flows from the top; direct key points to those in authority
- Younger people may lower their heads when addressing senior individuals as a respect gesture

**Implication for Presentations:** Always acknowledge seniority in the room. Address key decision-makers directly. Frame recommendations as support for leadership vision, not challenges to it.

### 3.2 Communication Style

- Expressively communicated with animated gestures and vocal emphasis
- Passionate debate doesn't indicate conflict
- Humour and storytelling are frequently used to illustrate points
- Yoruba speakers often use proverbs and sayings ("Owe") to add depth
- Igbo culture: "proverbs are the palm oil with which words are eaten" (Chinua Achebe)
- Indirect communication for sensitive topics; direct for practical matters
- Conversations typically begin with warm greetings and context before specifics

### 3.3 Titles and Formality

- Use titles: "Mr.," "Mrs.," "Dr.," "Chief," "Mazi," "Uncle," "Auntie," etc.
- Professional formal attire is standard (conservative suits)
- Exchange business cards using right hand or both hands
- Warm handshakes with smiles are expected

### 3.4 Meeting Culture

- Personal relationships and trust are essential before business partnerships
- Expect extended greetings, inquiries about health and family before business
- Accepting refreshments is customary before getting down to business
- Allow flexibility in discussion flow rather than rigid agenda adherence
- Individual greetings are important -- don't rush introductions

### 3.5 Storytelling Traditions in Nigerian Business

**Yoruba Tradition:** Storytellers employ dramatic expressions, songs, and proverbs. "Owe" (proverbs) add layers of meaning, making stories more impactful and thought-provoking.

**Igbo Tradition:** High value on the art of conversation and eloquence. Proverbs are integral to persuasive communication.

**Business Application:** Nigerian professionals who skillfully integrate proverbs and storytelling into presentations gain credibility and engagement. This is a differentiator SABIficate can teach that Western courses completely miss.

### 3.6 Virtual Presentation Considerations (Nigeria-Specific)

- Test internet strength before meetings (platforms like Zoom require strong connections)
- Power outages are a major challenge -- ensure backup power
- Join via software not browser for better quality
- Use wired connections when possible
- Know the platform before the meeting to reduce anxiety

**Sources:** https://commisceo-global.com/country-guides/nigeria-guide/; https://www.worldbusinessculture.com/country-profiles/nigeria/business-communication-style/; https://businessculture.org/nigerian-business-culture/; https://insight.ng/tech/virtual-meeting-etiquette/

## 4. WHY PRESENTATIONS FAIL & SUCCEED

### 4.1 Failure Statistics

- **80% of presentations fail** to deliver their objective (Phillip Khan-Panni, UK champion business speaker)
- **80% of B2B sales presentations fail** -- not because the product isn't good, but because the presentation doesn't resonate (OnboardCRM)
- **4 out of 5 businesspeople** shift focus away from the speaker during presentations (Perzi.com survey)
- **90% of pre-presentation anxiety** is attributed to lack of practice
- **73% of working professionals** experience anxiety related to public speaking
- **75% of the population** is affected by glossophobia (fear of public speaking)
- **Nearly 90%** of businesspeople say strong narrative/story is critical for engagement
- **64% of Nigerian bank PR/communication officers** have not received formal communication training (IJRISS)
- **48% of respondents** believe poor crisis handling in Nigerian banks is due to lack of preparedness

### 4.2 Common Failure Modes

1. **Data overload / information dumps** -- dry monologues that lose audiences
2. **Text-heavy slides** -- cluttered screens cause audiences to disengage
3. **No audience customization** -- generic one-size-fits-all approaches
4. **Lack of narrative structure** -- no story arc to maintain engagement
5. **Poor preparation** -- the single biggest anxiety driver
6. **Over-designed slides** -- busy imagery and excessive animation distract
7. **No clear ask/conclusion** -- audience leaves without knowing what to do
8. **Ignoring cultural context** -- missing formality norms, title usage, relationship-building

### 4.3 Nigerian-Specific Failure Modes

- **Language intelligibility issues** in international business contexts (SCIRP research)
- **Deficiency in intercultural communication skills** leading to business transaction failures
- **Absence of corporate communication policies** leading to stakeholder disengagement
- **Lack of training** -- the fundamental gap that SABIficate addresses

### 4.4 Success Factors

1. **Strong narrative/story structure** (90% of businesspeople cite this as critical)
2. **Audience-tailored content** (stakeholder mapping, persona adaptation)
3. **Visual evidence over bullet points** (assertion-evidence approach: 10x fewer misconceptions)
4. **Practice and preparation** (reduces 90% of anxiety)
5. **Confident body language** (open stances, purposeful gestures, direct eye contact)
6. **Cultural awareness** (titles, greetings, proverbs in Nigerian context)
7. **Clear call to action** (what should the audience do after?)
8. **Simplicity** (3 or fewer bullet points per slide, 20 or fewer words)

**Sources:** https://janicetomich.com/80-of-presentations-fail-do-yours/; https://blog.interamark.com/six-reasons-why-business-presentations-fail; https://www.crossrivertherapy.com/public-speaking-statistics

## 10. RECOMMENDED MODULE STRUCTURE

### Module 1: Structure Your Story (The Presentation Blueprint)
**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply the Minto Pyramid Principle to structure any business presentation
- Use the SCQA framework to create compelling openings
- Identify and eliminate common structural failures in presentations
- Adapt presentation structure for Nigerian business contexts

**Lesson Topics:**
1. **Why Your Last Presentation Failed** (diagnostic) -- 80% fail rate, common failure modes, self-assessment artifact
2. **Answer First: The Pyramid Principle** -- Minto framework, assertion-argument-data hierarchy, practice with real scenarios
3. **The SCQA Opening** -- Situation-Complication-Question-Answer framework for hooking your audience
4. **Storytelling for Nigerian Business** -- STAR method, Duarte's "what is vs. what could be," integrating Nigerian storytelling traditions (proverbs, narrative culture)
5. **The One-Page Presentation Plan** -- Template artifact: audience, objective, key message, 3 supporting points, call to action

**Artifact:** Restructured outline of a real presentation using the Pyramid Principle + SCQA opening

**Source Basis:** Minto Pyramid Principle, SCQA framework, Duarte Resonate, STAR method, Nigerian storytelling traditions, cognitive load research

---

### Module 2: Design Slides That Communicate (Visual Design for Impact)
**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply the assertion-evidence approach to replace bullet-point slides
- Design data visualizations that tell a story, not just display numbers
- Use the SUCCESs framework to make slides memorable
- Create presentation visuals on mobile devices and low-bandwidth tools

**Lesson Topics:**
1. **Kill Your Bullet Points** -- Assertion-evidence design, cognitive load science (4-7 items, 3 bullets/20 words max), before/after examples
2. **Data That Tells a Story** -- Tufte's principles (data-ink ratio, chartjunk), Duarte's DataStory approach, choosing the right chart type
3. **Making Ideas Stick** -- SUCCESs framework (Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories) applied to slide design
4. **Visual Design Essentials** -- Color, typography, white space, image selection, Presentation Zen principles, accessibility
5. **Mobile-First Slide Creation** -- Tools for Nigerian professionals (Google Slides, Canva, PowerPoint mobile), designing for projection and screen sharing

**Artifact:** Redesigned slide deck (before/after) using assertion-evidence format with proper data visualization

**Source Basis:** Assertion-evidence research (Alley, Penn State), Tufte data visualization principles, Made to Stick SUCCESs framework, Presentation Zen, slide:ology

---

### Module 3: Deliver with Confidence (Presence and Performance)
**Learning Objectives:**
- Manage presentation anxiety using evidence-based techniques
- Project executive presence through body language, voice, and eye contact
- Handle Q&A sessions, interruptions, and difficult audiences
- Deliver effective virtual presentations (Nigeria-specific challenges)

**Lesson Topics:**
1. **Conquering Presentation Anxiety** -- Glossophobia statistics (75% affected), preparation as anxiety reducer (90%), breathing techniques, reframing nervousness as energy
2. **Body Language That Commands Respect** -- Mehrabian's 55/38/7, open posture, purposeful gestures, eye contact, Nigerian cultural considerations (respect gestures, formality)
3. **Voice and Pacing** -- Vocal variety, strategic pauses, the 18-minute rule (Gallo/TED), managing pace for engagement
4. **Handling Q&A Like a Pro** -- Anticipating objections, bridging techniques, maintaining composure, respecting hierarchy in Nigerian settings
5. **Virtual Presentation Mastery** -- Camera positioning, lighting, internet backup, Zoom/Teams best practices, power outage contingencies for Nigeria

**Artifact:** Recorded 3-minute presentation opening (self-evaluation using provided rubric)

**Source Basis:** Public speaking anxiety research, Mehrabian communication research, Talk Like TED, Nigerian virtual meeting etiquette research, Dale Carnegie methodology

---

### Module 4: Know Your Audience (Executive Communication and Stakeholder Mapping)
**Learning Objectives:**
- Conduct audience analysis before any presentation
- Adapt content for different stakeholder types (C-suite, technical, client, board)
- Navigate Nigerian corporate hierarchy in presentations
- Customize presentations for different Nigerian business contexts (banking, oil & gas, tech, public sector)

**Lesson Topics:**
1. **The Audience Analysis Framework** -- Who are they? What do they know? What do they need? What objections will they have? Stakeholder power/interest mapping
2. **Presenting to Executives** -- Answer-first communication, respecting their time, the "elevator pitch" version, Nigerian title and hierarchy protocols
3. **Presenting to Technical Audiences** -- Balancing detail with clarity, using data appropriately, avoiding jargon overload
4. **Presenting Across Nigerian Business Sectors** -- Banking/finance (post-CBN recapitalization context), oil & gas, technology/fintech, public sector, multinational environments
5. **The Corporate Culture Decoder** -- Reading the room, adapting to formal vs. informal settings, when to use storytelling vs. data, proverb usage for engagement

**Artifact:** Audience analysis worksheet completed for a real upcoming presentation

**Source Basis:** Stakeholder mapping methodology, Nigerian business communication norms, CIPM/CIPAN requirements, sector-specific case studies

---

### Module 5: The Presentation Portfolio (Putting It All Together)
**Learning Objectives:**
- Integrate structure, design, delivery, and audience analysis into a complete presentation
- Build a professional presentation portfolio demonstrating competency
- Apply lessons to real-world Nigerian business scenarios
- Develop a personal presentation improvement plan

**Lesson Topics:**
1. **The Executive Briefing** -- Build a 5-minute executive summary presentation for a real business scenario (banking, consulting, or sector of choice)
2. **The Client Pitch** -- Structure a persuasive client presentation using Pyramid Principle + Duarte storytelling + assertion-evidence slides
3. **The Data Presentation** -- Present financial or operational data using Tufte principles + DataStory approach
4. **The Crisis Communication Brief** -- Prepare a 3-minute crisis communication statement (drawing on Nigerian bank PR training gap research)
5. **Your Presentation Improvement Plan** -- Self-assessment, identified growth areas, 30-day practice plan, portfolio compilation

**Artifact:** Complete presentation portfolio containing: restructured outline (M1), redesigned slides (M2), recorded delivery (M3), audience analysis (M4), and capstone presentation (M5)

**Source Basis:** Integration of all frameworks; CIPM professional standards; portfolio-based assessment methodology
