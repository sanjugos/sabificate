## 1. PRIMARY REGULATORY SOURCES

### 1.1 Revised Regulation on the Administration of Retirement and Terminal Benefits (March 2022)

- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **Date:** March 2022 (maiden comprehensive review since initial issuance in 2007)
- **Official PDF:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/REVISED-REGULATION-ON-THE-ADMINISTRATION-OF-RETIREMENT-AND-TERMINAL-BENEFITS-APPROVED-MAR-2022.pdf
- **Proshare Analysis (Part I):** https://proshare.co/articles/pencom-revises-regulation-on-retirement-and-terminal-benefits
- **Proshare Analysis (Part II):** https://proshare.co/articles/pencom-revises-regulation-on-retirement-and-terminal-benefits-part-ii
- **Proshare Analysis (Part III):** https://proshare.co/articles/pencom-revises-regulation-on-retirement-and-terminal-benefits-part-iii
- **KPMG Summary:** https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/ng/pdf/revised-regulation-on-the-administration-of-retirement-and-terminal-benefits.pdf
- **Key Content:**
  - Covers Programmed Withdrawal (PW) and Retiree Life Annuity (RLA) retirement benefit modes
  - Introduces provisions on Pension Enhancement, Voluntary Contributions, and Temporary Access to RSA due to loss of job
  - Covers Payment under the Micro Pension Plan
  - Mandates PFAs process retirement benefit requests and forward to PenCom for no-objection within 2 working days of receipt of complete documentation
  - Administrative sanctions introduced to guard against delays in payment of retirement and terminal benefits
  - Lump sum withdrawal: retirees may withdraw up to 25% of RSA balance provided remaining balance funds a reasonable monthly pension
  - Standard Retirement Benefit Computation (SRBC) template: automated template developed by PenCom for PFAs to calculate lump sum and monthly/quarterly pension using age, gender, final salary, and RSA balance
  - Monthly Pension formula (Programmed Withdrawal): RSA Balance / Number of Expected Monthly Payments (Life Expectancy in Months), using PenCom-determined mortality (actuarial) tables
  - From start of CPS to end of 2022, PenCom approved en-bloc payment of retirement benefits to 145,313 retirees totalling N38.71 billion

### 1.2 Circular on Approval and Payment of Benefits by PFAs (March 2025)

- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **Date:** March 2025; effective June 1, 2025
- **Official PDF:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/CIRCULAR-ON-APROVAL-AND-PAYMENTS-OF-BENEFITS-BY-PFAS.pdf
- **KPMG Flash Alert:** https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/gms-flash-alert/flash-alert-2025-078.html
- **Key Content:**
  - Effective June 1, 2025, Licensed Pension Fund Operators now responsible for approving qualifying benefits applications for RSA holders
  - PFAs empowered to independently process and approve benefit applications including retirement benefits, voluntary contributions, mortgage equity contributions, and additional benefits without prior PenCom approval
  - PFCs required to process payments within 24 hours of receiving instructions from PFAs
  - Eliminates the need for PenCom's pre-approval, speeding up the benefits application and payment process
  - PenCom approved amendments to five regulatory instruments on benefits administration to support the change

### 1.3 Regulation on Investment of Pension Fund Assets (February 2019, Revised September 2025, Amended February 2026)

- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **Original 2019 Regulation PDF:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/AMENDED-INVESTMENT-REGULATION-FEBRUARY-2019-.pdf
- **Revised 2025 Regulation PDF:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/The-Revised-Regulation-on-Investment-of-Pension-Fund-Assets.pdf
- **PensionNigeria Highlights:** https://www.pensionnigeria.com/pension-news/key-highlights-of-the-revised-regulation-on-investment-of-pension-fund-assets/
- **Key Content:**

  **Multi-Fund Structure (6 Funds):**

  | Fund | Target Contributors | Risk Profile |
  |------|-------------------|--------------|
  | Fund I | Active contributors who formally request; highest equity exposure | Aggressive |
  | Fund II | Active contributors aged 49 and below (default fund) | Growth |
  | Fund III | Active contributors aged 50 and above | Conservative |
  | Fund IV | RSA retirees only | Capital Preservation |
  | Fund V | Micro Pension Contributors | Flexible |
  | Fund VI | Active and Retiree -- Sharia Compliant | Ethical/Sharia |

  **2019 Key Provisions:**
  - Minimum 2.5% of AUM in alternative assets (Infrastructure, Private Equity, Real Estate Investment) for Funds I, II, and V
  - Permissible instruments: MBS, REITs (SEC-registered), Supranational Bonds (MDFO-issued), Private Equity funds, Infrastructure funds, money market instruments, commercial papers
  - Minimum credit rating "A" for bonds and debt securities (by at least two rating companies)
  - Minimum credit rating "BBB" for bank instruments
  - Prohibition on margin trading and borrowing/lending of pension fund assets
  - PFAs cannot hold more than 25% of instruments issued by a single corporate entity

  **September 2025 Revision:**
  - Stronger provisions on shareholder voting, engagement, transparency, and accountability
  - Increased Infrastructure Fund allocation limits to promote impact investing and inflation hedging
  - Considered one of the most progressive governance-focused reforms in Nigeria's pension regulation history

  **February 2026 Addendum:**
  - RSA Fund I ordinary shares limit raised from 30% to 35%
  - RSA Fund II ordinary shares limit raised from 25% to 33%
  - RSA Fund III ordinary shares limit raised from 10% to 15%
  - Changes address structural gaps in investable asset universe and buildup of excess liquidity

  - PFAs required to establish transparent complaint processes in line with PenCom's minimum standards
  - Unresolved PFA complaints can be escalated to PenCom for review

### 1.8 RSA Transfer System (RTS) Guidelines

- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **PensionNigeria -- Understanding the RSA Transfer Process:** https://www.pensionnigeria.com/pension-news/understanding-the-retirement-savings-account-transfer-process-a-publication-by-pencom/
- **PensionNigeria -- Review of the RSA Transfer System:** https://www.pensionnigeria.com/pension-news/review-of-the-retirement-savings-account-transfer-system-a-publication-by-pencom/
- **PensionNigeria -- FAQs on RSA Transfer Window:** https://www.pensionnigeria.com/pension-news/faqs-on-rsa-transfer-window/
- **LegalNaija Analysis:** https://legalnaija.com/understanding-transfer-window-for/
- **Key Content:**
  - RSA Transfer launched November 16, 2020
  - RSA holder may transfer RSA from one PFA to another only once in every 365 days (12 calendar months)
  - Pre-requisite: personal details (Biometrics & Biodata) must be recaptured and updated on Enhanced Contributor Registration System (ECRS)
  - Process: RSA holder approaches Receiving PFA to submit transfer request
  - Transfer requests batched and processed quarterly (March, June, September, December)
  - Only requests received by second month of transfer quarter processed within that quarter
  - Transferring PFA transfers all funds in RSA to Receiving PFA under PenCom supervision
  - PenCom and Receiving PFA notify RSA holder upon completion

- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom), developed with PenOp
- **Date:** Operational April 1, 2025; employer transition deadline June 1, 2025
- **PenCom Official Page:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/pension-contribution-remittance-system-pcrs/
- **Premium Times Coverage:** https://www.premiumtimesng.com/business/business-news/784190-pencom-unveils-automated-pension-remittance-platform.html

- **PensionRemit Portal:** https://www.pensionremit.ng/
- **Key Content:**
  - Digital platform automating pension contribution remittances; replaces manual processing
  - All contributions routed through approved Payment Solution Service Providers (PSSPs)
  - PSSPs validate employee information before funds credited
  - System integrates directly with PenCom's database; only compliant remittances with matching RSA PINs and PFA codes processed
  - Eliminates paperwork, reduces errors, increases transparency
  - Resolves long-standing issues related to uncredited pension contributions and verification delays
  - No additional cost to employers

### 4.1 Kaduna Electricity Distribution Company (KEDC) -- Prolonged Pension Remittance Default

- **ICIR Investigation:** https://www.icirnigeria.org/kaduna-electric-shock-50-months-pension-remittance-default-leaves-staff-in-distress/
- **Daily Post Coverage:** https://dailypost.ng/2025/10/28/former-staff-of-kaduna-electric-protest-alleged-nonpayment-of-88-months-entitlements/
- **National Industrial Court Judgment:** https://www.nicnadr.gov.ng/judgement/judgement.php?id=9073
- **Industrial Court Order:** https://www.nicnadr.gov.ng/news/1805/industrial-court-orders-kaduna-electricity-distribution-to-remit-n1-8m-pension-deduction-to-stanbic-ibtc-awards-n1m-damages-in-favour-of-exstaff
- **Case Summary:**
  - Kaduna Electric deducted pension contributions from employee salaries for approximately 52-54 months (2018-2023) without remitting to PFAs
  - Former employees protested demanding payment of 88 months' outstanding pension arrears
  - Industrial Court ordered KEDC to remit N1.8 million in pension deductions to Stanbic IBTC and awarded N1 million in damages to affected former employee
  - PenCom filed enforcement action (NICN case reference available)
  - Demonstrates systemic failures in employer compliance, inadequacy of existing enforcement mechanisms, and human impact on workers and retirees

### 4.2 Leadway Pensure PFA & NLPC PFA -- Investment Regulation Violation (January 2024)

- **Nairametrics Coverage:** https://nairametrics.com/2025/10/01/pencom-clarifies-overturning-n1-billion-leadway-sanction-denies-working-for-odukales/
- **TheCable Investigation:** https://www.thecable.ng/matters-arising-is-pencom-now-working-for-the-odukales/
- **Guardian Nigeria Coverage:** https://guardian.ng/news/leadway-pencom-clarifies-pension-fund-asset-investment/
- **Case Summary:**
  - Leadway Pensure PFA and NLPC PFA sanctioned for engaging in repurchase transactions in January 2024
  - Violation of Section 2.7 of the Regulation on Investment of Pension Fund Assets (transacting at prejudicial prices)
  - Initial sanctions: administrative penalties of N72 million (Leadway) and N24 million (NLPC)
  - Initial refund orders: N1.45 billion (Leadway) and N384 million (NLPC) into RSA funds
  - Following review, PenCom acknowledged computation error (losses-only calculation vs. net position)
  - Revised computation: Leadway had gains of N1.1 billion and losses of N1.7 billion, net loss of N598 million
  - Refund amounts reduced to recomputed figures; administrative sanctions maintained and paid
  - Demonstrates regulatory enforcement of investment rules, transparency in sanction review, and importance of accurate benefit computation

### 4.3 N29.84 Billion Pension Contribution Backlog

- **Guardian Nigeria Investigation:** https://guardian.ng/business-services/n29-84bn-pension-backlog-traps-workers-funds-over-remittance-failures/
- **Case Summary:**
  - Nearly N30 billion in pension contributions remains uncredited to workers' RSAs
  - Backlog caused by employer errors: irregular remittance schedules, incomplete documentation, mismatched employee data
  - Some employers deducted employees' 8% but failed to remit, remitting only employer's 10% or neither
  - Highlights systemic data quality and validation challenges that the PCRS system aims to resolve

### 4.5 PenCom-NLC Joint Enforcement Campaign Against Employer Defaults

- **PensionNigeria Coverage:** https://www.pensionnigeria.com/pension-news/pencom-and-nlc-launch-crackdown-on-employers-who-fail-to-remit-pension-contributions-what-you-need-to-know/

- **AllAfrica Coverage:** https://allafrica.com/stories/202512100217.html
- **Case Summary:**
  - PenCom and Nigeria Labour Congress (NLC) announced joint enforcement operations targeting defaulting employers
  - Between June 2012 and September 2025, PenCom's recovery agents recovered N32.27 billion total:
    - N15.87 billion in principal contributions
    - N16.40 billion in penalties
  - N2.06 billion recovered in Q3 2025 alone from 49 defaulting employers
  - Under Section 11(6) of PRA 2014, employers failing to remit contributions face penalty of not less than 2% of total outstanding contributions for each month of default
  - Cumulatively, defaulting employers paid approximately N14.348 billion in penalties from June 2012 onwards

### 4.6 PenCom Vendor Pension Compliance Requirement

- **BusinessDay Coverage:** https://businessday.ng/insurance/article/pencom-bans-vendors-without-evidence-of-pension-compliance-from-sector/
- **Case Summary:**
  - PenCom issued directive prohibiting Licensed Pension Fund Operators from transacting with service providers and vendors that do not remit pensions for their employees (evidenced by Pension Clearance Certificate)
  - Creates supply chain pressure for pension compliance
  - Novel enforcement approach using procurement leverage

## 6. GAPS REQUIRING SME INPUT

### 6.1 Internal PFA Compliance Procedures

Desk research cannot adequately capture the specific internal compliance workflows, checklists, and SOP documents used by PFAs for benefit processing, contribution reconciliation, and investment monitoring. **SME needed:** Compliance officer or operations manager from Trust Fund Pensions or similar PFA to provide internal process maps, sample compliance checklists, and common error patterns.

### 6.2 PenCom Examination and Supervision Process Details

While the regulatory framework is well-documented, the specific procedures PenCom uses during routine examinations of PFAs, on-site inspections, and supervision activities are not publicly available in detail. **SME needed:** Former PenCom examiner or PFA compliance officer who has experienced PenCom examinations to describe the process, common findings, and preparation best practices.

### 6.3 Actuarial Tables and Computation Parameters

The specific mortality tables and actuarial parameters used by PenCom for the Standard Retirement Benefit Computation (SRBC) template are not publicly available. The formula (RSA Balance / Expected Monthly Payments based on life expectancy) is known, but the actual mortality assumptions and how frequently they are updated require insider knowledge. **SME needed:** Actuary or senior benefits computation officer at a PFA.

### 6.4 Complaints Handling Metrics and Turnaround Data

While the Consumer Protection Framework establishes the structure, specific SLA data (average complaint resolution times, complaint categories, volume by PFA) is not publicly available. **SME needed:** PenCom Consumer Protection Department staff or PFA customer service manager to provide real operational metrics.

### 6.5 Investment Committee Operations and Decision-Making

How PFA investment committees practically evaluate and approve investment decisions within PenCom's guidelines, including the tools, models, and approval chains used, is not documented in public sources. **SME needed:** Fund manager or Chief Investment Officer from a PFA.

### 6.6 RSA Data Migration and Transfer Operational Challenges

Technical challenges encountered during RSA transfers (data mismatches, delayed processing, contributor disputes) are referenced but not documented in detail. **SME needed:** Operations or IT manager at a PFA who handles RSA transfer processing.

### 6.7 Ethical Dilemma Scenarios for PFA Staff

Real-world ethical dilemma scenarios specific to PFA operations (e.g., pressure from employers, agent conflicts of interest, preferential treatment requests, data privacy conflicts) would strengthen the ethics module but require practitioner input. **SME needed:** Compliance officer or HR manager from a PFA to develop realistic case scenarios.

### 6.8 PCRS Implementation Challenges

The PCRS system is newly operational (April 2025). Early implementation challenges, employer onboarding issues, PSSP integration problems, and data validation failures would provide valuable teaching material but require real operational data. **SME needed:** PFA operations manager or PenCom PCRS implementation team member.
