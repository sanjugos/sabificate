## 1. Primary Regulatory Sources

### 1.1 Industrial Training Fund Act (Cap I9, LFN 2004)

**Citation:** Industrial Training Fund Act, Cap I9, Laws of the Federation of Nigeria, 2004
**Full text:** [GitHub — Nigerian Laws Repository](https://github.com/mykeels/nigerian-laws/blob/master/2004/industrial-training-fund-act-cap-i9-lfn-2004.md)

**Key provisions:**
- **Establishment:** Creates the Industrial Training Fund to "promote and encourage the acquisition of skills in industry or commerce" and develop indigenous workforce capacity
- **Levy obligation (original):** Employers with 25+ employees must contribute 1% of annual payroll, due by April 1 of the following year
- **Refund provision (original):** Council may refund up to 60% of contributions if employer's training programme is deemed adequate
- **Employee scope:** Full-time, part-time, and temporary workers (3+ month tenure) all included
- **Employer duties:** Provide adequate skills training for indigenous staff; accept students for industrial attachment/internships (SIWES); submit evidence of training programmes
- **Penalties (original):** 5% monthly surcharge on unpaid amounts; N5,000-N10,000 fines for companies; N1,000 fines or 2-3 year imprisonment for officers
- **Governance:** 13-member Governing Council with representatives from industry ministries, employer associations, labour unions, and state representatives on two-year rotations
- **Funding sources:** Employer contributions, Federal Government allocations, student work experience scheme funds

**Source:** [Mondaq — Overview of Employment Related Laws in Nigeria](https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/employee-rights-labour-relations/763172/overview-of-employment-related-laws-in-nigeria)

### 1.2 Industrial Training Fund (Amendment) Act, 2011

**Citation:** Industrial Training Fund (Amendment) Act, 2011
**Full text PDF:** [ITF Official — ITF-ACT-2011.pdf](https://www.itf.gov.ng/ftp/ITF-ACT-2011.pdf)
**Commencement date:** 3 June 2011

**Key amendments:**

| Provision | Before (2004 Act) | After (2011 Amendment) |
|---|---|---|
| Employee threshold | 25+ employees | 5+ employees |
| Turnover threshold | Not specified | N50 million+ annual turnover |
| Temporary worker inclusion | 3+ months tenure | 30+ days tenure |
| Maximum reimbursement | Up to 60% | Up to 50% |
| Corporate penalty (first breach) | N5,000 | N500,000 |
| Corporate penalty (subsequent) | N10,000 | N1,000,000 |
| Officer penalty (first breach) | N1,000 | N50,000 or 2 years imprisonment |
| Officer penalty (subsequent) | N1,000 | 2 years imprisonment (no fine option) |
| Late payment penalty | 5% monthly | 5% compounded monthly interest |

**Additional changes:**
- Payroll definition expanded to "sum total of all basic pay, allowances and other entitlements payable within and outside Nigeria to any employee"
- ITF empowered to appoint agents and consultants for audit and compliance enforcement
- Government contract bidders must demonstrate ITF compliance
- Free trade zone entities seeking expatriate quotas or customs clearance must show statutory compliance
- Vocational skills acquired by apprentices, craftsmen, and technicians can now be certified

**Sources:**
- [Mondaq — What You Should Know About ITF Amendment Act 2011](https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/employee-rights-labour-relations/199880/what-you-should-know-and-do-about-the-industrial-training-fund-amendment-act-2011)
- [LawNigeria — ITF Amendment Act 2011](https://lawnigeria.com/2020/01/industrial-training-fund-amendment-act-2011/)
- [PolicyVault Africa](https://www.policyvault.africa/policy/industrial-training-fund-amendment-act2011-2/)

### 1.3 ITF Levy Collection Rules

- **Who pays:** Employers with (a) 5+ employees, OR (b) fewer than 5 employees but N50M+ annual turnover, OR (c) suppliers/contractors/consultants bidding for federal government or private contracts
- **Rate:** 1% of total annual payroll
- **Payroll definition:** All basic pay, allowances, and other entitlements paid to employees within or outside Nigeria; includes part-time and temporary workers (30+ days)
- **Due date:** Not later than 1 April of the following year
- **Payment method:** Generate Remita Retrieval Reference (RRR) via ITF Pay-Portal or Remita platform; pay at any authorised bank
- **Late payment:** 5% compounded monthly penalty; penalty waiver authority rests with Director-General
- **Exemptions:** Organisations with fewer than 5 employees AND turnover below N50M; entities registered in government-approved Free Trade Zones

**Source:** [AP Professional Services — The ITF Act](https://www.ap-proservices.com/blog-post/the-industrial-training-fund-itf-act/10/2024/)

### 1.4 Reimbursement Procedures & Qualifying Training Criteria

**Reimbursement rate:** Up to 50% of qualifying training expenditure incurred during the contribution year

**Qualifying training expenditure includes:**
- Fees paid to external trainers or training institutions for staff development
- Costs of training materials directly related to skill development
- Costs of organising internally delivered training programmes including facilitator costs
- Apprenticeship costs for registered apprenticeship schemes

**Pre-conditions for reimbursement:**
1. Annual Training Plan (ATP) must be submitted to ITF and approved before the beginning of the training year
2. Any amendments to the approved ATP must be communicated to ITF within one month of the change
3. Two (2) weeks' advance notice before local training using Form 4A (details: course title, trainee qualifications, nationalities, job titles, venue, dates)
4. Three (3) weeks' advance notice before international training
5. Minimum 15% of total workforce must be trained annually for full reimbursement eligibility
6. Training must cover all categories: Management, Supervisory, and Operative levels

**Required documentation:**
- Course Approval Form 5A (for training personnel)
- Form 6A (for all other staff)
- Course fee receipts (showing complete levy payment)
- Training invoices and vouchers
- Certificates of completion/attendance
- Receipt of levy payment to ITF

**Source:** [ITF Nigeria — Basic Materials/Conditions for Reimbursement](https://www.yumpu.com/en/document/view/35349707/the-basic-materials-conditions-for-itf-nigeria)

### 1.5 Form 7A — Employer Registration

**Purpose:** Employer Registration and Payment of Training Contribution Form
**Download:** [ITF Official — Form 7A PDF](https://www.itf.gov.ng/ftp/ITF_Form_7A.pdf)

**Required accompanying documents:**
1. Certificate of Incorporation (Corporate Affairs Commission)
2. Certified True Copy of Audited Financial Statements
3. Tax Clearance Certificate (or Tax Identification Number slip)

**Process after submission:**
1. ITF Revenue Inspectorate and Compliance Officers assess company liability based on payroll data in audited accounts
2. Company generates RRR via ITF Pay-Portal or Remita
3. Payment made at authorised bank
4. Payment proof and bank teller returned to ITF Area Office
5. Receipt and acknowledgement letter issued
6. ITF Headquarters processes employer details and issues ITF National Number
7. Area Office scripts and issues Certificate of Compliance

### 1.8 Federal High Court Interpretations

**Shell Petroleum v. ITF Governing Council** (FHC/L/CS/1865/2023, decided 27 November 2024)

- **Issue:** Whether ex-gratia severance payments and pension contributions form part of "payroll" for ITF levy calculation
- **Ruling:** The court held they do NOT constitute payroll for Section 6 purposes, applying the *ejusdem generis* rule
- **Financial impact:** ITF had assessed additional contributions exceeding N18 billion against the Shell Entities
- **Practical significance:** Clarifies that severance payments to former employees and pension contributions paid to third-party administrators are excluded from payroll calculations
- **Ongoing dispute:** Fees paid to Non-Executive Directors (NEDs) — whether these constitute "payroll cost" remains contested during ITF audits

**Source:** [Aluko & Oyebode — Federal High Court ITF Act Payroll Rulings](https://www.aluko-oyebode.com/insights/federal-high-court-itf-act-payroll-rulings/)

### 1.9 NSITF Relationship & Distinction

Both ITF and NSITF are employer-only statutory obligations, but serve different purposes:

| Feature | ITF | NSITF |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Skills training development | Employee compensation insurance |
| Rate | 1% of annual payroll | 1% of monthly payroll |
| Payment frequency | Annual (by 1 April) | Monthly (by 16th of following month) |
| Late penalty | 5% monthly | 10% penalty |
| Reimbursement | Up to 50% for qualifying training | Compensation for workplace injuries |
| Governing Act | ITF Act Cap I9 LFN 2004 (as amended 2011) | Employee Compensation Act 2010 |

**Source:** [MyWorkPay — Statutory Deductions in Nigeria](https://www.myworkpay.com/blogs/statutory-deductions-in-nigeria-a-comprehensive-overview-and-detailed-guide)

### 1.11 ITF Criticisms & Reform Arguments

A significant legal-academic critique (Afolabi Elebiju, 2020) argues:
- ITF is "an anachronism and a regulatory overhang" — companies already prioritize workforce development for survival
- 1% payroll levy constitutes "double taxation" since employers independently incur training costs
- Reimbursement scheme's extensive documentation and advance notice requirements are "cumbersome"
- Maximum 50% reimbursement (reduced from 60%) is "arguably oppressive, expropriatory"
- ITF "most probably lagging behind its regulated entities, especially in specialist sectors"
- Suggested reforms: merge ITF with NDE, fund through general budget, exempt employers with adequate training

**Source:** [Mondaq — Do We Still Need the ITF?](https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/constitutional-administrative-law/941052/vestiges-do-we-still-need-the-industrial-training-fund-itf)

## 3. Existing Courses & Syllabi

### 3.1 ITF's Own Training Programmes

1. **ITF/NECA Technical Skills Development Programme (TSDP)**
   - Joint programme between ITF and Nigeria Employers' Consultative Association (NECA)
   - Running since 31 March 2009
   - Intensive 9-month training in: Mechanical/Automation Maintenance & Engineering, Quality Assurance, Logistics & Distribution
   - Locations: Lagos, Aba (Abia), Ibadan (Oyo), Enugu, Ota and Ijebu-Ode (Ogun), Kaduna
   - Target: HND & ND holders
   - Includes monthly stipends and industry certification
   - [NECA — Technical Skills Development Project](https://neca.org.ng/technical-skills-development-project/)
   - [AfterSchoolAfrica — ITF-NECA TSDP 2025](https://www.afterschoolafrica.com/97340/itf-neca-technical-skills-development-program/)

2. **Skill Up Artisans Programme (SUPA)**
   - Three-phase structure: Foundation, SUPA I, SUPA II
   - 522,778 artisans registered in 2024 (319,000 males, 203,000 females)
   - 29,000 artisans graduated in Foundation phase (2024)
   - 100,000 artisans enrolled in second phase (2025)
   - Target: Train and certify 10 million artisans over full programme cycle
   - Covers 20+ technical and vocational trades
   - [Channels TV — ITF SUPA Phase 2](https://www.channelstv.com/2025/03/03/itf-to-enroll-additional-100000-artisans-in-second-phase-of-supa/)
   - [Tribune — ITF registers 522,778 artisans](https://tribuneonlineng.com/itf-registers-522778-artisans-for-supa-training/)

3. **Students Industrial Work Experience Scheme (SIWES)**
   - Designed in 1973 by ITF, approved by federal government in 1974
   - Provides tertiary students with practical industrial exposure
   - 2023 statistics: N1.1 billion paid as allowances — N733M to 48,869 university students, N233M to 23,297 polytechnic students, N72M to 7,238 college of education students
   - Over N24.6 billion paid in SIWES allowances over seven years (2016-2023)
   - [Gazette Nigeria — ITF SIWES Allowances 2023](https://gazettengr.com/itf-paid-n1-1-billion-as-siwes-allowances-in-2023-official/)

4. **ITF Direct Training Programmes**
   - Health and Safety Training (open to all employers)
   - Food Safety and Quality Assurance Workshop
   - Industrial Health, Safety and Environment Management Workshop
   - National Industrial Skills Acquisition Development Programme
   - Women Skills Empowerment Program
   - Skills Development Program for Youths in Construction Trade

### 3.2 Related Government Training Programmes

5. **National Directorate of Employment (NDE) — Core Programmes**
   - Vocational Skills Development (VSD) — equipping unemployed with functional, marketable skills using NDE training centres and informal sector master craftsmen
   - Small Scale Enterprise (SSE) — entrepreneurship training for graduates, artisans, women, vulnerable groups
   - Special Public Works (SPW) — public sector employment creation
   - Rural Employment Promotion (REP)
   - 2024: Trained 33,692 unemployed youths, women, and PWDs in various skills
   - Renewed Hope Employment Initiative (RHEI) Phase 2 ongoing
   - [NDE — Core Programme Departments](https://nde.gov.ng/4-core-program-departments/)

6. **3 Million Technical Talent (3MTT) Programme**
   - Federal government initiative to train 3 million digital professionals by 2027
   - Separate from ITF but addresses same skills gap

7. **Digital Bridge Institute**
   - Plans to train 5 million workers in AI by 2027

### 3.3 International Comparisons

8. **UK Apprenticeship Levy / Growth and Skills Levy**
   - Rate: 0.5% of total pay bill for employers with annual pay bill over GBP 3 million
   - Transitioning to "Growth and Skills Levy" from April 2026 with greater flexibility
   - Key difference from Nigeria: Much higher threshold (only large employers), lower rate
   - [British Council — UK Apprenticeship Levy](https://www.britishcouncil.org/education/skills-employability/skills-policy/global-spotlight/blog/uk-apprenticeship-levy)

9. **Singapore Skills Development Levy (SDL)**
   - Rate: 0.25% of monthly remuneration (minimum S$2, capped at S$11.25 per employee per month)
   - Applies to ALL employers regardless of size (unlike UK and Nigeria)
   - Funds: Course fee subsidies, absentee payroll funding, training provider QA
   - [GoBusiness Singapore — SDL](https://skillsfuture.gobusiness.gov.sg/skills-development-levy)

**Comparison matrix:**

| Feature | Nigeria ITF | UK Apprenticeship Levy | Singapore SDL |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rate | 1% of annual payroll | 0.5% of annual pay bill | 0.25% of monthly pay |
| Threshold | 5+ employees or N50M+ turnover | GBP 3M+ annual pay bill | All employers |
| Reimbursement | Up to 50% of qualifying training | 100% via digital account | Via SkillsFuture credits |
| Penalty | 5% monthly compound | 3% interest rate | Fines up to S$5,000 |
| Advance notice | 2-3 weeks | N/A | N/A |

## 4. Nigerian Case Studies

### 4.1 Successful Reimbursement Claims

1. **Rubber Estate Nigeria Limited (RENL) — 2020**
   - Sector: Agricultural (rubber production, Ovia South West LGA, Edo State)
   - Workforce: 2,382 employees
   - Reimbursement received: N8,801,503.01 (50% claim via ITF Benin Area Office)
   - Training approach: Dual-level (operational + leadership), domestic and international
   - Managing Director quote: "continuous training and upgrading of skills...is key to achieving meaningful competitive advantage"
   - [The Guardian — ITF Rewards Rubber Factory](https://guardian.ng/business-services/itf-rewards-rubber-factory-for-training-its-personnel/)

2. **580 Companies — 2023 Aggregate**
   - ITF paid N13,805,143,635 (N13.8 billion) to 580 companies as reimbursement for 2023
   - Reimbursement "in line with the ITF's enabling act to reimburse contributing employers up to 50% of the statutory annual training contribution"
   - [Gazette Nigeria](https://gazettengr.com/itf-paid-n1-1-billion-as-siwes-allowances-in-2023-official/)

3. **430 Companies — 2017 Aggregate**
   - ITF reimbursed N6.4 billion to 430 companies
   - Additional 90,000+ Nigerians trained on employability and entrepreneurship
   - 37,000 from 1,454 organisations trained through various ITF programmes
   - [Mondaq/Lexology — ITF Effectiveness Data](https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/constitutional-administrative-law/941052/vestiges-do-we-still-need-the-industrial-training-fund-itf)

### 4.2 Enforcement & Legal Cases

4. **Shell Petroleum v. ITF (2024)**
   - ITF assessed additional contributions exceeding N18 billion against Shell Entities
   - Shell successfully challenged the inclusion of ex-gratia severance payments and pension contributions in payroll calculation
   - Landmark ruling clarifying payroll definition for levy computation
   - [Aluko & Oyebode](https://www.aluko-oyebode.com/insights/federal-high-court-itf-act-payroll-rulings/)

5. **Non-Executive Director (NED) Fee Disputes**
   - Ongoing area of friction between employers and ITF during audits
   - ITF position: NED fees should be included in payroll for levy calculation
   - Employer position: NED fees are not a "payroll cost" — NEDs are not employees
   - No definitive court ruling yet; currently resolved on case-by-case basis during audit
   - [TNP — ITF Levy, To Pay or Not To Pay?](https://tnp.com.ng/insights/the-itf-levy-to-pay-or-not-to-pay-is-that-a-question-1)

### 4.3 SME Training Challenges

6. **Barriers to SME Training Investment**
   - Cost of training programmes prohibitive for small firms
   - Opportunity cost: SMEs cannot afford to release staff for training
   - Dearth of relevant training programmes tailored to SME contexts
   - Fear of employee resignation after investing in training
   - Negative attitudes of owner-managers about formalised training
   - Complex regulatory frameworks and excessive bureaucracy add administrative burden
   - Nigeria ranks 171st out of 190 countries in "Getting Electricity" — infrastructure constraints limit digital training options
   - [TechEconomy — Challenges Facing SMEs](https://techeconomy.ng/the-biggest-challenges-facing-smes-in-nigeria/)
   - [MacTay — L&D Strategic Imperative for SMEs 2025](https://mactay.com/ld-a-strategic-imperative-for-smes-in-2025/)

7. **ITF/NECA Technical Skills Development Programme — Employer Partnership Model**
   - Nigerian Breweries Plc is among companies participating in the 2026 TSDP cycle
   - Demonstrates how large employers can partner with ITF to meet training obligations while addressing skills gaps
   - [JobNow — Nigerian Breweries/ITF-NECA TSDP 2026](https://jobnow.ng/nigerian-breweries-plc-industrial-training-fund-itf-neca-technical-skills-development-programme-2026/)

8. **ITF Reimbursement Service Providers**
   - Commercial ecosystem of professional service firms (e.g., SimplexSystem, Dewattos Nigeria) that help employers navigate ITF reimbursement claims, indicating complexity level
   - Typical professional services cost: N50,000-N150,000
   - [SimplexSystem — ITF Reimbursement Service](https://simplexsystem.com/itf-reimbursement-service/)
   - [Dewattos — ITF Reimbursement](https://dewattosnigeria.com/itf-reimbursement/)

## 6. ITF Compliance Process

### 6.1 Step-by-Step: New Employer Registration

```
STEP 1: DETERMINE LIABILITY
├── Does your company have 5+ employees? → LIABLE
├── Does your company have <5 employees but N50M+ turnover? → LIABLE
├── Are you bidding for government/private contracts? → LIABLE
└── None of the above → EXEMPT (but verify with Area Office)

STEP 2: GATHER DOCUMENTS
├── CAC Certificate of Incorporation
├── Certified True Copy of Audited Financial Statements
└── Tax Clearance Certificate (or TIN slip)

STEP 3: COMPLETE FORM 7A
├── Download from itf.gov.ng or nearest Area Office
├── Complete with accurate company and payroll information
└── Attach required documents

STEP 4: SUBMIT TO NEAREST ITF AREA OFFICE
├── Revenue Inspectorate assesses liability
├── Assessment based on payroll data in audited accounts
└── Company receives assessment notification

STEP 5: MAKE PAYMENT
├── Generate Remita Retrieval Reference (RRR) via ITF Pay-Portal
├── OR generate RRR via Remita platform directly
├── Pay 1% of annual payroll at any authorised bank
└── Retain bank teller and Remita receipt

STEP 6: OBTAIN COMPLIANCE CERTIFICATE
├── Return payment proof and bank teller to ITF Area Office
├── Receive receipt and acknowledgement letter
├── ITF HQ processes employer details
├── ITF National Number issued
└── Area Office scripts and issues Certificate of Compliance
```

### 6.2 Step-by-Step: Annual Levy Payment (Existing Employers)

```
STEP 1: PREPARE ANNUAL RETURN
├── Complete Form 5A (Annual Return)
├── Calculate 1% of total annual payroll
│   ├── Include: all basic pay, allowances, entitlements
│   ├── Include: part-time and temporary workers (30+ days)
│   ├── Exclude: ex-gratia severance payments (per Shell ruling)
│   ├── Exclude: pension contributions to third-party administrators
│   └── Dispute: NED fees (arguable either way)
└── DEADLINE: 1 April of the following year

STEP 2: SUBMIT AND PAY
├── Submit Form 5A to registered Area Office
├── Area Office vets liability assessment
├── Generate RRR via ITF Pay-Portal or Remita
├── Pay at authorised bank
└── Submit payment evidence to Area Office

STEP 3: RECEIVE CERTIFICATE
├── Annual Compliance Certificate issued
└── Digitally signed PDF downloadable from employer portal
```

### 6.3 Step-by-Step: Training Reimbursement Claim

```
STEP 1: PLAN TRAINING (BEFORE YEAR BEGINS)
├── Develop Annual Training Plan (ATP)
│   ├── Must cover Management, Supervisory, and Operative levels
│   ├── Must plan for training of minimum 15% of total workforce
│   └── Submit ATP to ITF Area Office for approval
└── Receive ATP approval before training year commences

STEP 2: NOTIFY ITF BEFORE EACH TRAINING (MANDATORY)
├── Local training: Submit Form 4A at least 2 WEEKS before training
│   ├── Include: course title, trainee qualifications, nationalities
│   ├── Include: job titles, venue, implementation dates
│   └── Include: facilitator/trainer details
├── International training: Submit at least 3 WEEKS before training
└── ATP amendments: Communicate to ITF within 1 month of change

STEP 3: CONDUCT TRAINING & DOCUMENT EVERYTHING
├── Maintain attendance records
├── Collect course fee receipts
├── Retain training invoices and vouchers
├── Issue/collect certificates of completion
└── Photograph training sessions (recommended for evidence)

STEP 4: COMPILE REIMBURSEMENT APPLICATION
├── Complete Course Approval Form 5A (for training personnel)
├── Complete Form 6A (for all other staff)
├── Attach all supporting documentation:
│   ├── Course fee receipts (showing complete levy payment)
│   ├── Training invoices and vouchers
│   ├── Certificates of completion/attendance
│   └── Receipt of levy payment to ITF
└── Submit Reimbursement Application to ITF Area Office

STEP 5: RECEIVE REIMBURSEMENT
├── ITF Area Office reviews application
├── Governing Council reviews training outline
├── If satisfied: Up to 50% of qualifying training costs reimbursed
└── Reimbursement paid to employer
```

### 6.4 Step-by-Step: Audit Preparation

```
ITF AUDIT PREPARATION CHECKLIST
├── Registration documents
│   ├── ITF National Number confirmation
│   ├── Current Compliance Certificate
│   └── Historical Compliance Certificates (retain 6+ years)
├── Payroll records
│   ├── Complete annual payroll summaries
│   ├── Individual employee records
│   ├── Temporary and part-time worker records
│   └── Clear separation of payroll vs. non-payroll costs
├── Training documentation
│   ├── Approved Annual Training Plans (all years)
│   ├── Submitted Form 4A advance notices
│   ├── Course Approval Forms (5A and 6A)
│   ├── All training invoices, receipts, certificates
│   └── Reimbursement claim records and payments received
├── Payment records
│   ├── Remita payment confirmations (all years)
│   ├── Bank tellers
│   └── ITF acknowledgement letters
└── Corporate documents
    ├── Audited financial statements
    ├── Staff lists and payroll summaries
    └── CAC documents (if updated)
```

### 6.5 Common Compliance Pitfalls

1. **Missed advance notice:** Training conducted without the mandatory 2-week (local) or 3-week (international) advance notice invalidates reimbursement claims entirely
2. **Insufficient workforce coverage:** Training fewer than 15% of total workforce disqualifies employer from full reimbursement
3. **Missing ATP:** Conducting training without an approved Annual Training Plan on file
4. **Incomplete documentation:** Missing even one receipt or certificate can delay or deny reimbursement
5. **Late payment:** The 5% monthly compound penalty accrues rapidly — a N1M levy unpaid for 12 months would generate approximately N795,856 in penalties
6. **Payroll miscalculation:** Including/excluding disputed items (NED fees, severance) without clear documentation invites audit friction
7. **Not separating training categories:** Reimbursement requires training across Management, Supervisory, AND Operative levels
8. **Failure to update ATP:** Changes to training plan not communicated to ITF within one month
