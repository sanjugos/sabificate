## 1. PRIMARY REGULATORY SOURCES

### 1.1 Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) — Business Registration

- **Governing Body:** Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC), established under Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA) 2020
- **Official Portal:** https://pre.cac.gov.ng/
- **Source:** https://cacbusinessregistration.ng/requirements-for-cac-registration-in-nigeria/

**Types of Business Registration:**
- **Business Name (BN):** For sole proprietors and partnerships; processing time 3-5 working days
- **Limited Liability Company (LLC/Ltd):** For incorporated entities; processing time 3-7 working days
- **Incorporated Trustee (IT/NGO):** For non-profits; processing time 6-8 weeks

**2026 Key Requirements:**
- Fully digitalised registration process via CAC online portal (mandatory since 2026)
- Minimum share capital: N100,000 (private company); no minimum for business name
- Required documents: Completed registration form, valid government-issued ID, passport photographs, proof of business address, Memorandum & Articles of Association (for companies)
- **Persons with Significant Control (PSC):** Every company must disclose PSC to CAC open register (anti-money-laundering compliance)
- **RC Number = TIN:** Effective January 1, 2026, a company's CAC Registration Number (RC Number) automatically serves as its Tax Identification Number (TIN), eliminating need for separate FIRS TIN registration

**Post-Incorporation Obligations:**
- Appoint auditors within 6 months of incorporation
- Hold first Annual General Meeting (AGM) within 18 months of incorporation
- File annual returns every year after year of incorporation
- Maintain proper books of account

**Registration Fees (2026):**
- Business Name: N15,000-N25,000
- Private Limited Company: N20,000-N50,000 (depending on share capital)
- Online payment via Remita or bank transfer

**Relevance to Efiko Builders:** Module on business formalization; participants should register their businesses during the program. The simplified RC=TIN merger reduces bureaucratic steps for early-stage entrepreneurs.

### 1.3 Nigeria Tax Administration — FIRS TIN & Small Business Taxation

- **Agency:** Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) / Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS, effective 2026)
- **Governing Laws:** Nigeria Tax Administration Act 2025; Companies Income Tax Act (CITA); Personal Income Tax Act (PITA); Value Added Tax Act
- **TIN Portal:** https://taxpromax.firs.gov.ng/
- **Sources:** https://keepam.ng/company-tax-registration-nigeria | https://ao2law.com/taxation-of-small-companies-under-the-new-tax-regime-as-2026-approaches-overview-of-key-areas-of-interest/

**TIN Registration Process:**
- Application via TIN Registration Form (Form 001) online or at nearest FIRS office
- Processing time: 2-5 working days
- Free of charge
- **2026 Change:** RC Number now automatically serves as TIN upon incorporation (no separate registration needed)
- Mandatory deadline: All taxable persons must obtain TIN by January 2026

**Small Business Tax Exemptions (2026 Tax Regime):**
- **0% CIT Rate:** For small companies with turnover not exceeding N50 million AND fixed assets below N250 million
- Excludes professional service providers
- **VAT Registration Threshold:** Businesses with annual turnover below N25 million exempt from VAT registration
- **Research & Development Costs:** Wholly incurred in Nigeria are tax-deductible for labelled startups

**Tax Obligations for Small Businesses:**
- Companies Income Tax (CIT): 0% for small companies (turnover < N50M); 20% for medium (N50M-N200M); 30% for large (> N200M)
- Value Added Tax (VAT): 7.5% on eligible goods and services
- Pay-As-You-Earn (PAYE): Employer withholding on employee salaries
- Withholding Tax (WHT): Deducted at source on specified transactions

**Relevance to Efiko Builders:** Critical content for financial management module; most Efiko participants will qualify for 0% CIT given the NGN 100K revenue target. Understanding when and how tax obligations kick in at growth stages.

### 1.4 Nigeria Startup Act 2022

- **Full Title:** Nigeria Startup Act, 2022
- **Signed into Law:** October 19, 2022 by President Muhammadu Buhari
- **Implementing Agency:** NITDA (National Information Technology Development Agency)
- **Portal:** https://startup.gov.ng/
- **Sources:** https://www.forvismazars.com/ng/en/insights/publications/local-insights/start-up-act-2022 | https://chambers.com/articles/tax-breaks-more-what-the-nigerian-startup-act-offers

**Startup Label Requirements:**
- Registration as a domestic limited liability company
- Existence for not more than 10 years from date of incorporation
- At least 33% ownership by a Nigerian founder or co-founder
- Objective of innovation, development, production, improvement, and commercialization of a digital technology product or process
- Application via NITDA portal

**Key Incentives for Labelled Startups:**
- **Income Tax Exemption:** 3 years + additional 2 years if still within labelled startup period
- **R&D Tax Deduction:** Research and development costs wholly incurred in Nigeria fully deductible
- **Investment Tax Credit:** 30% tax credit on investments in labelled startups
- **Capital Gains Tax Exemption:** Gains from disposal of labelled startup assets held 24+ months exempt from CGT
- **Foreign Currency Repatriation:** Foreign investors can repatriate proceeds in freely convertible currency
- **Pioneer Status:** Labelled startups can apply for pioneer status under Industrial Development Act
- **Intellectual Property Protection:** Enhanced IP protections under the Act

**Relevance to Efiko Builders:** Participants building tech-enabled businesses should understand the Startup Label pathway. Even non-tech businesses benefit from understanding the incentive framework. Include in business model module.

### 1.5 NOTAP — Technology Acquisition & Transfer

**When NOTAP Registration is Required:**
- Any contract involving transfer of foreign technology to Nigerian entities
- Technology licensing agreements, technical service agreements, management agreements
- Franchise agreements with foreign partners
- Software licensing from foreign vendors (for commercial deployment)

**Registration Requirements:**

**Relevance to Efiko Builders:** Relevant for participants licensing foreign technology, using franchise models, or entering tech partnerships. Not all participants will need this — include as advanced/elective content.

## 2. TEXTBOOKS & REFERENCE WORKS

### 2.1 The Lean Startup — Eric Ries (2011)

- **Full Title:** The Lean Startup: How Today's Entrepreneurs Use Continuous Innovation to Create Radically Successful Businesses
- **Author:** Eric Ries
- **Publisher:** Crown Business, 2011
- **ISBN:** 978-0307887894

**Core Frameworks for Efiko Builders:**
- **Build-Measure-Learn Feedback Loop:** The fundamental process driving the lean startup — build a minimum viable product, measure results, learn whether to pivot or persevere
- **Minimum Viable Product (MVP):** The version of a new product that allows a team to collect the maximum amount of validated learning about customers with the least effort
- **Validated Learning:** Using data from real customers to test fundamental business hypotheses, not vanity metrics
- **Innovation Accounting:** Framework to evaluate progress when traditional metrics like revenue are premature — measuring actionable metrics vs. vanity metrics
- **Pivot or Persevere:** Structured process for deciding whether to change course (pivot) or continue on current path based on validated learning
- **The Five Whys:** Root cause analysis technique for identifying systemic problems

**Nigerian Context Adaptation:**
- MVP approach particularly suited to resource-constrained Nigerian entrepreneurs
- Build-Measure-Learn loop can work with limited capital, testing hypotheses with minimal investment
- Validated learning addresses the 40% of Nigerian SMEs that fail due to "no market demand"
- Innovation accounting relevant for Efiko's milestone tracking toward NGN 100K revenue

### 2.2 Business Model Generation — Alexander Osterwalder & Yves Pigneur (2010)

- **Full Title:** Business Model Generation: A Handbook for Visionaries, Game Changers, and Challengers
- **Authors:** Alexander Osterwalder, Yves Pigneur
- **Publisher:** John Wiley & Sons, 2010
- **ISBN:** 978-0470876411

**Core Frameworks for Efiko Builders:**
- **Business Model Canvas (BMC):** Visual framework with 9 building blocks:
  1. Customer Segments
  2. Value Propositions
  3. Channels
  4. Customer Relationships
  5. Revenue Streams
  6. Key Resources
  7. Key Activities
  8. Key Partnerships
  9. Cost Structure
- **Value Proposition Design:** Companion framework for mapping customer jobs, pains, and gains against product features
- **Business Model Patterns:** Common patterns (freemium, platform, long tail) adapted for African markets

**Nigerian Context Adaptation:**
- BMC widely used in FATE Foundation, TEF, and other Nigerian accelerator programmes
- Revenue Streams block particularly critical for Efiko's revenue verification requirement
- Key Partnerships block essential in Nigerian context where partnerships offset infrastructure gaps
- Channel design must account for WhatsApp/social commerce prevalence in Nigeria

### 2.3 Value Proposition Design — Alexander Osterwalder et al. (2014)

### 2.4 The Mom Test — Rob Fitzpatrick (2013)

- **Full Title:** The Mom Test: How to Talk to Customers & Learn if Your Business is a Good Idea When Everyone is Lying to You
- **Author:** Rob Fitzpatrick
- **Publisher:** Robfitz Ltd, 2013
- **ISBN:** 978-1492180746

**Key Principles for Customer Discovery:**
- Ask about their life, not your idea
- Ask about specifics in the past, not generics about the future
- Talk less, listen more
- Focus on the customer's problems, not your solution

**Nigerian Context Adaptation:**
- Customer interviews must be adapted for cultural context — Nigerian social norms around politeness can produce false positives
- WhatsApp-based customer discovery particularly effective in Nigeria
- Market traders and informal sector operators may provide richer feedback than formal surveys
- Language considerations: interviews in Pidgin, Yoruba, Igbo, or Hausa may yield more honest responses

### 4.1 Flutterwave — From Lagos Desk to $3B Payments Giant

- **Founded:** 2016, Lagos
- **Founders:** Iyinoluwa "E" Aboyeji, Olugbenga "GB" Agboola, Adeleke Adekoya
- **Valuation:** $3 billion
- **Status:** Banking license obtained (2026); dual HQ Lagos + San Francisco
- **Sources:** https://techeconomy.ng/how-flutterwave-turned-a-lagos-startup-into-africas-3bn-payments-giant/ | https://research.contrary.com/company/flutterwave

**Founder Story:**
- Aboyeji experienced repeated payment delays while running Andela with remote talent across borders

- Started from a shared desk in Lekki, Lagos
