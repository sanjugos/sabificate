## 1. Primary Regulatory Sources

### 1.1 CIBN Act No. 5 of 2007
- **Full Title:** An Act to repeal the Chartered Institute of Bankers of Nigeria Act 1990 and to re-enact the Institute to provide for the control of membership and practice of the banking profession in Nigeria
- **Signed:** 11 April 2007
- **Predecessor:** Decree No. 12 of 1990 (which first granted Chartered status)
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Section 2(7): "No person shall be entitled to be employed or appointed or headed any of the Technical Departments of a bank unless he is duly registered as a member" of CIBN
  - Establishes the Investigating Panel and Disciplinary Tribunal with authority over all banking institutions (except CBN and NDIC themselves) and their employees
  - Empowers CIBN to "determine the standards of knowledge and skill to be attained by persons seeking to become members of the Banking Profession"
  - Empowers the Institute to "ensure the furtherance, maintenance and observance of ethical standard and professionalism among practitioners"
  - CIBN is governed by a Governing Council including the President/Chairman of Council, Vice Presidents, National Treasurer, Representatives of the Body of Past Presidents, the Governor of CBN, and the MD/CEO of NDIC
- **Source:** [CIBN Codes, Acts and Rules](https://cibng.org/cibn-codes-acts-rules-downloads/) | [Wikipedia - CIBN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chartered_Institute_of_Bankers_of_Nigeria)

### 1.2 CIBN Compulsory Continuing Professional Development (CCPD) Framework
- **Practice License Holders:** Minimum 60 CCPD credit units per annum required for practice license renewal
- **E-Payment Certification Holders:** 20 recertification credits required within a 3-year validity cycle
- **CCPD Activities Include:** Seminars, webinars, conferences, open classroom sessions, e-learning modules, and e-payment programmes
- **CPD for Professional Bankers (aligned with CISI framework):** Minimum 6 hours per annum, with at least 1 hour relevant to Ethics and at least 3 hours via Institute-provided events or online modules
- **CISI Partnership:** CISI accredited as an Educational Service Training Provider (ESTP); CISI's CPD digital platform (180+ e-learning modules, 500+ videos, 1,300+ articles) counts toward CIBN members' annual CPD requirements
- **Source:** [CIBN Policy Rules & Regulations](https://cibng.org/policy-rules-regulations/) | [CIBN CCPD Page](https://cibng.org/ccpd)

### 1.3 Banks and Other Financial Institutions Act (BOFIA) 2020
- **Enacted:** 13 October 2020; signed into law 17 November 2020
- **Key Provisions Relevant to Banker Certification:**
  - Banks must provide information about staff training as part of banking licence applications
  - Tightens incentive structures for compliance and widens regulatory breadth
  - Penalises banks' office holders more severely for regulatory breaches
  - Emphasises banks' compliance with prudential ratios and improved disclosure
  - For Microfinance Banks: members of the top management team shall submit evidence of certification; executive management must possess certification on microfinance management within 3 years of programme commencement
- **Source:** [BOFIA 2020 Full Text - LawGlobal Hub](https://www.lawglobalhub.com/bofia-act-2020/) | [KPMG BOFIA Review](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/ng/pdf/tax/bofia-2020-review.pdf)

### 1.4 CBN Competency Framework for the Nigerian Banking Industry (2012/2014)
- **Released:** November 2012 (final version); full effect from November 2014
- **Purpose:** "Re-direct the banking industry towards the path of entrenching a sequenced competency development programme that will build capacity for the stability of the financial system"
- **Background:** "The recent global financial crisis exposed the inadequacy of skills and dearth of executive capacity in the banking industry"
- **Key Features:**
  - CIBN appointed as Sole Accreditation Agency in 2016 with 9 Terms of Reference
  - Framework for accrediting Educational Training Service Providers (ETSPs) and their programmes
  - Power to accredit and disaccredit TSPs based on Section 7.5 of the CF
  - As of 2020: CIBN accredited 25+ ETSPs, the CBN Learning Centre, and 2+ Bank Academies
- **Source:** [CBN Competency Framework PDF](https://www.cbn.gov.ng/out/2012/ccd/competency%20framework.pdf) | [BusinessDay - CIBN Accreditation](https://businessday.ng/companies/article/cibn-presents-accreditation-certificates-to-cbn-heritage-bank-nse-23-others/)

### 1.5 Code of Conduct in the Nigerian Banking Industry
- **Scope:** Applies to all Directors, Managers, Officers, Supervisors, and Employees including full-time, part-time, temporary, casual, and in-sourced staff of all banks/Discount Houses in Nigeria
- **Objective:** "Ensure strict adherence to best banking practices and a strong commitment to ethical and professional standards in behavior within the Nigerian banking industry"
- **CBN Mandate:** All staff must complete an Attestation Form demonstrating understanding of the Code's provisions
- **Code Functions:**
  - Develops standards and codes for ethical and professional banking practice
  - Considers complaints from bank customers and the general public
  - Considers complaints between banks and regulatory authorities
  - Considers complaints from bank staff against employers or vice versa
- **Source:** [CIBN Code of Conduct PDF](https://www.cibng.org/files/resourceDownloads/codeOfConduct.pdf) | [CIBN Ethics & Professionalism](https://cibng.org/ethics-professionalism/)

### 1.6 CBN Corporate Governance Guidelines (2023)
- Updated guidelines for corporate governance in financial institutions
- Include provisions for board competency, compliance culture, and professional standards
- **Source:** [CBN Corporate Governance Circular](https://www.cbn.gov.ng/Out/2023/FPRD/Circular%20and%20Guidelines%20for%20Corporate%20Governance.pdf)

### 1.7 Nigeria AML/CFT Regulatory Framework
- **Key Legislation:** Money Laundering (Prohibition) Act; Terrorism (Prevention) Act
- **Regulatory Bodies:** CBN, Nigerian Financial Intelligence Unit (NFIU), Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC)
- **Enforcement:** CBN imposed penalties totalling N15 billion on 29 banks for AML/CTF violations
- **Training Gap:** Whistleblowing policies (40%) and training (45%) perceived as weak; KYC/AML compliance (65%) and internal control (60%) seen as relatively more effective
- **Source:** [Sanctions.io AML Guide Nigeria](https://www.sanctions.io/blog/guide-anti-money-laundering-aml-compliance-in-nigeria)

### 1.8 CBN Banking Sector Recapitalisation Programme (2024-2026)
- **Announced:** March 2024
- **Deadline:** 31 March 2026 (concluded)
- **New Capital Requirements:**
  - International commercial banks: N50bn to N500bn
  - National commercial banks: N25bn to N200bn
  - Regional commercial banks: N10bn to N50bn
  - Non-interest banks: N20bn (national), N10bn (regional)
- **Results:** 33 banks met requirements; N4.65 trillion ($3.38bn) raised; 72.55% domestic, 27.45% international sources
- **Relevance to CPD:** Recapitalisation creates demand for upgraded staff competencies in risk management, capital markets, regulatory compliance, and corporate governance
- **Source:** [African Business](https://african.business/2026/04/finance-services/nigeria-concludes-4-65-trillion-bank-recapitalisation-programme)

- Five major banks collectively spent N1.23 trillion on personnel costs (2025), up 18% YoY

### 4.4 CIBN Disciplinary Framework in Action
- Two-tier system: Investigating Panel conducts preliminary investigations; Disciplinary Tribunal determines referred cases
- Jurisdiction covers all banking institutions and their employees
- Filing requirements: 2 hard copies + electronic submission, completed complaint form, non-refundable N100,000 filing fee
- Case restrictions: No infractions older than 6 years; minimum case value N5 million
- Common issues: Excessive charges, unauthorised debits, credit suppression to avoid interest payments
- **Source:** [Guardian Nigeria - CIBN Tribunal](https://guardian.ng/opinion/cibn-tribunal-for-erring-bankers/)

### 4.5 March 2026 Induction Ceremony — AI in Banking

- FinTech Association of Nigeria President: Institutions failing to adopt AI "risk losing relevance"
- Key challenges identified: High implementation costs, skill gaps in Nigerian banks
- **Source:** [Guardian Nigeria - CIBN Induction](https://guardian.ng/business-services/cibn-inducts-2020-members-urges-banks-to-adopt-ai/)

### 6.2 Seven Routes to ACIB
1. **Regular Route** — For HND/degree holders in non-banking disciplines; starts at Diploma or Intermediate level
2. **Linkage Route** — For students at CIBN-partnered tertiary institutions; enters at Chartered Banker level
3. **Practitioners' Route** — For professionals with 10+ years industry experience at manager grade; 4-8 subjects + case study (limited 5-year window)
4. **Chartered Banker/MBA Route** — For MBA holders with 3+ years managerial experience; enters at Chartered Banker level
5. **Certification Route** — Completion of specialist certification programmes grants exemptions in ACIB exams
6. **Bank Academy Route** — Graduates of accredited Bank Academies
7. **MSc/ACIB Route** — Combined degree programme

### 6.3 CCPD Credit Structure
- **Practice licence holders:** 60 credit units per annum (mandatory for renewal)
- **E-Payment certified professionals:** 20 credits per 3-year cycle
- **General professional bankers:** 6 hours per annum minimum
- **Eligible activities:** CIBN seminars, webinars, conferences, classroom training, e-learning modules, approved external programmes (including CISI)
- **Ethics minimum:** At least 1 hour of ethics content per annum
- **Institute-provided minimum:** At least 3 hours from CIBN events/modules per annum

### 6.4 Practice Licence Requirements
- Minimum 5 years post-ACIB professional experience
- Must pass qualifying professional examinations
- "Fit and proper" evaluation
- 14-day public disclosure period for applicant vetting
- Employer references and regulatory authority consultations
- Cannot receive or accept deposits from the public
- Restricted to advisory and management capacities

### 6.5 Allowed Professional Roles (Practice Licence)
1. Financial Administrators and Managers
2. Banking/Finance Consultants
3. Credit Analysts and Managers
4. Financial Advisers and Investigators
5. Arbitrators and Mediators
6. Industry Analysts

## 8. Proposed Module Structure

### Course: CIBN Mandatory CPD for Bankers
**Format:** 4 modules, 15 lessons
**Target Audience:** Nigerian banking professionals (CIBN members and aspiring members) at all career stages
**CPD Credit Alignment:** Designed to fulfil CIBN CCPD requirements including the Ethics minimum

---

#### Module 1: Regulatory Framework & Professional Standards (4 lessons)

**Lesson 1.1: The CIBN Legal Framework**
- CIBN Act No. 5 of 2007 — establishment, powers, and scope
- BOFIA 2020 — banker qualification and certification requirements
- Section 2(7) — mandatory CIBN registration for technical department heads
- Relationship between CIBN, CBN, and NDIC

- Origins in the 2008 global financial crisis skills gap
- Structure: competency areas, accreditation standards, ETSPs
- CIBN as Sole Accreditation Agency (2016 appointment)
- How the framework drives CPD requirements

**Lesson 1.3: CIBN Membership & Certification Pathway**
- 7 individual membership categories (Student through Fellow)
- ACIB examination structure: 4 levels, 13+ subjects
- 7 routes to ACIB qualification
- 16 specialist certification programmes
- Practice licence requirements and permitted roles

**Lesson 1.4: CPD Obligations & Compliance**
- CCPD credit structure (60 units for practice licence; 6 hours general)
- Eligible CPD activities and approved providers
- Ethics CPD minimum (1 hour per annum)
- Consequences of non-compliance: Investigating Panel and Disciplinary Tribunal
- Record-keeping and reporting requirements

**Lesson 2.2: Credit Analysis & Lending**
- Credit risk assessment frameworks

**Lesson 2.4: Treasury Operations & Trade Finance**

- Cash flow management and liquidity optimisation

- CIBN Professional Certification in Treasury and Financial Risk Management (PCTFRM)

#### Module 3: Digital Banking, Fintech & Financial Inclusion (4 lessons)

**Lesson 3.1: Digital Banking Transformation**
- Nigeria's digital banking landscape (83% mobile banking adoption)
- Core banking system modernisation
- Digital channels: mobile, internet, USSD, POS
- Cybersecurity and fraud prevention in digital banking
- CIBN E-Payment Certification Programme (CePA/CePP/CePS)

**Lesson 3.2: Fintech & Innovation**
- Nigeria's fintech ecosystem (19% GDP contribution, 45% of African deals)
- Payment Service Banks and mobile money operators

- Nigeria's AML framework: Money Laundering Act, Terrorism Prevention Act
- CBN AML/CFT guidelines and enforcement (N15bn fines to 29 banks)
- Role of NFIU, EFCC, and CBN in compliance oversight
- Suspicious transaction reporting and record-keeping
- Whistleblowing frameworks and their effectiveness gaps

- CIBN Code of Conduct in the Nigerian Banking Industry

- *[Satisfies CIBN Ethics CPD minimum requirement]*

- Post-recapitalisation governance challenges

**Lesson 4.3: Career Development & Lifelong Learning**
