## 1. Primary Regulatory Sources

### 1.1 Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) 2021
- **Citation**: Petroleum Industry Act, 2021 (No. 6)
- **Full text**: https://pia.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/PIA-2021_compressed-1.pdf
- **HSE Relevance**: The PIA is the overarching legislation that restructured Nigeria's petroleum regulatory architecture. It created two distinct regulators: the Nigerian Upstream Petroleum Regulatory Commission (NUPRC) for upstream and the Nigerian Midstream and Downstream Petroleum Regulatory Authority (NMDPRA) for midstream/downstream. Key HSE provisions include:
  - Establishing NUPRC's mandate to set and enforce health, safety, and environmental standards for upstream operations
  - Section 104(1): Penalises gas flaring except in emergencies or where conducted in line with safety measures
  - Mandates environmental impact assessments before project commencement
  - Requires operators to maintain valid environmental permits for emissions, effluents, flaring, and gas-utilisation activities
  - Empowers NUPRC to develop, implement, and review HSE guidelines, policies, procedures, and management systems
- **Source**: https://sabilaw.org/petroleum-industry-act-2021-pia-and-its-impacts-on-environmental-sustainability-in-nigeria/

### 1.2 Upstream Petroleum Safety Regulations 2022 / 2024
- **Citation**: Upstream Petroleum Safety Regulations, made under the PIA
- **Full text (2022)**: https://www.nuprc.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Upstream-Petroleum-Safety-Regulations.pdf
- **Full text (2024 revision)**: https://www.nuprc.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/SAFETY-REGULATIONS.pdf
- **Key Provisions**:
  - Revokes and replaces the Mineral Oils (Safety) Regulations 1963
  - Regulation 38: Prohibits unauthorised entry into tanks and confined/semi-confined spaces
  - Requires development of Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for each facility and operation
  - Mandates safe conduct of offshore operations under qualified Manager supervision
  - Requires annual offshore safety permits for all personnel operating in onshore and offshore locations
  - Personnel must complete basic safety and survival training before obtaining permits
  - Managers must appoint supervisors, provide training, develop communication channels, display medical contact information
  - Operations must follow good industry practices (API, ASME, Institute of Petroleum codes)

### 1.3 NUPRC Safety Case Guideline 2020
- **Citation**: Safety Case Guidelines for Oil and Gas Facilities in Nigeria, 2020
- **Full text**: https://www.nuprc.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/Safety-Case-Guidelines-for-Oil-and-Gas-Facilities-in-Nigeria.pdf
- **Key Provisions**:
  - Obligates operators to reduce accident/hazard occurrence As Low As Reasonably Practicable (ALARP)
  - Defines a Safety Case as a structured argument showing facility is acceptably safe
  - Requires Formal Safety Assessment (FSA) of all facilities and installations
  - FSA must include: facility layout/process description, technical safety studies, comprehensive risk/hazard identification, risk control techniques, critical aspects, safety management systems
  - FSA Team must include: NUPRC representatives, operations personnel, maintenance/inspection staff, instrumentation personnel, HSE personnel, project team, and subject matter experts
  - Identifies Major Accident Hazards (MAHs) requiring specific controls
- **Review article**: https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/oil-gas-electricity/1367754/a-review-of-the-safety-case-guidelines-for-oil-and-gas-facilities-in-nigeria-2020

### 1.4 NUPRC Safety & Environment Guidelines
- **Portal**: https://www.nuprc.gov.ng/safety-environment/
- **Key Functions**:
  - Safety Standards Development & Coordination Unit: develops and reviews HSE guidelines, policies, procedures, and management systems
  - Reviews and harmonises health and safety standards across facility design, construction, operation, maintenance, and decommissioning
  - Ensures compliance with both local and international best practices, codes, and standards
  - Requires ISO 45001 certification for management systems
  - Mandates rapid incident reporting to NOSDRA with forensic oil-fingerprinting techniques

### 1.5 Upstream Petroleum Environmental Regulations 2022
- **Full text**: https://www.nuprc.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Upstream-Petroleum-Environmental-Regulations.pdf
- **Key Provisions**: Environmental permitting, emissions controls, waste management, and environmental monitoring requirements for upstream petroleum operations

### 1.6 Factories Act, Cap F1, LFN 2004
- **Scope**: All factories in Nigeria, including oil and gas processing facilities
- **Key Provisions**:
  - Section 23: Prohibits employment of inexperienced workers on dangerous machinery or processes without adequate training and supervision
  - Mandates: ventilation, lighting, cleanliness, machinery safety, welfare facilities, occupational health services
  - Requires employers to acquaint employees about hazards before commencement of work and train on preventive measures
  - Enforcement through: OSH inspections, workplace safety/health audits, investigation of occupational accidents/diseases/dangerous occurrences
  - Sanctions: improvement notices, warning notices, cautionary letters, prohibition notices, prosecution
- **Source**: https://disciplines.ng/industrial-occupational-safety-regulations/

### 1.7 Employees' Compensation Act (ECA) 2010
- **Citation**: Employees' Compensation Act, 2010 (No. 13)
- **Key Provisions**:
  - Replaced the Workmen's Compensation Act
  - Introduced no-fault liability principle: employees entitled to compensation without proving employer negligence
  - Covers all employers and employees in public and private sectors (excluding armed forces in active service)
  - Compensable injuries: those arising out of or in the course of employment
  - Benefits: medical expenses, temporary/permanent disability benefits, survivor benefits for work-related fatality
  - Administered by the Nigeria Social Insurance Trust Fund (NSITF)
  - Employer contribution: 1% of monthly payroll to the Employees' Compensation Fund
- **Source**: https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/employee-rights-labour-relations/1673746/understanding-the-employees-compensation-act-and-tortious-claims-for-workplace-injuries-in-nigeria

### 1.8 NSITF Act / Employees Compensation Scheme
- **Administering Body**: Nigeria Social Insurance Trust Fund (NSITF)
- **Portal**: https://nsitf.gov.ng/
- **Key Data**:
  - 7.6 million employees enrolled in the Employees Compensation Scheme (ECS) as of May 2026
  - 22,350 compensation claims processed in 2024 (21% increase in claims payout year-on-year)
  - 23,855 compliant employers as at March 2024
  - Investments in automated workflows, real-time claims tracking, standardised processing timelines

### 1.9 Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) Act, No. 86 of 1992 (amended 2004)
- **Scope**: Mandates comprehensive EIA for projects likely to have significant environmental impacts
- **Oil and Gas Activities Requiring EIA**:
  - Oil and gas field development
  - Construction of offshore, onshore, and overland pipelines
  - Construction of oil and gas processing facilities
  - Construction of oil refineries
  - Waste treatment and disposal
- **Process**: EIA approval timelines typically range from 30 to 90 working days
- **Oversight**: National Environmental Standards and Regulations Enforcement Agency (NESREA) oversees assessments
- **Challenges**: Gaps in enforcement, inadequate public participation, insufficient procedural clarity
- **Source**: https://solomaygeoservices.com/environmental-impact-assessment-eia-in-nigeria-approval-process-and-requirements/

### 1.10 NOSDRA Act (Act No. 15 of 2006)
- **Establishing Legislation**: Act No. 15 of October 18, 2006
- **Portal**: https://nosdra.gov.ng
- **Key Provisions**:
  - Establishes the National Oil Spill Detection and Response Agency
  - Implements the National Oil Spill Contingency Plan (NOSCP)
  - Complies with International Convention on Oil Pollution Preparedness, Response and Cooperation (OPRC 1990)
  - Oil companies must stop spills within 24 hours of notification
  - NOSDRA conducts inspections, audits, and assessments of oil facilities
  - No legally binding regulatory penalties or fines for oil spills (significant enforcement gap)
  - Companies must fund clean-up and may pay community compensation for spills caused by their operations
- **Spill Monitoring**: https://nosdra.oilspillmonitor.ng/about.html

### 1.11 EGASPIN (Environmental Guidelines and Standards for the Petroleum Industry in Nigeria)
- **Versions**: 1991 (original), 2002 (revised), 2016 (revised), 2018 (current)
- **Issuing Authority**: Originally DPR, now NUPRC (upstream) / NMDPRA (midstream/downstream)
- **Scope**: Outlines environmental and safety standards for oil operators to prevent, minimise, and control pollution
- **Covers**: Oil spill practices (prevention, contingency planning, emergency response, containment, control, remediation, restoration), effluent limitations, standards and procedures for evaluation and monitoring of E&P waste discharge
- **Source**: https://www.iea.org/policies/8676-environmental-guidelines-and-standards-for-the-petroleum-industry-in-nigeria-egaspin

### 1.12 ISPON Act 2014
- **Citation**: Institute of Safety Professionals of Nigeria Act 2014
- **Portal**: https://ispon.gov.ng/ and https://isponact.com.ng/
- **Key Provisions**:
  - Establishes ISPON as the regulatory body for safety practice in Nigeria
  - Sections 3(d) and 4(1): Any individual who desires to practice safety in Nigeria must register with ISPON
  - Mandates ISPON to promote, regulate, standardise, train, certify, and maintain ethics in safety practice
  - ISPON is the technical arm of the National Industrial Safety Council of Nigeria (NISCN)
  - Conducts examinations and awards certificates/diplomas in safety management

### 1.13 Mineral Oils (Safety) Regulations (L.N. 45 of 1963, revised 1997, 2004)
- **Status**: Revoked and replaced by Upstream Petroleum Safety Regulations 2022
- **Historical Significance**: Was the primary safety regulation for oil and gas operations for nearly 60 years
- **Key Provisions** (historical):
  - Equipment handling requirements for derricks, hooks, boilers, oil treaters, fuel storage tanks
  - Annual renewable offshore safety permits for all personnel
  - Basic safety and survival training requirements
  - Supervisor appointment and training requirements
  - Referenced API codes, ASME standards, and Institute of Petroleum Safety codes for gap-filling
- **Source**: https://natlex.ilo.org/dyn/natlex2/r/natlex/fe/details?p3_isn=52884

### 1.14 National Policy on Occupational Safety and Health
- **Full text**: https://nelex.gov.ng/documents/NATIONAL_POLICY_ON_OCCUPATIONAL_SAFETY_AND_HEALTH.pdf
- **Constitutional Basis**: Section 17.3(c) of the 1999 Constitution directs state policy toward ensuring health, safety, and welfare of persons in employment
- **ILO Conventions Ratified**:
  - Convention No. 155 on Occupational Safety, Health and Working Environment (ratified 1994)
  - Convention No. 187 on Promotional Framework for Occupational Safety and Health (ratified 2022)

### 1.15 Flare Gas (Prevention of Waste and Pollution) Regulations 2018
- **Key Provisions**:
  - Establishes procedures for flare permits, penalties, reporting obligations, and gas-commercialisation programmes
  - PIA 2021 raised statutory penalty to US $3.50 per Mscf of gas flared
  - Penalties increased from N70.42 billion (2022) to N140.54 billion (2023) to N391.26 billion (2024)
  - Oil companies paid approximately $646 million in gas-flaring penalties in 2025 (highest in five years)
- **Source**: https://flaringventingregulations.worldbank.org/nigeria

## 3. Existing Courses & Syllabi

### 3.1 NEBOSH International General Certificate (IGC)
- **Provider**: NEBOSH (National Examination Board in Occupational Safety and Health)
- **Duration**: 65 taught hours + ~40 hours private study (typically 10 working days in-class)
- **Structure**:
  - **IGC1 -- Management of International Health and Safety**: Foundations of H&S; health and safety management systems; strategic and operational management
  - **IGC2 -- Controlling Workplace Hazards**: Chemical/biological hazards and risk control; physical/physiological hazards and risk control
  - **Practical Assessment**: Real-time scenario report from any industry (oil and gas, construction, etc.)
- **Assessment**: Written examination + practical assessment
- **2026 Syllabus Update**: https://www.hsestudyguide.com/nebosh-igc-new-syllabus-2026/

### 3.2 NEBOSH International Technical Certificate in Oil and Gas Operational Safety
- **Status**: Being withdrawn by NEBOSH; final assessment date is 30 September 2026
- **Replacement**: NEBOSH HSE Certificate in Process Safety Management
- **Coverage**:
  - Safety and environmental management in the oil and gas industry
  - Hydrocarbon process safety
  - Fire protection and emergency response
- **Assessment**: 2-hour written exam
- **Study Hours**: Minimum 34 taught hours + ~20 hours private study
- **Link**: https://www.nebosh.org.uk/qualifications/international-oil-and-gas-certificate/

### 3.3 NEBOSH HSE Certificate in Process Safety Management
- **Status**: Active (replacement for oil and gas certificate above)
- **Coverage**: Process safety leadership, management of process risk, process safety hazard control, fire and explosion protection
- **Link**: https://www.nebosh.org.uk/qualifications/nebosh-hse-certificate-in-process-safety-management/

### 3.4 IOSH Managing Safely (Version 5.0)
- **Provider**: Institution of Occupational Safety and Health (IOSH)
- **Duration**: Typically 4 days
- **Structure -- 7 Modules**:
  1. Introducing Managing Safely (reasons for good H&S)
  2. Manager's responsibility and accountability
  3. Assessing Risks (risk assessment principles)
  4. Controlling Risks
  5. Understanding Responsibilities
  6. Understanding Hazards
  7. Investigating Incidents
- **Assessment**: Short-answer test + practical assessment
- **Certification**: IOSH-accredited certificate
- **Nigeria Providers**: The Knowledge Academy, Wise Global Training, Astutis International, McTimothy Associates, Qesh Standards
- **Link**: https://www.theknowledgeacademy.com/ng/courses/iosh-training/iosh-managing-safely/

### 3.5 ISPON HSE Competence Certification (Levels 1, 2 & 3)
- **Provider**: Institute of Safety Professionals of Nigeria (ISPON)
- **Requirement**: Applicants must complete ISPON HSE Competence Level 2 & 3 courses and have at least 1 year of safety work experience
- **Scope**: Certification, training, advocacy, and professional support for safety practitioners across industries
- **Examinations**: ISPON conducts examinations and awards certificates and diplomas in safety management
- **Link**: https://ispon.gov.ng/training-certification-2330/

### 3.6 WSO (World Safety Organization) HSE Levels 1, 2 & 3
- **Providers in Nigeria**: HSE Training Institute, Ciel Consulting, Absolute HSE Co. Ltd
- **Accreditation**: World Safety Organisation (Special Consultative Status to UN ECOSOC), ISO/IEC 17024:2012

## 4. Nigerian Case Studies

### 4.1 Jesse Pipeline Explosion (1998)
- **Date**: October 18, 1998
- **Location**: Jesse, Delta State
- **Casualties**: 1,082 dead, hundreds injured
- **Cause**: Pipeline vandalism; villagers scooping fuel from a ruptured pipeline when it ignited
- **Significance**: Deadliest pipeline disaster in Nigerian history; one of the worst globally
- **HSE Lessons**: Community awareness, pipeline integrity management, third-party interference prevention, emergency response inadequacy
- **Source**: https://www.penglobalinc.com/tragedy-of-a-nation-remembering-jesse-pipeline-explosion-the-most-deadly-in-nigeria

### 4.2 Abule Egba Pipeline Explosion (2006)
- **Date**: December 26, 2006
- **Location**: Abule Egba, Lagos
- **Casualties**: At least 260 dead (Red Cross figure)
- **Cause**: Pipeline vandalism in a densely populated neighbourhood
- **HSE Lessons**: Pipeline routing through populated areas, integrity management, community risk, emergency response
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2006_Abule_Egba_pipeline_explosion

### 4.3 Abule-Ado Explosion (2020)
- **Date**: March 15, 2020
- **Location**: Abule-Ado, Lagos
- **Casualties**: 23 dead, 25 injured, 50 houses destroyed, 276,000 displaced
- **Cause**: Truck collision with gas cylinders near a vandalised petroleum gas pipeline
- **HSE Lessons**: Process safety at gas processing plants, proximity of industrial operations to residential areas, transportation safety, emergency response
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abule-Ado_explosion

### 4.4 Imo-Rivers Illegal Refinery Explosion (2022)
- **Date**: April 22, 2022
- **Location**: Rivers State (Imo/Rivers border)
- **Casualties**: 110+ dead
- **Cause**: Explosion at an illegal oil refining depot
- **HSE Lessons**: Process safety fundamentals in refining, ventilation, ignition source control, inherent dangers of unregulated operations
- **Source**: https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2022/4/23/explosion-at-nigerian-illegal-oil-refinery-kills-more-than-100

### 4.5 K.S. Endeavor Offshore Rig Explosion -- Chevron (2012)
- **Date**: January 16, 2012
- **Location**: Funiwa Field, approximately 6 miles offshore Nigeria (Niger Delta)
- **Casualties**: 2 dead (installation manager Bruno Marce, French national; driller Albert Devadas, Indian national)
- **Cause**: Gas buildup through drilling pipe; blowout preventer failure; fire burned 46 days
- **Critical Finding**: Crew anticipated the explosion 3 days prior; evacuation request denied by Chevron; ordered to continue drilling
- **Litigation**: $5 billion Nigerian lawsuit against Chevron
- **HSE Lessons**: Well control, blowout preventer maintenance, management of change, crew safety alerts, stop-work authority, emergency evacuation procedures
- **Sources**: https://www.arnolditkin.com/oil-rig-explosions/major-oil-rig-disasters/k-s-endeavor/ and https://www.premiumtimesng.com/news/93455-chevron-ignored-sos-from-workers-in-rig-fire-that-killed-two.html

### 4.6 Dangote Refinery Worker Safety Issues (2015-2024)
- **Context**: $19+ billion, 650,000 bpd capacity, world's largest single-train refinery (Lekki Free Zone, Lagos)
- **Documented Worker Deaths**:
  - Nicholas George (Bayelsa State indigene): died at work approx. 4 years prior; employer (Onshore Construction Company) provided no compensation
  - Three workers fell from high-rise platform; fourth worker bled to death in hospital within one hour
  - Iron beam fell on a worker, causing death
  - Worker died from machine malfunction (blood loss)
  - HSE manager died of COVID-19 during construction lockdown; another worker committed suicide after testing positive
- **Safety Violations**:
  - Workers used flip-flops and torn gloves instead of safety boots, gloves, and helmets
  - Anti-union practices: refusal to allow construction workers to form unions
  - COVID-19 lockdown: hundreds of workers locked in construction site by contractors
  - Workers sacked for planning to demand international safety standards
- **June 2024 Fire Incident**: Fire at effluent treatment plant; no casualties reported; no government oversight agency (NMDPRA) conducted public investigation
- **HSE Lessons**: PPE provision and enforcement, contractor management, worker welfare, right to organise, incident investigation transparency, regulatory oversight gaps
- **Sources**: https://humanglemedia.com/hazards-maltreatment-and-human-rights-violations-at-dangote-refinery/ and https://inspectioneering.com/news/2024-06-26/11147/nigerias-dangote-refinery-says-it-is-operating-normally-after-fire

### 4.7 Shell SPDC Oil Spill Litigation -- Ogale and Bille Communities
- **Background**: Chronic oil spills over decades left communities of 50,000 people without clean water, unable to farm/fish
- **Case Timeline**: Launched 2008; Hague Court of Appeal 2015 allowed Dutch courts jurisdiction; UK High Court 2025 found Shell liable
- **Key Ruling**: Shell plc and its former Nigerian subsidiary can be held legally responsible for legacy/historic oil pollution
- **Remedies Ordered**: Installation of leak detection systems (LDS) at Oruma pipelines I and II within one year
- **Compensation**: $16 million to four farmers and communities
- **Trial for complicity**: Scheduled for early 2027
- **HSE Lessons**: Pipeline integrity management, leak detection, environmental monitoring, corporate accountability, community impact
- **Source**: https://www.leighday.co.uk/news/press-releases/2025-news/high-court-trial-finds-shell-plc-and-its-former-nigerian-subsidiary-can-be-held-legally-responsible-for-legacy-oil-pollution-in-nigeria/

### 4.8 UNEP Ogoniland Environmental Assessment (2011)
- **Scope**: 200+ locations examined, 122 km of pipeline rights of way surveyed
- **Findings**: 50+ years of oil operations contamination; drinking water contaminated in 10+ communities; benzene levels 900x above WHO guidelines in Nisisioken Ogale
- **Remediation**: $1 billion initial cost; 5 years for individual sites; 30 years for mangrove/swamp restoration
- **Government Response**: HYPREP (Hydrocarbon Pollution Restoration Project) established July 2012
- **HSE Lessons**: Long-term environmental monitoring, remediation planning, community health surveillance, corporate environmental liability
- **Source**: https://www.unep.org/topics/disasters-and-conflicts/country-presence/nigeria/environmental-assessment-ogoniland-report

### 4.9 Majiya Fuel Tanker Explosion (2024)
- **Date**: October 15, 2024
- **Location**: Majiya, Jigawa State
- **Casualties**: 209 dead, 124 injured
- **Cause**: Fuel tanker accident; villagers rushed to collect spilled fuel; explosion and massive fire
- **HSE Lessons**: Transportation safety, tanker design standards, community hazard awareness, emergency response, fuel handling safety
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Majiya_fuel_tanker_explosion

### 4.10 Nigeria Building Collapses (Structural Safety Context)
- **Statistics**: 135 building collapses and 26+ deaths between 2022-2024; Lagos State accounts for 55.15% of incidents
- **2022**: 62 building collapse cases (Lagos: 20); **2023**: 52 cases (Lagos: 17); **2024**: 17 cases
- **Lagos (2011-2025 total)**: 171 building collapses
- **Root Causes**: Weak enforcement, substandard materials, disregard for safety regulations
- **HSE Lessons**: Structural integrity assessment, regulatory enforcement, material quality control, construction safety management
- **Source**: https://leadership.ng/nigeria-records-145-building-collapse-incidents-in-4-years/

## 5. Key Facts & Figures

### 5.1 Upstream Oil and Gas Fatalities (2024)
- 22 oil and gas workers died on duty between January and December 2024 (NUPRC official data)
- 31 operational incidents recorded in the Nigerian upstream oil and gas sector in 2024
- Source: https://apanews.net/nigerian-commission-says-22-oil-workers-died-on-duty-in-2024/

### 5.2 Cumulative Oil and Gas Fatalities (2018-2022)
- Over 412 lives lost between 2018 and 2022 due to safety lapses in Nigeria's oil and gas industry
- Source: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/267461492

### 5.3 Oil Spill Data (2023-2024)
- **2023**: 1,162 oil spill incidents recorded; approximately 18,747 barrels of crude spilled (~3 million litres)
- **2024**: 589 oil spills recorded (49.3% decrease from 2023); approximately 19,000 barrels spilled
- **2024 causes**: 471 spills (66%) from sabotage/oil theft; 100 spills from operational challenges
- **Cumulative Niger Delta pollution**: Estimated equivalent of 13+ million barrels of crude since extraction began in the 1950s
- **Exiting oil firms**: Polluted Niger Delta with 36.1 million litres of crude in 8 years
- Sources: https://nairametrics.com/2025/01/17/nigeria-recorded-over-589-oil-spills-in-2024-most-caused-by-oil-theft/ and https://www.arise.tv/nuprc-sabotage-caused-66-of-oil-spills-in-niger-delta-in-2024/

### 5.4 Gas Flaring Data (2023-2024)
- **2023**: ~183 MMscf gas flared (N140.54 billion in penalties collected)
- **2024**: ~192 MMscf gas flared (N391.26 billion in penalties collected); 7.69% of produced gas

## 8. Proposed Module Structure

### Module 1: Regulatory Framework and HSE Management Systems (4 Lessons)

**Lesson 1.1: Nigerian HSE Legal Landscape**
- Constitutional basis for worker safety (Section 17.3.c)
- Overview of key legislation: PIA 2021, Factories Act 2004, ECA 2010, EIA Act 1992
- Regulatory bodies: NUPRC, NMDPRA, NOSDRA, NESREA, Federal Ministry of Labour
- ISPON Act 2014 and mandatory practitioner registration
- ILO Conventions ratified by Nigeria (No. 155 and No. 187)
- **Assessment Focus**: Matching regulations to their scope; identifying the correct regulatory body for different scenarios

**Lesson 1.2: Petroleum Industry-Specific Regulations**
- PIA 2021 HSE provisions (NUPRC mandate, gas flaring penalties under Section 104)
- Upstream Petroleum Safety Regulations 2022/2024 (SOPs, confined space, safety permits)
- EGASPIN (environmental guidelines and standards for petroleum industry)
- Safety Case Guideline 2020 (ALARP principle, Formal Safety Assessment, Major Accident Hazards)
- NOSDRA Act (oil spill reporting, detection, response obligations)
- Flare Gas Regulations 2018 (permits, penalties, reporting)
- **Assessment Focus**: Applying regulatory requirements to operational scenarios; calculating penalty exposure

**Lesson 1.3: HSE Management Systems -- Framework and Implementation**
- ISO 45001:2018 requirements and PDCA cycle
- ISO 14001:2015 integration for environmental management
- HSE policy development and leadership commitment
- Organisational roles and responsibilities (HSE department structure)
- Competence, training, and awareness requirements
- Documentation and record-keeping requirements
- **Assessment Focus**: Designing an HSE management system framework; gap analysis against ISO 45001

**Lesson 1.4: HSE Planning, Monitoring, Audit, and Review**
- HSE objectives and targets (leading vs. lagging indicators)
- Safety performance metrics and KPIs (TRIR, LTIR, near-miss frequency, days away from work)
- Internal audit programme design and execution
- Management review process
- Continual improvement and corrective/preventive actions
- NUPRC audit and compliance expectations
- **Assessment Focus**: Interpreting safety performance data; designing an audit checklist

### Module 2: Hazard Identification, Risk Assessment, and Control (4 Lessons)

**Lesson 2.1: Hazard Identification Techniques**
- Types of hazards in oil and gas: chemical, physical, biological, ergonomic, psychosocial, mechanical
- HAZID (Hazard Identification) studies -- brainstorming methodology, application in design phase
- HAZOP (Hazard and Operability) studies -- deviation analysis for process operations
- Job Safety Analysis (JSA) / Job Hazard Analysis (JHA)
- Safety observation programmes and near-miss reporting
- **Assessment Focus**: Conducting a HAZID for a given scenario; identifying deviations in a HAZOP study

**Lesson 2.2: Risk Assessment Methods**
- Qualitative risk assessment (risk matrices, likelihood x consequence)
- Quantitative risk assessment (Fault Tree Analysis, Event Tree Analysis)
- ALARP principle and application in Nigerian safety case regime
- Bow-tie risk assessment method
- Hierarchy of controls (elimination, substitution, engineering, administrative, PPE)
- **Assessment Focus**: Completing a risk assessment matrix; determining if residual risk is ALARP

**Lesson 2.3: Permit-to-Work Systems**
- Purpose and principles of PTW
- Types of work permits:
  - Hot work permits (welding, cutting, grinding near hydrocarbons)
  - Confined space entry permits (tanks, vessels, silos)
  - Electrical isolation permits
  - Excavation permits
  - Working at height permits
  - Cold work permits
- PTW process: application, authorisation, execution, close-out
- Integration with LOTO (Lockout/Tagout) procedures
- Common PTW failures and near-misses in Nigerian operations
- **Assessment Focus**: Processing a PTW application; identifying deficiencies in a permit scenario

**Lesson 2.4: Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)**
- PPE as last line of defence in the hierarchy of controls
- PPE categories for oil and gas operations:
  - Head protection (hard hats, bump caps)
  - Eye/face protection (safety goggles, face shields)
  - Hearing protection (earplugs, earmuffs)
  - Respiratory protection (respirators, SCBA -- protection against H2S, benzene, diesel fumes)
  - Hand protection (chemical-resistant gloves, cut-resistant gloves)
  - Foot protection (safety boots, metatarsal guards)
  - Body protection (flame-resistant clothing -- EN ISO 11612, EN 1149 for anti-static)
  - Fall protection (harnesses, lanyards)
- PPE selection, inspection, maintenance, and replacement
- PPE compliance challenges in Nigerian operations (reference: Dangote case study)
- **Assessment Focus**: Selecting appropriate PPE for specific hazard scenarios; inspecting PPE

---

### Module 3: Fire Prevention, Emergency Response, and Environmental Management (4 Lessons)

**Lesson 3.1: Fire Prevention and Protection in Oil and Gas**
- Fire triangle and fire tetrahedron in hydrocarbon environments
- Sources of ignition in oil and gas operations
- Fire classification (Class A, B, C, D, K) and appropriate suppression methods
- Fire protection systems: water/foam suppression, fire extinguishers, fire alarms, deluge systems
- Fire safety training and drill requirements
- Passive fire protection (fireproofing, fire walls, fire-rated barriers)
- Hot work controls and fire watch procedures
- **Assessment Focus**: Selecting fire suppression method for specific scenarios; designing a fire drill

**Lesson 3.2: Emergency Preparedness and Response**
- Emergency action plan development and requirements
- Emergency scenarios in oil and gas: blowouts, well kicks, fires, explosions, toxic gas releases, oil spills, man overboard
- Emergency response organisation and roles
- Muster and evacuation procedures (onshore and offshore)
- Emergency equipment: lifeboats, life rafts, SCBA, fire fighting equipment
- Business continuity and crisis management
- Case study: K.S. Endeavor (Chevron) -- failed emergency response
- **Assessment Focus**: Developing an emergency response plan; conducting a tabletop drill scenario

**Lesson 3.3: Environmental Management in Petroleum Operations**
- EGASPIN requirements and compliance
- EIA process and requirements for oil and gas projects
- Waste management: hazardous and non-hazardous waste streams in E&P operations
- Effluent discharge standards and monitoring
- Air emissions management (flaring, venting, fugitive emissions)
- Gas flaring: regulations, penalties, and reduction strategies (PIA Section 104)
- Oil spill prevention, detection, and response (NOSDRA obligations)
- Environmental monitoring and reporting requirements
- **Assessment Focus**: Developing a waste management plan; calculating gas flaring penalty exposure

**Lesson 3.4: Oil Spill Response and Environmental Remediation**
- National Oil Spill Contingency Plan (NOSCP 2020) -- Tier 1/2/3 response
- NOSDRA reporting requirements and timelines (24-hour rule)
- Oil spill response techniques: mechanical containment, dispersants, bioremediation
- Forensic oil fingerprinting for source identification
- Post-spill environmental assessment
- Community consultation and compensation
- Case study: Ogoniland UNEP Assessment -- scale of contamination and remediation requirements
- Case study: Shell SPDC litigation and court-ordered remediation
- **Assessment Focus**: Designing a Tier 1 spill response plan; conducting a post-spill assessment

### Module 4: Incident Investigation, Occupational Health, and Professional Development (3 Lessons)

**Lesson 4.1: Incident Investigation and Root Cause Analysis**
- Incident classification: near-miss, first aid, medical treatment, lost time, fatality
- Legal reporting requirements (NUPRC, Factories Act, NSITF)
- Investigation methodologies:
  - 5 Whys technique
  - Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram
  - Fault Tree Analysis
  - Tripod Beta
  - BSCAT (Barrier-based SCAT)
  - Influence and Causal Factor Charting
- Evidence preservation and witness interviewing
- Investigation report writing and recommendations
- Corrective action tracking and verification
- Under-reporting culture and barriers to effective investigation
- Case study: Analysis of Nigerian upstream fatalities (22 deaths, 31 incidents in 2024)
- **Assessment Focus**: Conducting a root cause analysis using multiple methods; writing an investigation report

**Lesson 4.2: Occupational Health and Workplace Welfare**
- Occupational health hazards in oil and gas: noise, vibration, chemical exposure, heat stress, radiation
- Occupational diseases: hearing loss, respiratory conditions, dermatitis, musculoskeletal disorders
- Health surveillance and medical monitoring programmes
- Fitness-to-work assessments
- Workplace welfare requirements under Factories Act (ventilation, lighting, cleanliness, sanitation)
- Mental health and psychosocial hazards
- Employees' Compensation Act 2010: no-fault liability, compensable injuries, benefits available
- NSITF claims process and employer obligations (1% payroll contribution)
- **Assessment Focus**: Designing a health surveillance programme; processing a compensation claim scenario

**Lesson 4.3: Professional Development and Safety Leadership**
- ISPON registration requirements and career pathway (Student to MISPN)
- NEBOSH, IOSH, and WSO certification pathways
- Safety culture: definition, assessment, improvement strategies
- Behavioural-based safety (BBS) programmes
- Safety leadership and management commitment
- Stop Work Authority (SWA) -- right and responsibility to stop unsafe work
- Contractor HSE management and pre-qualification
- Continuous professional development (CPD) requirements
- Industry networking: SPE Nigeria, ISPON conferences, IOSH Nigeria network
- **Assessment Focus**: Creating a personal development plan; designing a safety culture improvement programme
