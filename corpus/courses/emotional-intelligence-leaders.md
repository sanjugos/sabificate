- **Full Title:** Corporate Governance Guidelines for Commercial, Merchant, Non-Interest, Payment Services Banks and Financial Holding Companies in Nigeria
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN)

### 2.2 Cross-Cultural & African Context Texts

#### 2.2.1 Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions -- Nigeria Profile

- **Key Reference:** Hofstede's Cultural Dimensions of Values and Personal Value Orientation of Nigerian Managers (Academia.edu)
- **Source:** https://www.academia.edu/9965383/ | https://www.researchgate.net/publication/365207467
- **BusinessDay Analysis:** https://businessday.ng/columnist/article/evaluating-nigeria-using-geert-hofstedes-six-dimension-of-culture/

**Nigeria's Cultural Dimension Scores:**

| Dimension | Score | Implication for EI |
|-----------|-------|-------------------|
| Power Distance | 80 (Very High) | Leaders must bridge hierarchical gaps with empathy; subordinates expect benevolent authority |
| Individualism | 30 (Collectivist) | EI manifests through in-group loyalty and relationship obligation management |
| Masculinity | 60 (Moderately High) | Achievement orientation; emotional expression may be constrained by gender norms |
| Uncertainty Avoidance | 55 (Moderate) | Moderate tolerance for ambiguity; leaders need self-regulation in volatile economic conditions |
| Long-Term Orientation | 13 (Very Low) | Short-term thinking; EI needed to sustain long-term vision commitment |
| Indulgence | 84 (Very High) | Emotional expressiveness valued; leaders can leverage positive affect |

**Key Insight for Course:** Nigeria's combination of high power distance + collectivism + high indulgence creates a unique EI context: leaders are expected to be authoritative yet emotionally present, hierarchical yet relationally warm. This "benevolent autocrat" model is the Nigerian leadership archetype that EI training must work within rather than against.

#### 2.2.2 Cross-Cultural EI Research -- Meta-Analysis

- **Key Study:** "A Cross-Cultural Meta-Analysis of How Leader Emotional Intelligence Influences Subordinate Task Performance and Organizational Citizenship Behavior" (ScienceDirect)
- **Source:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1090951617304066

**Key Findings:**
- Leader EI --> subordinate task performance relationship is **stronger** in collectivistic, feminine, and high uncertainty avoidance cultures
- Leader EI --> subordinate OCB relationship is **stronger** in high power distance, collectivistic, feminine, high uncertainty avoidance, long-term oriented, and restraint cultures
- In high power distance cultures, emotionally intelligent leaders must prioritise empathy and interpersonal sensitivity to bridge hierarchical gaps
- In collectivist cultures, team-oriented recognition programmes are more effective than personalised approaches

**Implication:** Nigeria's cultural profile (high power distance + collectivist) means leader EI has an **amplified effect** on subordinate performance compared to Western individualist cultures. This is a powerful selling point for the course: EI training delivers higher ROI in Nigeria than in the US or UK.

## 4. NIGERIAN CASE STUDIES

### 4.1 Tony Elumelu -- Africapitalism and People-Centric Leadership

- **Subject:** Tony Elumelu, Chairman of Heirs Holdings, Founder of Tony Elumelu Foundation
- **EI Competencies Demonstrated:** Empathy, social skills, motivation, relationship management

**Case Details:**
- Leadership philosophy grounded in "Africapitalism" -- belief that Africa's prosperity comes from entrepreneurship and private-sector leadership, not aid
- People-centric approach: "the key to successful business lies in empowering employees and fostering a culture of innovation"
- "Leadership begins with clarity of purpose and consistency in execution, but is sustained by relationships"
- Mantra: "Africa's future is in the hands of its people"
- In January 2026, Elumelu publicly urged leaders to "rethink authority, trust and team relationships" -- a direct EI message
- Tony Elumelu Foundation: 24,000+ entrepreneurs supported, 1.5 million+ jobs created, billions in collective revenue

**EI Lesson:** Elumelu exemplifies how empathy and social skills scale -- from individual relationships to continental impact. His Africapitalism philosophy demonstrates how motivation (the EI domain of intrinsic drive toward purpose) can create systemic change.

**Source:** https://leadership.ng/tony-elumelu-the-architect-of-africapitalism-africas-entrepreneurial-renaissance/ | https://www.billionaires.africa/2026/01/16/tony-elumelu-urges-leaders-to-rethink-authority-trust-and-team-relationships/

### 4.2 Herbert Wigwe -- Visionary Leadership and Succession Crisis

- **Subject:** Dr Herbert Wigwe, Group CEO of Access Corporation Plc
- **EI Competencies Demonstrated:** Vision, motivation, relationship management; and the test of institutional EI when a leader is suddenly lost

**Case Details:**
- Wigwe and business partner Aigboje Aig-Imoukhuede acquired Access Bank in 2002 when it ranked 65th of 89 Nigerian commercial banks
- Under Wigwe's leadership, Access Bank became Nigeria's largest by total assets, expanding into Rwanda, South Africa, Mozambique, Kenya, Zambia, and beyond
- Wigwe died alongside his wife and son in a helicopter accident on February 9, 2024
- Access Bank successfully activated succession processes, appointing Bolaji Agbede as Acting Group Managing Director
- "He built a blueprint for what African business leadership can look like -- bold, continental in vision, socially conscious and unapologetically ambitious"

**EI Lesson:** Two-part case study: (1) How Wigwe's emotional intelligence -- his vision, relationship-building, and motivational leadership -- transformed a bottom-tier bank into a continental champion. (2) How institutional EI (the organisation's collective emotional resilience and crisis-readiness) enabled smooth succession in the face of tragic loss. Connects directly to CBN succession planning mandate.

**Source:** https://www.theaccessbankukltd.co.uk/honouring-the-life-of-herbert-wigwe-a-visionary-leader/ | https://www.semafor.com/article/02/12/2024/herbert-wigwe-access-bank-starts-succession-process-after-founders-death

### 4.3 Jim Ovia -- Zenith Bank Succession Planning as EI Legacy

- **Subject:** Jim Ovia, Founder of Zenith Bank (the "Godfather of Banking" -- Forbes Africa)
- **EI Competencies Demonstrated:** Self-awareness, long-term motivation, relationship management, succession planning as emotional intelligence

**Case Details:**
- Founded Zenith Bank and built it into one of Nigeria's top-tier financial institutions
- Ovia's talent development model meant Zenith Bank never needed to import senior leadership from outside
- Succession pipeline produced leaders who became nationally significant: Godwin Emefiele (later CBN Governor), Peter Amangbo, Dame Adaora Umeoji (CEO 2025)

- Philosophy: Integrity is "making your word your bond, respecting corporate governance, rule of law, and honouring contracts"
- Emphasised corporate governance, capital adequacy, operational efficiency, and early technology investment

**EI Lesson:** Ovia demonstrates how self-awareness and long-term thinking (EI's motivation domain) create institutional resilience. His succession planning proves that developing others -- a core relationship management competency -- is the ultimate expression of leadership EI. His 12-year governance-compliant exit shows self-regulation in practice.

**Case Details:**
- Appointed during 2009 banking crisis; immediately fired CEOs of 8 leading banks over massive non-performing loans
- Reformed Nigeria's banking sector; created AMCON (Asset Management Corporation of Nigeria) -- result: no depositor or creditor lost money in any Nigerian bank
- Suspended in February 2014 by President Goodluck Jonathan after revealing that approximately $20 billion in public oil revenue was unaccounted for
- "Widely respected by the international financial community for his technical expertise and for his reform of the Nigerian banking system"
- Navigated enormous political pressure while maintaining institutional integrity

**EI Lesson:** Sanusi's case illustrates the tension between self-regulation (maintaining composure under political threat) and social awareness (understanding the political landscape while choosing to speak truth). His firing of 8 bank CEOs demonstrates decisive action guided by empathy for depositors and the broader economy. The case also raises the question: when does high EI require accepting personal cost for institutional integrity?

**Source:** https://www.cfr.org/blog/lamido-sanusi-nigerias-central-bank-governor-bombshell-suspension | https://www.icirnigeria.org/why-jonathan-suspended-sanusi-lamido-as-cbn-governor/

### 4.5 Naira Redesign Crisis 2023 -- Leadership Failure and Stakeholder Empathy

- **Subject:** CBN under Governor Godwin Emefiele; corporate leadership response
- **EI Competencies Demonstrated (Failure):** Lack of empathy, poor social awareness, failed stakeholder management

**Case Details:**
- CBN announced naira redesign in late 2022 with January 31, 2023 deadline for old notes
- Deadline extended to February 10 after massive pressure from banks, politicians, and corporate organisations -- demonstrating poor initial stakeholder assessment
- Economy "almost came to a halt" between January and March 2023 due to inability to access cash
- Private sector productivity fell to 42.3 points in March 2023 -- most significant decline since PMI survey inception in January 2014 (excluding COVID-19)
- Manufacturing companies suffered 20-30% decreases in sales turnover
- Policy "disrupted the social and economic life of citizens"
- Emefiele was later arrested and charged with criminal offences (separate from naira redesign)

**EI Lesson:** This is a case study in empathy failure at the highest level. The policy demonstrated: (1) lack of social awareness -- failing to anticipate how a largely cash-based economy would respond; (2) poor self-regulation -- doubling down rather than course-correcting; (3) absence of relationship management -- alienating stakeholders across all sectors. Contrast with Sanusi's reform approach which was equally bold but accompanied by clear communication and stakeholder support mechanisms.

**Source:** https://carnegieendowment.org/2024/01/18/why-nigeria-s-controversial-naira-redesign-policy-hasn-t-met-its-objectives-pub-91405 | https://leadership.ng/manufacturers-suffer-30-decrease-in-sales-as-naira-crisis-lingers/

### 4.6 COVID-19 -- Compassionate Leadership in Nigerian Organisations

- **Subject:** Corporate leadership during COVID-19 pandemic in Nigeria
- **EI Competencies Tested:** Empathy, self-regulation, social awareness, adaptability

**Research Findings:**
- Study: "Exploring compassionate managerial leadership style in reducing employee stress level during COVID-19 crisis: the case of Nigeria" (Emerald Publishing / ScienceDirect)
- Focused on Nigerian banking and manufacturing sectors
- Key finding: Compassionate managerial leadership can drive considerate response to employees' concerns about job security, healthcare risk, work overload, underpayment, and delayed payment
- "Nigeria is yet to demonstrate genuine commitment to implementing policies that will protect employees' wellbeing"
- EI allows business leaders to deal with the "human and emotional complexities of situations like the COVID-19 pandemic"

**EI Lesson:** COVID-19 exposed the gap between Nigerian leaders who possessed and exercised emotional intelligence and those who did not. Leaders with high empathy and social awareness adapted faster, retained talent, and maintained productivity. The crisis demonstrated that EI is not a "soft" skill but a survival competency.

**Source:** https://emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/ER-06-2020-0302/full/html | https://www.researchgate.net/publication/351232994

### 4.7 Fuel Subsidy Removal 2023 -- Crisis Leadership and Employee Welfare

- **Subject:** Corporate responses to President Tinubu's fuel subsidy removal (May 29, 2023)
- **EI Competencies Tested:** Empathy, social awareness, crisis communication

**Case Details:**
- Petrol price skyrocketed 300% (from NGN 185 to NGN 650 per litre) immediately after announcement
- Trade Union Congress (TUC) and Manufacturing Association of Nigeria (MAN) warned of drastic impact on workers and businesses
- Labour unions called off work multiple times; protests across cities
- Corporate leaders faced immediate decisions: absorb transport costs for employees? Adjust salaries? Allow remote work? Reduce workforce?
- Subsidy had cost $10 billion in 2022 -- removal was fiscally necessary but socially devastating

**EI Lesson:** Leaders who demonstrated empathy (acknowledging employee hardship), social awareness (reading the political and social mood), and self-regulation (avoiding panic decisions) navigated the crisis more effectively. This case connects directly to the course's "leading through crisis" module.

**Source:** https://businessday.ng/columnist/article/nigerias-fuel-crisis-subsidy-removal-was-inevitable-but-more-transparency-is-needed/ | https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2023/5/31/nigeria-fuel-subsidy-cut-spiralling-costs-all-you-need-to-know

### 4.8 #EndSARS 2020 -- Corporate Leadership in Social Crisis

- **Subject:** Corporate responses to the #EndSARS movement and Lekki toll gate shooting (October 2020)
- **EI Competencies Tested:** Social awareness, self-regulation, moral courage, stakeholder management

**Case Details:**
- Decentralised social movement against police brutality; top Twitter hashtag globally
- Military opened fire on peaceful protesters at Lekki toll gate, October 20, 2020
- Lagos economy lost approximately NGN 700 billion (Lagos Chamber of Commerce estimate)
- Corporate leaders faced pressure from employees (especially younger staff) to take public positions
- Some brands spoke out; others stayed silent; some were criticised for performative responses
- "Nigeria's youth found its voice" -- generational leadership moment

**EI Lesson:** This case study sits at the intersection of social awareness, self-regulation, and generational EI. Leaders had to balance institutional risk (self-regulation) against employee expectations and social conscience (social awareness and empathy). The most effective responses came from leaders who showed authentic empathy while maintaining institutional stability -- the essence of emotionally intelligent crisis leadership.

**Source:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8918889/ | https://gjia.georgetown.edu/2021/12/13/endsars-a-evaluation-of-successes-and-failures-one-year-later/

### 4.9 Dangote -- The Benevolent Autocrat and Interfaith Neutrality

- **Subject:** Aliko Dangote, Founder of Dangote Group
- **EI Competencies Demonstrated:** Self-regulation, social awareness, motivation, cross-cultural management

**Case Details:**
- Transformational leadership style: "focuses on establishing links between him and those he leads, engaging with others freely and working towards creating a good connection that enhances morality and motivation"
- Corporate culture "prioritises competence and discipline over identity" -- faith-neutral recruitment and promotion
- Operates across diverse African regions with massive ethnic, religious, and linguistic diversity
- Built the largest cement factory with latest technology "just to increase efficiency"
- Management approach combines hierarchical authority (expected in Nigerian culture) with relationship-based trust-building

**EI Lesson:** Dangote demonstrates how social awareness (reading and navigating Nigeria's complex ethnic and religious landscape) combined with self-regulation (maintaining interfaith neutrality) creates a leadership model that transcends tribal and sectarian boundaries. His "competence over identity" principle is an EI-informed approach to diversity management in a high-diversity context.

**Source:** https://samploon.com/successful-leadership-analysis-aliko-dangote/ | https://leadership.ng/aliko-dangote-and-the-ethics-of-interfaith-neutrality-in-african-business/

### 4.10 Generational Leadership Transition in Nigerian Workplaces

- **Subject:** The silent generational cold war in Nigerian boardrooms
- **EI Competencies Required:** Empathy, social awareness, relationship management, self-awareness

**Research Findings:**
- "The Nigerian boardroom is currently the site of a silent, generational cold war" (BusinessDay)
- Generation Z views corporate social responsibility as a hiring requirement; wants labour to contribute to a better society
- Millennials are "more dedicated to their careers than companies" and believe work should be measured by output not hours
- Younger employees feel undervalued while older colleagues perceive a lack of respect for experience and authority
- Recommended solution: "reverse mentorship" where younger employees train senior executives on emerging technologies and inclusive culture
- Trust between younger employees and management plays a pivotal role in enhancing workplace productivity

**EI Lesson:** Managing generational diversity in Nigeria requires the full spectrum of EI competencies. Self-awareness (recognising one's own generational biases), empathy (understanding different generational values without judgment), and relationship management (building trust across age divides) are essential. The Nigerian context amplifies this challenge because traditional age-based respect hierarchies collide with younger workers' expectations for meritocratic, purpose-driven leadership.
