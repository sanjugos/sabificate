### 1.1 Pension Reform Act 2014 (PRA 2014)

- **Full Title:** Pension Reform (Repeal and Re-Enactment) Act 2014
- **Enacted:** July 1, 2014
- **Purpose:** Repeals the Pension Reform Act 2004; governs and regulates the administration of the uniform contributory pension scheme for both public and private sectors in Nigeria
- **Official Source (PenCom):** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/pra2014/
- **Full Text PDF (PLACNG):** http://lawsofnigeria.placng.org/laws/P4.pdf
- **Law Nigeria Mirror:** https://lawnigeria.com/2020/01/12/pension-reform-act-repeal-and-re-enactment-act-2014/
- **KPMG Analysis:** https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/pdf/2014/07/nigeria-july23-2014.pdf
- **PwC Analysis:** https://www.pwc.com/ng/en/assets/pdf/tax-bites-july-2014.pdf
- **Mondaq Highlights:** https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/retirement-superannuation-pensions/328318/pension-reform-act-2014-the-key-highlights-and-salient-points
- **Academic Analysis (2025):** https://eajournals.org/gjplr/wp-content/uploads/sites/36/2025/05/An-Analysis-of-The-Pension-Reform-Act-2014.pdf

**Structure:** 15 Parts, 121 Sections, 2 Schedules

**Key Parts and Sections:**

| Part | Subject Area | Key Sections |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| Part I | Objectives and Application | Ss. 1-2: Scope -- applies to public and private sectors; employers with 3+ employees |
| Part II | Contributory Pension Scheme | Ss. 3-8: Contribution rates (employer 10%, employee 8% of monthly emoluments); employer may bear full 20%; emoluments = basic salary + housing + transport allowance |
| Part III | Retirement Benefits | Ss. 9-16: Lump sum withdrawal (max 25% of RSA balance at retirement); balance used for programmed withdrawal or annuity purchase |
| Part IV | Retirement Savings Account | Ss. 17-22: RSA portability; right to transfer between PFAs; one RSA per contributor |
| Part V | Pension Fund Administrators (PFAs) | Ss. 23-41: Licensing, obligations, investment of pension funds, prohibited transactions |
| Part VI | Pension Fund Custodians (PFCs) | Ss. 42-52: Custodial responsibilities, safekeeping of assets, reporting |
| Part VII | National Pension Commission (PenCom) | Ss. 53-62: Establishment, composition, powers, functions of the Commission |
| Part VIII | Investment of Pension Fund Assets | Ss. 63-68: Permissible investments, risk management requirements, compliance officers |
| Part IX | Pension Transitional Arrangements | Ss. 69-76: Transition from Defined Benefit Scheme to CPS |
| Part X | Penalties | Ss. 77-88: Sanctions for non-compliance by employers, PFAs, PFCs |
| Part XI | Exempted Persons | Ss. 89-91: Military, intelligence services exemptions |
| Part XII | Additional Voluntary Contributions | S. 92: Voluntary contributions above mandatory minimum |
| Part XIII | Group Life Insurance | Ss. 93-95: Mandatory group life insurance by employers |
| Part XIV | General Provisions | Ss. 96-119: Miscellaneous provisions |
| Part XV | Transitional and Savings | Ss. 120-121: Transitional provisions |

**Critical Sections for Compliance Officers:**
- **Section 4(1):** Minimum contribution rates -- employer 10%, employee 8%
- **Section 11(3-7):** Payment of retirement benefits procedures
- **Section 18:** RSA registration and maintenance
- **Section 20:** Transfer of RSA between PFAs
- **Section 55(f-g):** PFA obligation to compute and pay retirement benefits
- **Section 60(1)(b), 62(b), 115(1):** Basis for minimum capital requirements
- **Section 66:** Requirement for Risk Management Committee in every PFA
- **Section 68:** Requirement for Compliance Officer in every PFA/CPFA
- **Section 94:** Employer must maintain Group Life Insurance (minimum 3x annual emoluments)

### 1.2 PenCom Corporate Governance Guidelines (February 2021)

- **Full Title:** Guidelines on Corporate Governance for Pension Fund Operators
- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **Date Issued:** February 2021
- **Official PDF:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Guidelines-on-Corporate-Governance-for-Pension-Fund-Operators-February-2021.pdf
- **Mondaq Analysis:** https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/shareholders/1471344/understanding-corporate-governance-for-licensed-pension-operators-in-nigeria

**Key Provisions:**
- Board composition: NEDs (excluding Chairman) must equal executive members; minimum 1 Independent Director
- Role separation: CEO and Chairman must be separate offices; if combined, relationship must be disclosed to PenCom
- Director independence: no employment by company/related companies in past 3 years; no family members employed; no 5%+ equity stake
- Mandatory annual performance evaluations of board, committees, and individual directors; results submitted to PenCom
- Conflict of interest: PFAs cannot maintain undisclosed relationships with PFCs; spousal relationships with Commission staff prohibited
- Related-party transactions require disclosure in audited financial statements
- Remuneration policies and director compensation require annual disclosure
- Non-compliance triggers Commission-prescribed sanctions

### 1.4 Regulation on Investment of Pension Fund Assets (Revised 2025)

- **Full Title:** Revised Regulation on Investment of Pension Fund Assets
- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **Date Revised:** September 2025
- **Official PDF:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/The-Revised-Regulation-on-Investment-of-Pension-Fund-Assets.pdf
- **PensionNigeria Highlights:** https://www.pensionnigeria.com/pension-news/key-highlights-of-the-revised-regulation-on-investment-of-pension-fund-assets/
- **BusinessDay Report:** https://archive.businessday.ng/insurance/article/pencoms-new-investment-guideline-seeks-safety-fair-returns-funds/

**Investment Allocation Limits:**

| Asset Class | Maximum Allocation |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Federal Government Bonds | Up to 100% |
| State Government Bonds | Up to 20% (max 2% per state) |
| Corporate Bonds / Debt Instruments (incl. REITs, mortgages, ABS) | Up to 30% |
| Money Market Instruments | Up to 35% |
| Ordinary Shares (Equities) | Up to 25% |

**Key Rules:**
- PFAs must recruit and retain highly skilled investment department personnel
- No trading on margin accounts with pension fund assets
- No borrowing or lending of pension fund assets
- No investment in instruments subject to prohibitions on sale/purchase (except allowed open/close-end/hybrid/specialist funds)

- **Introduced:** July 2018 (pursuant to PRA 2014 and 2018 amendment to Investment Regulation)

- **FCMB Pensions Explainer:** https://fcmbpensions.com/multi-fund-structure
- **BusinessDay Explainer:** https://businessday.ng/news/article/contributory-pension-scheme-the-multi-fund-structure-explained/

**Six-Fund Structure:**

| Fund | Target Contributors | Risk Profile | Key Feature |
|------|-------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Fund I | Active contributors aged 49 and below (by election) | High growth | Highest equity allocation; suitable for long work-life horizon |
| Fund II | Active contributors aged 49 and below (default) | Moderate growth | Default fund for most active contributors |
| Fund III | Active contributors aged 50+ (pre-retirement) | Conservative | Reduced equity exposure; capital preservation focus |
| Fund IV | Retirees | Very conservative | Retiree fund; prevents asset value diminishment between retirement and benefit collection |
| Fund V | Micro Pension contributors | Flexible | For informal sector participants |
| Fund VI | Sharia-compliant (Active + Retiree) | Non-interest | Separated into active and retiree sub-funds; all PFAs must create and maintain |

### 1.6 Guidelines for Risk Management Framework for Licensed Pension Operators

- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **Official PDF:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/Guidelines_for_Risk_Management_Framework_for_Licensed_Pension_Operators.pdf
- **PenCom Page:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/guidelines-for-risk-management-framework-for-licensed-pension-operators/
- **Legal Basis:** Section 66 of PRA 2004 (carried forward in PRA 2014)

**Key Requirements:**
- Every PFA must establish a Risk Management Committee
- Committee purpose: determine risk profile of investments under management and advise on associated risks
- PFAs must exercise due diligence and implement robust risk management practices
- Risk management essential to growth and credibility of pension industry
- PenCom adopted risk-based supervision approach aligned with Financial Services Regulation Coordinating Committee (FSRCC) framework

### 1.7 Regulations for Compliance Officers

- **Full Title:** Regulations for Compliance Officers (RR/P&R/09/03)
- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **Official PDF:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/Regulations_on_Compliance_Officers.pdf
- **PenCom Category Page:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/category/regulations-codes/regulations/regulation-for-compliance-officers/
- **Legal Basis:** Section 68 of PRA 2004 (carried forward in PRA 2014)

**Key Provisions:**
- Every PFA and CPFA must employ a Compliance Officer
- Compliance Officer responsibilities: ensure compliance with PRA provisions, PenCom rules/regulations/circulars, and the PFA's internal rules
- Compliance Officers must attend PenCom-organized training workshops
- Pension operators must ensure Compliance Officers attend training programmes to address identified knowledge gaps
- Must report directly to the Board/senior management on compliance matters

### 1.10 Guidelines for Micro Pension Plan (2018) / Personal Pension Plan (2025)

- **Micro Pension Plan Guidelines (2018):** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/GUIDELINES-FOR-MICRO-PENSION-PLAN.pdf
- **Personal Pension Plan Guidelines (2025):** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Guidelines-for-Personal-Pension-Plan.pdf
- **Legal Basis:** Section 2(3) of PRA 2014

**Micro Pension Plan (MPP) Key Features:**
- Target: self-employed, employees of organizations with fewer than 3 employees, informal sector workers
- Eligibility: age 18+, resident in Nigeria, belonging to trade/profession/cooperative/business association or having business registration
- Contributions: daily, weekly, or monthly; via cash, electronic transfer, or CBN-approved payment platforms
- Uptake as of 2025: approximately 200,000 contributors, approximately N1 billion AUM

**Personal Pension Plan (PPP) -- Redesign of MPP:**
- PenCom redesigned Micro Pension Plan under new name to improve uptake
- Addresses challenges of low enrollment in informal sector coverage

### 1.11 Guidelines on Foreign Currency Pension Contributions (September 2025)

- **Official PDF:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Guidelines-on-Foreign-Currency-Pension-ContributionsSeptember-2025.pdf
- **Nairametrics Report:** http://nairametrics.com/2025/09/24/pencom-allows-nigerians-abroad-to-save-pensions-in-dollars/
- **PensionNigeria Explainer:** https://www.pensionnigeria.com/pension-news/understanding-pencoms-new-guidelines-on-foreign-currency-pension-contributions/

**Key Provisions:**
- Eligible contributors: Nigerians abroad; Nigerian/foreign workers in Nigeria earning in foreign currencies
- Diaspora contributors must open Non-Resident Nigerian Ordinary Account (NRNOA)
- Nigeria-resident contributors must open Domiciliary Accounts (DA) with PFC collecting bank
- Contribution split: 60% accessible for contingent withdrawals before retirement; 40% preserved strictly for retirement
- Permissible investments: dollar-denominated FGN bonds, Eurobonds, supranational bonds, eligible Nigerian corporate bonds on global exchanges
- AML reporting: contributions exceeding $10,000 must be reported to NFIU within 24 hours

### 1.13 PenCom Circular on Approval and Payment of Benefits by PFAs (March 2025)

- **Effective Date:** June 1, 2025
- **KPMG Analysis:** https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/ng/pdf/2025/03/PenCom%20Circular%20on%20Approval%20and%20Payments%20of%20Benefits%20by%20PFAs%20-%2012%20March%202025.pdf
- **KPMG Flash Alert:** https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/gms-flash-alert/flash-alert-2025-078.html

**Key Change:**
- PFAs now responsible for approving qualifying benefits applications (previously required PenCom pre-approval)
- Amendments to five regulatory instruments enabling PFAs to process and approve: retirement benefits, voluntary contributions, mortgage equity contributions, and additional benefits without prior PenCom approval

### 1.14 Anti-Money Laundering Requirements for Pension Operators

**Key Requirements:**
- Foreign currency contributions exceeding $10,000: report to NFIU within 24 hours (aligned with Section 3(1) of Money Laundering (Prevention and Prohibition) Act 2022)
- Local voluntary contributions of N5 million and above: report to NFIU per Money Laundering Act 2011 and NDLEA requirements
- Comprehensive KYC checks for all contributors: NIN or international passport plus next-of-kin information
- Voluntary contributions can only be withdrawn once every two years
- Full traceability of all transactions required; complete KYC records maintained

## 4. NIGERIAN CASE STUDIES

### 4.1 Pension Fund Scandals and Failures

**Police Pension Fund Scandal (2012):**
- Police Pension Reform Task Team accused of looting N197 billion
- Abdulrasheed Mina, head of pension fund under President Goodluck Jonathan, implicated in embezzlement (2013)
- One of the most high-profile pension fraud cases in Nigeria's history
- **Source:** https://samphina.com.ng/pension-scandal-nigeriaimpact-public-fund-management/

**Railway Corporation Pension Crisis:**
- Three pensioners of Nigerian Railway Corporation died while waiting for pension arrears of 20 months
- Umbrella body alleged that 3,000 members had died since the pension crisis started
- Highlights consequences of non-remittance and delayed payments

**2026 Pension System Scandal:**
- Vanguard investigation into ongoing pension system issues
- **Source:** https://www.vanguardngr.com/2026/04/inside-nigerias-pension-system-scandal/

### 4.2 Employer Non-Compliance

**PenCom Recovery Operations (2012-2025):**
- Cumulative N32.27 billion recovered from defaulting employers (June 2012 to September 2025)
- Comprising N15.87 billion in outstanding contributions and N16.40 billion in penalties
- Q3 2025 alone: N2.06 billion recovered from 49 defaulting employers (N775 million principal + N1.27 billion penalties)
- PenCom executed MoU with ICPC to hold management of recalcitrant employers personally accountable, with potential criminal implications
- **Sources:**
  - https://guardian.ng/business-services/pencom-to-sanction-non-compliance-with-pension-law-recovers-n32-27-billion/
  - https://businessday.ng/news/article/pencom-recovers-n32-27bn-from-defaulting-employers-since-2012/

**State Government Non-Compliance:**
- As of 2024-2025, only approximately 11 of 36 states have fully implemented CPS
- Many states operate old Defined Benefit Scheme without adequate funding or fail to remit employer and employee contributions
- **Source:** https://www.ajol.info/index.php/ngjsd/article/download/279251/263421/654907

### 4.3 PFA Regulatory Issues

**RSA Holder Inducement:**
- PenCom warned PFAs against inducing RSA holders to transfer accounts
- Sanctions include: monetary penalties, other regulatory sanctions, suspension of RSA Transfer window for affected PFAs
- **Source:** https://punchng.com/pencom-warns-pfas-against-inducing-rsa-holders/

**Forced Pension Fund Transfers:**
- PenCom announced sanctions against PFAs involved in forced pension fund transfers
- **Source:** https://thenationonlineng.net/pencom-to-sanction-pfas-over-forced-pension-fund-transfer/

### 4.4 Successful Reform Milestones

**Contributory Pension Scheme (CPS) Achievement:**
- Transformation from unsustainable Defined Benefit Scheme to funded, transparent, and sustainable system
- Pension assets CAGR of over 20% since 2007
- RSA membership grew from 9,529,127 (2021) to 11,084,127 (January 2026) -- 15.9% increase
- Private sector fund management improved efficiency and reliability
- **Sources:**
  - https://pmnewsnigeria.com/2025/01/09/evaluating-achievements-challenges-of-nigerias-pension-reforms/
  - https://businessday.ng/opinion/article/the-journey-so-far-20-years-of-contributory-pension-scheme-in-nigeria/

**Pension Revolution 2.0 (September 2025):**
- Described as "the most significant advancement since 2004" by PenCom director-general
- Five core objectives: dignity for retirees (health insurance, minimum pension guarantee); investment performance optimization; expanded coverage including informal sector
- Daily regulatory releases beginning September 2025 covering investment management, governance, compliance, financial inclusion, service delivery
- Health insurance pilot for retirees and Minimum Pension Guarantee activation within first 3 months
- **Sources:**
  - https://nairametrics.com/2025/09/23/pencom-unveils-pension-revolution-2-0-sets-stage-for-industry-transformation/
  - https://leadership.ng/pencom-unveils-pension-revolution-2-0-to-strengthen-retirement-system-broaden-access/

**Multi-Fund Structure Implementation (2018):**
- Successful transition from single-fund to six-fund structure
- Aligned contributor risk appetite with investment horizon
- Fund II became the largest pool at N11.86 trillion NAV (January 2026)

### 5.12 Benefit Computation Key Formulas

**Programmed Withdrawal:**
- Monthly Pension = RSA Balance / Number of Expected Monthly Payments (Life Expectancy in Months)
- Contributor can withdraw lump sum (max 25% of RSA balance at retirement)
- Remaining 75% funds programmed withdrawal or annuity

**Retirement Options:**
- Programmed Withdrawal: periodic payments based on life expectancy; balance held by PFA
- Annuity: lump sum from RSA used to purchase annuity from licensed insurance company providing regular income for life
