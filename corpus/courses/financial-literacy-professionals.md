## Corpus Summary

This corpus covers personal and professional financial literacy for Nigerian working professionals, anchored in the CBN National Financial Literacy Framework (NFLF), the Nigeria Tax Act 2025 (effective January 2026 with revised PAYE bands and NRS rebrand), the Pension Reform Act 2014 and PenCom's Multi-Fund Structure, the Investments and Securities Act 2025 (criminalizing Ponzi schemes and regulating digital assets), the Money Laundering (Prevention and Prohibition) Act 2022 (personal cash limits of N5M), and the National Financial Inclusion Strategy (NFIS 3.0). The corpus includes 8 primary regulatory sources, 8 textbooks and reference works, 7 existing courses and syllabi, 9 Nigerian case studies (including MMM, MBA Forex, CBEX, and naira devaluation impact), 14 key facts and figures, 7 SME input gaps, and a proposed 4-module/15-lesson structure with adaptive difficulty tiers (beginner/intermediate/advanced). Cross-over potential with Canvas Credit Union noted for Module 2 budgeting content.

## 1. Primary Regulatory Sources

### 1.1 CBN National Financial Literacy Framework (NFLF)
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Relevance:** The overarching national blueprint for financial literacy. Goal is to empower citizens with knowledge through quality financial education for informed financial choices. Curriculum themes: money, savings, credit, insurance, deposit insurance, capital markets, pension, planning/budgeting, financial landscape, and entrepreneurship. Developed in collaboration with the Nigeria Education Research Development Council (NERDC).
- **Key Content:** Multi-stakeholder framework covering all financial sectors; defines competency levels from basic to advanced; intermittent professional in-house programs recommended for workplace delivery.
- **2026 Update:** CBN intensified youth financial literacy during 2026 Global Money Week ("Smart Money Talks" theme) as part of broader strategy to deepen financial inclusion and consumer awareness.
- **Reference:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/FinInc/FinLit/ | https://www.cbn.gov.ng/out/2016/cfpd/financial%20literacy.pdf

### 1.2 National Financial Inclusion Strategy (NFIS 3.0)
- **Issuing Body:** CBN / Federal Government of Nigeria
- **Relevance:** Sets national targets for financial inclusion, directly shaping financial literacy priorities. NFIS 3.0 target: reduce financial exclusion to under 5% by 2024, achieve 95% overall inclusion. As of 2023, exclusion stands at 26% (28.8 million adults). Financial inclusion reached 74% in 2023 (up from 68% in 2020). Formal financial inclusion at 64%.
- **Key Targets:** Savings: 60% target (actual 38% as of 2020); Credit: 40% target; Insurance: 40% target (actual penetration <1%); Payments: 70% target.
- **SANEF Expansion:** 2,021,338 agents onboarded; 1,903 access points per 100,000 adults.
- **Reference:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/dfd/Financialinclusion.html | https://www.cbn.gov.ng/out/2019/ccd/national%20financial%20inclusion%20strategy.pdf

### 1.3 Nigeria Tax Act (NTA) 2025 / Nigeria Revenue Service
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Republic of Nigeria (signed by President Tinubu, 26 June 2025)
- **Effective Date:** 1 January 2026
- **Relevance:** Comprehensive overhaul of personal income tax for all Nigerian salary earners. Replaces PITA provisions and renames FIRS to Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS). Critical for every working professional.
- **Key PAYE Provisions:**
  - **Tax Exemption:** Employees earning NGN 800,000 or less annually are exempt from PIT.
  - **Revised Tax Bands:** 7% on first N300K; 11% on next N300K; 15% on next N500K; 19% on next N500K; 21% on next N1.6M; 24% on next amounts; 25% on income above N50M.
  - **CRA Elimination:** Consolidated Relief Allowance (20% of gross + N200,000) replaced by rent relief of 20% of annual rent paid, capped at N500,000.
  - **Enhanced Penalties:** Stiffer penalties for employers who under-deduct or delay tax remittance.
  - **Broader PAYE Base:** All forms of remuneration included.
- **Reference:** https://www.safeguardglobal.com/resources/blog/nigeria-tax-act-2025/ | https://bizedgeapp.com/blog/what-you-need-to-know-about-the-new-paye-tax-law-in-nigeria

### 1.4 Investments and Securities Act (ISA) 2025
- **Issuing Body:** Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) Nigeria
- **Date:** 2025 (repeals ISA 2007)
- **Relevance:** Governs all investment activity by Nigerian professionals. Explicitly criminalizes Ponzi schemes with severe penalties. Expands "securities" definition to include virtual/digital assets, NFTs, and cryptocurrency. Makes it illegal to operate digital asset exchanges or online forex platforms without SEC registration.
- **Key Provisions:** Investor Protection Fund (IPF); enhanced oversight of capital market operators; fraud prevention provisions; SEC licensing requirements for investment platforms.
- **Reference:** https://sec.gov.ng/documents/1326/INVESTMENT-AND-SECURITIES-ACT-NIGERIA-2025_1.pdf | https://nairacompare.ng/blogs/current-sec-investment-regulations-in-nigeria-what-investors-must-know

### 1.5 Pension Reform Act 2014 & PenCom Regulations
- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **Relevance:** Governs retirement savings for all employed Nigerians. Mandates Contributory Pension Scheme (CPS) for public and private sector (10+ employees). Minimum contribution: 8% employee + 10% employer = 18% of basic salary + housing + transport.
- **Key Contributor Rights:** Choose PFA; choose investment fund type; determine pension payment mode; transfer between PFAs; access 25% of RSA balance after 4 months unemployment.
- **Multi-Fund Structure (2018 amendment):**
  - Fund I: Contributors aged 49 and below (highest equity exposure)
  - Fund II: Default fund for active contributors (moderate risk)
  - Fund III: Pre-retirement fund for contributors aged 50+ (conservative)
  - Fund IV: Retiree fund (capital preservation)
  - Fund V: Micro Pension Plan / Personal Pension Plan
  - Fund VI: Sharia-compliant fund
- **Personal Pension Plan (PPP) Guidelines (September 2025):** Re-designated from Micro Pension Plan; harmonizes voluntary contributions; mandatory CPS contributors can make additional voluntary contributions through PPP via same PFA.
- **Consumer Protection:** PenCom's Consumer Protection Department handles complaints and monitors contributor rights compliance.
- **Reference:** https://nairacompare.ng/blogs/the-complete-guide-to-compulsory-pensions | https://www.pensionnigeria.com/popular-topics/multifund-structure/

### 1.6 Money Laundering (Prevention and Prohibition) Act, 2022
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Republic of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2022-05-13
- **Relevance:** Establishes cash transaction limits that every professional must understand: no individual can make or accept cash payments exceeding N5,000,000 outside a financial institution. Breaking transactions into smaller amounts to circumvent the threshold is a criminal offense. Voluntary disclosure threshold: N1,000,000 for individuals. Essential for professionals handling large personal transactions (property purchases, vehicle sales, etc.).
- **Key Provisions for Individuals:** N5M individual cash limit; anti-structuring provisions; voluntary disclosure threshold of N1M; penalties for non-compliance.
- **Reference:** https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/money-laundering/1218416/money-laundering-prevention-and-prohibition-act-2022 | https://www.pwc.com/ng/en/publications/key-provisions-of-the-money-laundering-act.html

### 1.7 SEC Investor Protection & Education Framework
- **Issuing Body:** Securities and Exchange Commission Nigeria
- **Relevance:** SEC's mandate includes investor education to help Nigerians make informed capital market decisions. Operates Investor Protection Fund (IPF). Working on infusing capital market studies into secondary school and university curricula. Post-ISA 2025, SEC has greater enforcement powers against fraudulent schemes.
- **Key Programs:** Investor education portal; capital market studies curriculum for schools; approved SEC-licensed platforms list; investor complaint mechanism.
- **Reference:** https://sec.gov.ng/our-mandate/development/investor-education/investor-protection/

### 1.8 NGX Rules and Listing Requirements
- **Issuing Body:** Nigerian Exchange Limited
- **Relevance:** Sets admission criteria for listed companies and rules governing market participation by retail investors. The NGX Rule Book defines investor categories: retail investors, high-net-worth investors, and institutional investors. In December 2025, NGX introduced Commercial Paper listings, expanding product suite. The NGX Invest platform makes market access easier for retail participants.
- **Key for Learners:** Minimum investment amounts; how to access the market via broker-dealers; understanding of listing standards for evaluating company quality.
- **Reference:** https://ngxgroup.com/exchange/raise-capital/listing-requirements/ | https://ngxgroup.com/regulation/rules-regulations-and-notices/

## 4. Nigerian Case Studies

### 4.1 MMM Nigeria Collapse (2016)
- **Type:** Ponzi scheme catastrophe
- **Details:** Russian-origin "social financial network" promising 30% monthly returns. Attracted over 3 million Nigerian subscribers. Collectively lost approximately US$50 million (N18 billion) when it crashed December 2016. Gained massive traction during Nigeria's worst recession in decades as inflation surged and banks restricted credit.
- **Lesson for Course:** Module 3 -- recognizing unrealistic returns; understanding that returns above risk-free rate carry risk; emotional vulnerability during economic downturns.
- **Adaptive Tier:** Beginner (red flags), Intermediate (risk-return analysis), Advanced (systemic factors enabling fraud).

### 4.2 MBA Forex Fraud (2018-2021)
- **Type:** Fraudulent forex trading scheme
- **Details:** Homegrown scheme promising 15% returns. Defrauded Nigerians of approximately US$500 million (N213 billion) -- nearly 50% of total funds lost to Ponzi schemes in Nigeria over 10 years. Minimum investment US$1,000. CEO Maxwell Odum declared wanted by EFCC.
- **Lesson for Course:** Module 3 -- due diligence on investment platforms; importance of SEC registration; forex market risks.

### 4.3 CBEX Crypto Collapse (2024-2025)
- **Type:** AI-hyped crypto Ponzi scheme
- **Details:** Crypto Bridge Exchange defrauded over 250,000 Nigerians. Total losses estimated at US$840 million across Nigeria, Kenya, and Egypt. Promised 100% returns within 30 days via "AI-powered trading." Operated from July 2024, collapsed April 2025. Used TRON blockchain to mask fund flows. Linked to darknet marketplace Huione Guarantee. EFCC arrested suspects in Nigeria and Kenya.
- **Lesson for Course:** Module 3 -- crypto investment risks; AI marketing as a red flag; ISA 2025 requirements for SEC registration of digital asset platforms.

### 4.4 Naira Devaluation and Savings Erosion (2023-2025)
- **Type:** Macroeconomic case study
- **Details:** June 2023 CBN decision to float the naira caused 25% single-day drop. Naira fell from N450/$ to N1,600/$ between June 2023 and January 2024. Pension fund assets declined 56.8% in dollar terms (from USD 33.3 billion to USD 14.4 billion) within one year. Inflation climbed from 21.9% (January 2023) to 35% (November 2024). N70,000 minimum wage (July 2024) already "catastrophically inadequate" by 2026.
- **Lesson for Course:** Module 2 -- inflation-adjusted budgeting; Module 3 -- currency risk in savings strategies; importance of real return vs. nominal return thinking.
- **Adaptive Tier:** Beginner (understand inflation erodes savings), Intermediate (calculate real returns), Advanced (dollar-cost averaging and multi-currency allocation strategies).

### 4.5 NGX Five-Year Bull Run (2020-2024)
- **Type:** Investment success case study
- **Details:** NGX All-Share Index returned 283.45% from 2019 to 2024, closing 2024 at +37.65%. Individual stocks: Presco Plc surged 2,000%+; Transcorp Plc appreciated 1,000%; Vitafoam rose 1,900%. Retail investors outperformed institutional investors by 28% in first 9 months of 2024. Technology platforms enabled micro-investments from N8,000.
- **Lesson for Course:** Module 3 -- long-term equity returns in Nigeria; power of consistent investing; accessing the market through digital platforms.
- **Caveat:** Returns partly driven by naira depreciation (nominal vs. real returns distinction critical).

### 4.6 Digital Platform Investment Revolution
- **Type:** Access and inclusion case study
- **Details:** Platforms like Cowrywise, PiggyVest, Bamboo, Risevest, and Trove have democratized investment access. Retail NGX investments surged 88% month-on-month to N516.5 billion in July 2025. Bamboo and Trove allow micro-investments in US stocks from $1. Risevest offers managed dollar portfolios (14% stocks, 15% real estate, 10% fixed income historical returns). All regulated by SEC/CBN.
- **Lesson for Course:** Module 3 -- platform comparison; understanding fee structures; regulatory status verification.

### 4.7 Money Market Fund Outperformance
- **Type:** Low-risk investment case study
- **Details:** In 2025, Chapel Hill Denham Money Market Fund led with 24% gross yield. ARM Money Market Fund: 19.7% annual returns. Many funds offer N10,000 minimum investment with T+1 (24-hour) liquidity. Treasury Bills: 91-day yielding 17.98%, 364-day yielding 19.60% (minimum N50,000). Returns 4-5x better than savings accounts.
- **Lesson for Course:** Module 3 -- money market as first step beyond savings accounts; understanding yield, maturity, and liquidity trade-offs.

### 4.8 Consumer Credit Surge and Debt Risk
- **Type:** Household financial health case study
- **Details:** Consumer credit hit N4.42 trillion in November 2024 (+26.29% growth). Personal loans grew 37.76% to N3.32 trillion, accounting for 74.95% of total consumer credit. Occurred while Monetary Policy Rate rose from 18.75% to 27.50% (875 basis points increase in 2024 alone). Rising debt in a high-interest environment creates repayment sustainability concerns.
- **Lesson for Course:** Module 2 -- debt management; understanding interest rate environment; Module 1 -- reading loan terms and total cost of borrowing.

### 4.9 Pension Withdrawal for Unemployment
- **Type:** Safety net case study
- **Details:** 483,464 persons withdrew N247.47 billion from pension savings due to job losses. PRA 2014 allows 25% RSA withdrawal after 4 months of unemployment. Highlights tension between retirement savings adequacy and near-term financial needs.
- **Lesson for Course:** Module 4 -- understanding pension withdrawal rules; long-term impact of early withdrawals; emergency fund as alternative to pension raids.

## 5. Key Facts & Figures

- Less than 50% of Nigerian adults have fundamental grasp of essential financial principles (CBN data).
- Only 40% access banking services.
- **Source:** Credit Nigeria / CBN Financial Inclusion reports.

- Overall financial inclusion: 74% (up from 68% in 2020).
- Formal financial inclusion: 64% (up from 56% in 2020).
- Financially excluded: 26% (28.8 million adults).
- Gender gap: 9% (women 70%, men 79%).
- Youth (18-35): 71% included.
- Rural exclusion: 37% vs urban 17%.
- Regional: North West 47% excluded vs South West 5%.
- **Source:** https://a2f.ng/ | https://efina.org.ng/research/

## 6. Gaps Requiring SME Input

### 6.1 Real-World PAYE Calculation Walkthroughs
- **Gap:** No existing course provides step-by-step PAYE calculations under the NTA 2025 regime with realistic Nigerian salary scenarios (e.g., N250K/month entry-level professional vs. N1.2M/month senior manager). Need SME input from tax consultants or NRS practitioners to build interactive calculation exercises.
- **SME Profile:** Tax consultant or CITN (Chartered Institute of Taxation of Nigeria) member.

### 6.2 PFA Selection and Multi-Fund Optimization Strategy
- **Gap:** PenCom materials explain multi-fund structure mechanically but do not provide decision frameworks for choosing between Fund I and Fund II, or evaluating PFA performance. No existing content addresses how professionals should adjust fund allocation based on career stage, risk tolerance, and market conditions.
- **SME Profile:** Pension administrator or actuarial consultant at a top-5 PFA.

### 6.3 Naira-Economy Budgeting Models Under High Inflation
- **Gap:** The standard 50/30/20 rule does not account for Nigeria's reality where food inflation alone can consume 50%+ of income. Need validated alternative budgeting frameworks (e.g., the 70/20/10 model suggested by Nigerian personal finance sources) with worked examples at different salary levels (N70K minimum wage, N250K entry professional, N600K mid-career).
- **SME Profile:** Certified Financial Planner or Nigerian personal finance coach with track record.

### 6.4 Financial Statement Literacy for Non-Accountants
- **Gap:** The adaptive difficulty model requires teaching financial statements at three tiers: beginner (revenue minus expenses = profit/loss), intermediate (operating profit, gross margin), advanced (EBITDA, cash flow analysis). No existing Nigerian course structures this progression for non-accountant professionals. Need ICAN or ACCA member input on simplification without oversimplification.
- **SME Profile:** ICAN or ACCA member specializing in financial literacy training.

### 6.5 Practical Investment Platform Comparison and Due Diligence
- **Gap:** While many comparison articles exist (nairaCompare, Zikoko Money, TechCabal), none provide a structured due diligence checklist for evaluating investment platforms: SEC registration verification, fee transparency analysis, historical return verification, liquidity terms, and complaint resolution mechanisms. Need fintech practitioner input.
- **SME Profile:** SEC-licensed investment adviser or fintech compliance officer.

### 6.6 Cross-Border Remittance and Dollar-Denominated Savings
- **Gap:** Many Nigerian professionals receive diaspora remittances or seek dollar-denominated savings to hedge naira depreciation. No existing course systematically covers: legal ways to hold dollar assets (domiciliary accounts, dollar mutual funds, Bamboo/Risevest), CBN regulations on foreign currency holdings, tax implications of foreign investment income under NTA 2025.
- **SME Profile:** International tax specialist or CBN-licensed bureau de change operator.

### 6.7 Insurance Literacy and Adequate Coverage Assessment
- **Gap:** With insurance penetration at 0.3%, most Nigerian professionals lack even basic life insurance or health insurance coverage assessment skills. No existing course covers how to evaluate employer-provided group life insurance adequacy, voluntary life insurance options, or health insurance beyond NHIA. Critical gap given that only 2 million Nigerians have any insurance.
- **SME Profile:** Insurance broker or NAICOM-licensed underwriter.

## 7. Proposed Module Structure
