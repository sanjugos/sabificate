## 1. PRIMARY REGULATORY SOURCES

### 1.1 CBN Consumer Protection Framework 2016

- **Full Title:** Central Bank of Nigeria Consumer Protection Framework
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria, Consumer Protection Department (CPD)
- **Date Issued:** November 7, 2016
- **Official PDF:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/out/2016/cfpd/consumer%20protection%20framework%20(final).pdf

**Nine Core Principles:**
1. **Legal, Regulatory and Supervisory Framework** -- robust institutional structure
2. **Responsible Business Conduct** -- fair treatment throughout product lifecycle
3. **Disclosure and Transparency** -- clear, complete, accurate product information
4. **Fair Treatment and Business Conduct** -- non-discriminatory, ethical treatment
5. **Protection of Consumer Assets Against Fraud and Misuse** -- safeguarding deposits and data
6. **Protection of Consumer Data and Privacy** -- data handling standards
7. **Effective Complaints Handling and Redress** -- accessible, timely resolution
8. **Competition** -- fair competitive market environment
9. **Consumer Financial Education** -- literacy and awareness

**Customer Service Requirements:**
- Board-level consumer protection governance mandatory
- Designated Consumer Protection Compliance Officer required
- Consumer protection policy must be approved by the Board
- Regular consumer protection training for all staff
- Financial institutions shall conduct business in a responsible, professional, and ethical manner
- Institutions must not impose any product, service, or channel on consumers
- Accurate and updated consumer data required at all times
- Internal complaint management system with defined timelines
- Annual consumer protection compliance reports to CBN

**Scope:** All CBN-regulated financial institutions -- commercial banks, merchant banks, microfinance banks, primary mortgage banks, development finance institutions, payment service banks, bureau de change, finance companies, mobile money operators, payment service providers.

**Course Relevance:** Foundational document for all customer service standards in Nigerian financial services. Every lesson module should reference at least one of the nine principles.

### 1.2 CBN Consumer Protection Regulations 2019

- **Full Title:** Central Bank of Nigeria Consumer Protection Regulations
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Date Issued:** December 2019
- **Official PDF:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/out/2019/ccd/cbn%20consumer%20protection%20regulations.pdf
- **Also at:** https://sterling.ng/wp-content/uploads/2020/02/CBN-Consumer-Protection-Regulations.pdf

**Complaints Handling Requirements (Part 6):**
- Acknowledge customer complaint within 24 hours (some sources say 3 days for certain categories)
- Resolve simple complaints within 72 hours
- Maximum 2-week (14 working days) resolution for complex complaints
- Maintain complaint management channels: letters, emails, telephone lines, social media, digital platforms
- Internal Dispute Resolution (IDR) structures and processes mandatory
- Penalty: N500,000 per complaint per week for non-resolution within prescribed timelines
- Penalty: N2 million for failure to acknowledge complaints

**Disclosure Requirements:**
- All fees, charges, and interest rates must be disclosed before product/service activation
- Total Cost of Credit (TCC) must be provided in writing
- Changes in terms must be communicated with 30 days advance notice
- Product terms must be in plain language

**Fair Treatment:**
- Prohibition on unfair, deceptive, or abusive practices
- Prohibition on tying and bundling of products without consent
- Right to switch financial institutions and products
- Right to terminate contracts with appropriate notice
- Prohibition on discrimination in service delivery

**Course Relevance:** The specific timelines and penalties here form the backbone of complaint-handling modules. Frontline staff must know the 24-hour/72-hour/2-week escalation ladder.

### 1.5 CBN 48-Hour Refund Guidelines for Failed ATM Transactions (Draft, 2025)

- **Full Title:** Draft Guidelines on the Operations of Automated Teller Machines in Nigeria
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Date:** October 2025 (exposure draft)
- **Source:** https://nairametrics.com/2025/10/11/cbn-releases-new-guidelines-for-instant-refund-for-failed-atm-transactions/

**Key Provisions:**
- Failed "on-us" transactions (customer's own bank's ATM): instant reversal
- Technical failure preventing instant reversal: manual refund within 24 hours
- All other failed ATM transactions ("not-on-us"): refund within 48 hours
- Quarterly reporting of ATM uptime, failed transactions, and refund compliance
- FCCPC publicly endorsed the guidelines

**Course Relevance:** ATM/failed transaction complaints are the #1 complaint category (51.50% of all complaints are electronic/card-related). Staff must know these timelines cold.

### 2.1 SERVQUAL Model -- Banking Service Quality Framework

- **Original Authors:** Parasuraman, Zeithaml, and Berry (1988)
- **Application:** Extensively applied in Nigerian banking research

**Five Service Quality Dimensions:**
1. **Tangibles** -- appearance of physical facilities, equipment, personnel, communication materials
2. **Reliability** -- ability to perform promised service dependably and accurately
3. **Responsiveness** -- willingness to help customers and provide prompt service
4. **Assurance** -- knowledge and courtesy of employees and their ability to inspire trust
5. **Empathy** -- caring, individualized attention the firm provides to customers

**Nigerian Banking Research:**
- "The Influence of Service Quality Dimensions on Customer Satisfaction in the Nigerian Banking Industry" -- ResearchGate, 2022
- "Enhancing Service Quality Dimensions as Means of Sustaining Customer Satisfaction in Nigerian Banking Industry" -- ResearchGate, 2023
- "Service Quality in the Nigerian Banking Industry" -- GRIN, various years
- "Service Quality Delivery and Customer Preference for Agency Banking Services in Nigeria" -- ResearchGate, 2024
- Source: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/361693426

**Key Finding:** Service quality factors (service attractiveness, customer friendliness, bank automation effectiveness, safe transactions) have significant effects on customer satisfaction in Nigerian banks.

**Course Relevance:** SERVQUAL provides the theoretical backbone for the course. All five dimensions should be explicitly taught and applied to Nigerian financial services scenarios.

### 2.2 KPMG Six Pillars of Customer Experience

- **Source:** KPMG International, applied annually in West Africa Banking CX Survey
- **Latest Report:** 2025 KPMG West Africa Banking Industry Customer Experience Survey Report
- **URL:** https://kpmg.com/ng/en/home/insights/2025/12/2025-kpmg-west-africa-banking-industry-cx-survey-report.html
- **Full PDF:** https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/ng/pdf/2025/12/2025%20KPMG%20West%20Africa%20Banking%20Industry%20CX%20Survey%20Report.pdf

**Six Pillars:**
1. **Integrity** -- being trustworthy and engendering trust (highest-rated pillar in Nigeria, 2024 and 2025)
2. **Resolution** -- turning a poor experience into a great one (weakest pillar for 4 consecutive years)
3. **Expectations** -- managing, meeting, and exceeding expectations
4. **Time and Effort** -- minimizing customer effort and creating frictionless processes
5. **Personalisation** -- using individualized attention to drive emotional connection
6. **Empathy** -- achieving an understanding of the customer's circumstances

**2025 Nigeria Survey Data:**
- 35,000+ retail customers, 5,000 SMEs, 600 corporates surveyed
- Integrity: highest-rated pillar in Nigeria
- Resolution: weakest pillar for 4th consecutive year
- Non-savers decreased from 18% to 8% (2024 to 2025)
- Corporate segment surpassed 80-point mark in CX ratings
- SME segment: CX score increased by 2+ percentage points YoY
- Retail segment: only marginal improvement
- Fintech leaders (PalmPay, OPay, Moniepoint) outperforming traditional banks in retail CX
- Security of account information: most critical satisfaction measure for 2nd consecutive year
- Source: https://punchng.com/nigerian-banks-struggle-to-resolve-customers-complaints-kpmg/

**Course Relevance:** The KPMG Six Pillars framework should be taught alongside SERVQUAL. The persistent weakness in "Resolution" is the central problem this course aims to address.

### 4.1 CBN Consumer Protection Department -- Complaint Resolution at Scale

**Oct 2023 - Sep 2024:**
- 19,988 customer complaints addressed within eight months
- 15,306 complaints (76.58%) successfully resolved
- Refunds facilitated: approximately N7.05 billion and $714,569.03
- Source: https://www.legit.ng/business-economy/industry/1634233-cbn-refunds-n705-billion-714000-bank-customers-bank-glitches-continue/

- 10,704 complaints received (143.38% increase from 4,398 in H1 2024)
- 9,771 complaints resolved (91.3% closure rate)

### 4.4 Fintech Disruption -- OPay, Moniepoint, PalmPay

- Moniepoint: 16+ million banked customers, 6+ million active businesses
- PalmPay: Significant user base (specific numbers not publicly reported at time of research)

- Younger bank customers migrating to fintechs due to poor customer service at traditional banks
- Fintechs offered faster, cheaper alternatives

**Fintech Customer Service Challenges:**
- Documented response pattern across OPay, PalmPay, Kuda, Moniepoint: delay, generic replies, requests for documentation rarely producing outcomes within reasonable timeframes

- Some users report accounts blocked after large transactions without clear explanation
- Social media has become the primary "banking hall" for fintech customer complaints
- Sources: https://technext24.com/2026/06/09/fintech-customer-complaints-social-media/ and https://theeconomictimes.com.ng/2024/10/17/nigerians-turn-to-opay-palmpay-moniepoint-amid-banks-tech-disruptions/

## 6. GAPS REQUIRING SME INPUT

### 6.1 Nigeria-Specific NPS Benchmarks

**Gap:** No publicly available NPS scores for individual Nigerian banks or industry-wide Nigerian banking NPS benchmarks were found. The KPMG CX Survey uses its own Six Pillars framework rather than NPS.

**SME Input Needed:** A financial services consultant or banking industry association representative who can provide internal NPS data or knows which Nigerian banks have implemented NPS programs and what scores they achieve.

### 6.2 Detailed Internal Complaint Management Workflows

**Gap:** While regulatory timelines are well-documented (24-hour acknowledgment, 72-hour resolution, 2-week complex), the actual internal workflows banks use to manage complaints (escalation matrices, team structures, technology platforms, staffing models) are not publicly available.

**SME Input Needed:** A senior customer service or operations manager from a Tier 1 Nigerian bank who can share anonymized process flows and organizational structures for complaint management departments.

### 6.3 Agent Banking Customer Service Training Materials

**Gap:** The CIBN ABC certification covers agent banking, and CBN 2025 guidelines mandate agent training, but no publicly available training curriculum or materials for agent-specific customer service were found.

**SME Input Needed:** A SANEF representative or super-agent network operator (e.g., Moniepoint, FirstMonie, FCMB Agents) who can share or describe agent training content, particularly for customer interaction scenarios at POS points.

### 6.4 Branch Experience Optimization Best Practices (Nigerian Context)

**Gap:** While customer journey mapping frameworks are well-documented internationally, Nigeria-specific branch experience optimization case studies (e.g., queue management systems, branch layout redesigns, self-service kiosk implementations, accessibility features for elderly/disabled customers) were not found in public sources.

**SME Input Needed:** A branch operations head or transformation officer from a bank that has recently renovated or redesigned its branch experience (Access Bank, GTBank, Zenith Bank have all invested in branch modernization).

### 6.5 PFA Customer Service Standards and Metrics

**Gap:** PenCom regulates pension fund administrators but detailed customer service performance metrics, complaint categories specific to PFAs, and service level benchmarks are not publicly available.

**SME Input Needed:** A PenCom official or PFA operations head who can provide data on complaint volumes, resolution rates, common complaint types (delayed access to RSA, inaccurate balance statements, transfer delays between PFAs, etc.).

### 6.6 Offline-First Digital Training Delivery for Branch Staff

**Gap:** While the course is designed for offline-first delivery, no documented examples of Nigerian financial institutions deploying offline-capable mobile training for branch or agent staff were found.

**SME Input Needed:** A learning & development professional from a Nigerian bank (or FITC) who has experience deploying mobile or tablet-based training in low-connectivity environments. Alternatively, a Moniepoint or OPay field training coordinator who can describe how they train agents in remote areas.

### 6.7 Customer Service Metrics in Nigerian Microfinance Banks

**Gap:** Microfinance banks serve a large segment of the underbanked population but their customer service standards, complaint data, and regulatory compliance performance are largely absent from public research.

**SME Input Needed:** An MFB association representative or regulator who can provide customer service data for the MFB segment, which differs significantly from commercial banking in service delivery models.

### 6.8 Cultural and Linguistic Factors in Nigerian Financial Service Delivery

**Gap:** Nigeria's multi-ethnic, multilingual context creates unique customer service challenges (language barriers, cultural expectations around formality/informality, gender dynamics in financial services, Islamic finance customer service norms in Northern Nigeria). No structured research on these factors in financial service contexts was found.

**SME Input Needed:** A sociolinguist or anthropologist with financial services experience, or a senior customer service trainer who has worked across multiple Nigerian regions and can describe how service delivery must be adapted for different cultural contexts.

## 7. PROPOSED MODULE STRUCTURE

### Module 1: Foundations of Customer Service Excellence in Nigerian Financial Services

**Learning Objective:** Equip learners with a thorough understanding of regulatory standards, service quality frameworks, and the Nigerian customer service landscape.

**Lesson 1: The Nigerian Financial Customer -- Context and Expectations**
- Financial inclusion landscape: 74% included, 26% excluded (28.8 million adults)
- Geographic and demographic disparities (North vs. South, urban vs. rural, digital literacy gaps)
- Multi-channel reality: branches (4,477), agents (1.92 million), USSD, mobile apps, internet banking
- The customer's regulatory rights: CBN Consumer Protection Framework 9 principles
- Dual regulatory oversight: CBN + FCCPC
- **Assessment:** Scenario quiz -- identify which regulatory principle applies to given customer situations

**Lesson 2: Regulatory Compliance for Customer-Facing Staff**
- CBN Consumer Protection Regulations 2019 -- key timelines staff must know
- The complaint escalation ladder: 24 hours (acknowledge) -> 72 hours (simple) -> 2 weeks (complex) -> CBN escalation
- Penalties for non-compliance: N500,000/complaint/week; N2M for failure to acknowledge
- FCCPC parallel authority and what it means for staff
- Sector-specific: NAICOM 60-day claim settlement; PenCom contributor rights
- Agent banking requirements: CBN 2025 Guidelines obligations
- **Assessment:** Timed drill -- given a complaint scenario, learner selects correct timeline and action

**Lesson 3: Service Quality Frameworks for Nigerian Financial Services**
- SERVQUAL five dimensions applied to Nigerian banking scenarios
- KPMG Six Pillars of CX -- Nigeria's scores and what they mean
- Resolution: why it has been the weakest pillar for 4 consecutive years and what staff can do
- Integrity as Nigeria's highest-rated pillar -- maintaining trust in every interaction
- NPS methodology and global benchmarks (41 average, 70+ world-class)
- **Assessment:** Self-assessment against SERVQUAL dimensions with personal development plan

**Lesson 4: The Cost of Poor Service -- Nigerian Case Studies**
- CBN complaint surge: 143% increase in H1 2025 (10,704 complaints)
- Penalty case studies: GTB N1.6B fine from mystery shopping, Zenith N15.4B, Access N1.21B
- Refund costs: N7.17B refunded in H1 2025 alone
- Fintech disruption: why customers switched to OPay, Moniepoint, PalmPay
- Insurance trust deficit: 0.3-0.5% penetration, 1,571 policyholder complaints
- FCCPC service disruption warnings to banks
- **Assessment:** Case analysis -- identify root causes and prevention strategies for 3 real complaint scenarios

### Module 2: Delivering Service Excellence Across Channels

**Learning Objective:** Develop practical skills for complaint resolution, customer journey management, and channel-specific service delivery.

**Lesson 5: Complaint Handling and Service Recovery**
- The complaint management lifecycle: receipt -> acknowledgment -> investigation -> resolution -> follow-up
- CBN-mandated timelines and internal workflow design
- Service recovery paradox: turning complaints into loyalty (addressing Nigeria's weakest CX pillar)
- De-escalation techniques for frustrated customers
- Documentation requirements and audit trails
- Common complaint categories: electronic/cards (51.50%), fraud (39.27%), account management/charges (9.23%)
- ATM failed transaction refund process: instant (on-us) -> 24 hours (technical failure) -> 48 hours (not-on-us)
- **Assessment:** Role-play simulation -- resolve a multi-stage complaint within regulatory timelines

**Lesson 6: Branch and In-Person Service Excellence**
- Customer journey mapping: awareness -> onboarding -> regular use -> complaint -> resolution -> advocacy
- Queue management and wait time optimization
- Face-to-face communication: empathy, active listening, clear explanation
- Handling difficult customers: elderly/low-literacy/disabled/frustrated
- Branch accessibility and inclusivity
- Cross-selling ethically: CBN Responsible Business Conduct guidelines
- Mystery shopping preparedness: what evaluators assess (emotional and technical attributes)
- **Assessment:** Branch service scenario -- learner guides a customer through account opening while meeting all disclosure requirements

**Lesson 7: Digital Channel and Agent Banking Service Delivery**
- USSD banking service: current N6.98/session charge, common user issues
- Mobile app support: guiding customers through transactions
- Agent banking service standards: CBN 2025 requirements for agents
- KYC at agent points: BVN, NIN, transaction limits
- Fraud prevention at POS: recognizing social engineering, phishing, SIM swap attempts
- Managing service during system outages: customer communication scripts
- Remote/offline service delivery challenges in underserved areas
- **Assessment:** Multi-channel scenario -- same customer issue handled across branch, USSD, and agent touchpoints

**Lesson 8: Building a Customer-Centric Culture**
- Frontline staff empowerment: autonomy within guidelines
- Knowledge sharing among staff (research shows direct link to service quality)
- Customer feedback loops: collecting, analyzing, acting on feedback
- Internal service culture: how back-office affects frontline
- Continuous improvement: using complaint data for process improvement
- Professional development pathways: CIBN, ICSN, CICRM certifications
- Personal service commitment: individual action plan
- **Assessment:** Capstone -- learner designs a 30-day customer service improvement plan for their unit/branch/agent point, incorporating regulatory compliance, complaint handling improvements, and one measurable CX metric
