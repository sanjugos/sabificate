## 1. Primary Regulatory Sources

### 1.1 ICAN Act 1965 (Cap. 185, Laws of the Federation)
- **Full title:** Institute of Chartered Accountants of Nigeria Act, 1965 (Act of Parliament No. 15)
- **Effective date:** 1 September 1965
- **Scope:** Establishes ICAN as the sole (later primary) regulatory body for professional accountancy in Nigeria; defines membership categories, examination authority, disciplinary powers, and practising certificate requirements
- **Key provisions:** Only ICAN members with a practising certificate may serve as auditors of limited liability companies; establishes the Accountants' Investigating Panel (AIP) and Accountants' Disciplinary Tribunal (ADT)
- **Source:** [ICAN Act PDF](https://icanig.org/documents/ICAN_Act.pdf)

### 1.2 ICAN MCPE / MPD Policy (Revised January 2024)
- **Original introduction:** ICAN introduced CPE in 1986; made compulsory (MCPE) for all members in 1996 -- the first professional body in Africa to do so
- **Current requirement (effective 1 January 2024):** 120 credit hours over 3 consecutive years (increased from 90 hours)
- **Annual minimum:** 40 credit hours per year
- **Eligible activities:** Both structured and unstructured learning activities
  - **Structured:** Faculty training sessions, certification programmes, conferences, webinars, ICAN e-learning courses
  - **Unstructured:** Self-study, reading professional journals, mentoring, on-the-job learning
- **Compliance:** MPD is compulsory for every qualified member whose membership is current
- **IES 7 alignment:** Fully aligned with International Education Standard 7 as of 2024
- **Source:** [IFAC - ICAN Member Profile](https://www.ifac.org/about-ifac/membership/members/institute-chartered-accountants-nigeria); [ICAN MPD Page](https://icanig.org/ican/mpd/)

### 1.3 Financial Reporting Council of Nigeria (FRCN) Act 2011 & Amendment Act 2023
- **FRCN Act 2011:** Established the FRC to promote compliance with IFRS, IPSAS, and international auditing standards; shared quality assurance responsibilities with ICAN
- **Amendment Act 2023 key changes:**
  - Broadened definition of "public interest entity" to include entities with annual turnover exceeding N30 billion
  - Directorate of Inspection and Monitoring given express authority over corporate governance compliance
  - Established centralised digital database for financial statement filings by public interest entities
  - Annual registration renewal now mandatory; minimum annual dues increased from N5,000 to N10,000
  - Non-registration fine increased to N5,000,000
  - Public interest entities failing to submit corrected financial reports within 60 days face fines of N25 million or more
- **Source:** [DLA Piper Africa - FRCN Amendment Act 2023](https://www.dlapiperafrica.com/en/nigeria/insights/2023/the-financial-reporting-council-of-nigeria-amendment-act-2023-transforming-governance-and-accountability-standards.html)

### 1.4 IFRS Adoption in Nigeria
- **Approval:** Federal Executive Council approved IFRS adoption on 28 July 2010, effective 1 January 2012
- **Applicability:** Required for all "public interest entities" -- quoted companies, unquoted companies, government organisations, and not-for-profit entities required by law to file returns
- **SMEs:** Required to apply IFRS for SMEs
- **Automatic adoption:** Any new standard published by the IASB is automatically applicable in Nigeria
- **Source:** [IFRS Foundation - Nigeria Jurisdiction](https://www.ifrs.org/use-around-the-world/use-of-ifrs-standards-by-jurisdiction/view-jurisdiction/nigeria/)

### 1.5 Nigerian Standards on Auditing (NSA) / International Standards on Auditing
- Auditing practice regulated by the FRCN; international ISA adopted as Nigerian Standards on Auditing (NSA)
- **ISA for LCE:** FRCN issued public notice in July 2025 adopting International Standards on Auditing for Less Complex Entities (effective for periods beginning on or after 15 December 2025)
- **Pending changes:** ISA 240 (Revised) on fraud and ISA 570 (Revised 2024) on going concern take effect for periods beginning on or after 15 December 2026
- **Source:** [FRC Nigeria - ISA for LCE Notice](https://frcnigeria.gov.ng/2025/07/28/public-notice-adoption-and-implementation-of-international-standards-on-auditing-for-less-complex-entities-isa-for-lce/)

### 1.6 Companies and Allied Matters Act 2020 (CAMA 2020)
- **Mandatory statutory audit** for most entities, with exemptions for single-shareholder and small companies (turnover < N120 million, net assets < N60 million)
- **Audit committee composition:** 5 members for public companies (3 shareholders + 2 non-executive directors), at least one member being a member of a professional accounting body in Nigeria
- **Public companies** must display audited accounts on their websites
- **Source:** [CAMA 2020 Key Highlights](https://www.gelias.com/images/Newsletter/CAMA_2020_Key_Highlights.pdf)

### 1.7 Nigerian Code of Corporate Governance (NCCG 2018)
- Issued by the FRC; unveiled 15 January 2019
- **Applies to:** All public companies (listed or not), private holding companies of public companies, concessioned/privatised companies, and regulated private companies
- **Basis:** "Apply and Explain" principle
- **Non-compliance:** Can result in fines and sanctions from the FRC
- **Source:** [KPMG - NCCG 2018 Analysis](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/ng/pdf/advisory/2018-Nigerian-Code-of-Corporate-Governance.pdf)

### 1.8 Nigeria Tax Reform Acts 2025
- **Signed into law:** 26 June 2025 by President Bola Ahmed Tinubu
- **Four acts:** Nigeria Tax Act (NTA), Nigeria Tax Administration Act (NTAA), Nigeria Revenue Service Act (NRSA), Joint Revenue Board Act (JRBA)
- **Effective date:** Core provisions from 1 January 2026
- **Consolidation:** Over 60 disparate taxes consolidated into fewer than 10 statutes; repeals and consolidates Companies Income Tax Act, Personal Income Tax Act, Petroleum Profits Tax Act, VAT Act, Capital Gains Tax Act, and Stamp Duties Act
- **VAT:** Rate remains at 7.5%; food and basic consumables zero-rated; healthcare, education, and passenger road transport exempt; mandatory fiscalisation system for VAT collection
- **PIT exemption:** Individuals earning N800,000 or less annually exempt from personal income tax
- **Minimum ETR:** 15% for multinationals, companies with N20 billion+ annual turnover, and non-resident companies with Nigerian parents
- **Unified development levy:** 4% on assessable profits (replacing multiple previous levies)
- **Nigeria Revenue Service** replaces FIRS
- **Tax Ombud Office** created for taxpayer dispute resolution
- **Source:** [EY - Nigeria Tax Act 2025 Highlights](https://www.ey.com/en_gl/technical/tax-alerts/nigeria-tax-act-2025-has-been-signed-highlights); [Chambers - 10 Major Shifts](https://chambers.com/articles/unpacking-the-10-major-shifts-in-nigeria-s-new-tax-acts-2)

### 1.9 ICAN Code of Ethics
- Based on IESBA International Code of Ethics for Professional Accountants
- Enforced through ICAN's disciplinary framework: Accountants' Investigating Panel (AIP) and Accountants' Disciplinary Tribunal (ADT)
- Separate disciplinary mechanisms for students (Students Investigating Committee) and accounting technicians (AAT Investigating Committee)
- Annual training provided to AIP, ADT, and legal team members
- **Source:** [IFAC - ICAN Member Profile](https://www.ifac.org/about-ifac/membership/members/institute-chartered-accountants-nigeria)

### 1.10 Money Laundering (Prevention and Prohibition) Act 2022
- Accountants designated as Non-Financial Institutions (NFIs) / DNFBPs
- Must register with SCUML (Special Control Unit Against Money Laundering)
- Required to maintain AML programme with: compliance officer, internal audit function, staff training, written compliance manual, documented due-diligence procedures, suspicious transaction reporting to NFIU
- **Penalties:** Minimum fine of N10 million, imprisonment of at least 3 years, or both for non-compliance
- **Source:** [AML Compliance Guide - Nigeria](https://www.sanctions.io/blog/anti-money-laundering-aml-in-nigeria-a-2025-guide)

### 1.11 IFRS Sustainability Disclosure Standards Roadmap (FRCN 2024)
- FRCN issued roadmap for adoption of IFRS S1, S2, and future ISSB Standards in March 2024
- **Timeline:** Voluntary reporting until 2026; mandatory phased approach beginning 2027 for public interest entities; full mandatory reporting from 1 January 2028; SMEs by fiscal year 2030
- FRCN published consultation on amendments to the roadmap in late 2025
- **Source:** [FRCN Roadmap Report](https://frcnigeria.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2024/07/FINAL-COPY-OF-SUSTAINABILITY-ROADMAP1.pdf)

### 1.12 IPSAS Adoption for Public Sector
- Federal Executive Council mandated IPSAS adoption by all three tiers of government by 1 January 2016
- Implementation faces ongoing challenges: political will, statutory adjustment, inadequate funding, poor IT infrastructure, change management issues
- **Source:** [EJAAFR - IPSAS Adoption Challenges in Nigeria](https://eajournals.org/ejaafr/vol12-issue-1-2024/international-public-sector-accounting-standards-adoption-and-challenges-of-implementation-in-nigeria/)

## 4. Nigerian Case Studies

### 4.1 Cadbury Nigeria PLC Financial Misstatement (2006)
- **What happened:** SEC discovered financial misstatements in Cadbury Nigeria annual reports for 2003-2005 totalling approximately N13-15 billion (US$85 million)
- **Nature of fraud:** Overstatement of profits, misrepresentation of sales figures, false supplier certificates
- **Key actors:** Former CEO Bunmi Oni and former Finance Director Ayo Akadiri deliberately overstated the company's financial position
- **Auditor implicated:** Akintola Williams Deloitte (now Deloitte Nigeria) was implicated in falsifying accounts
- **Outcome:** CEO and Finance Director sacked; shareholder lawsuits; significant impact on corporate governance reform in Nigeria
- **MCPE relevance:** Ethics, auditor independence, professional scepticism, corporate governance failures
- **Source:** [ResearchGate - Cadbury Nigeria Case](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/228273223_Creative_Accounting_Corporate_Governance_Watch_Dogs_Institutions_and_Systems-_the_Case_of_Cadbury_Nig_Plc)

### 4.2 Nigerian Banking Crisis (2009) -- Accounting Failures
- **What happened:** CBN injected N420 billion (US$2.8 billion) into 5 failed banks: Afribank, Finbank, Intercontinental Bank, Oceanic Bank, and Union Bank
- **Key issues:** Reckless lending without adequate collateral, share price manipulation, money laundering, fraudulent conversion of depositors' funds
- **Notable case:** Cecilia Ibru (Oceanic Bank MD) convicted of fraud; ordered to forfeit US$1.2 billion in cash and assets; moved GBP 1.7 billion to the UK in one transaction
- **Afribank:** Granted N91 billion in loans without adequate collateral
- **Outcome:** All 5 MDs sacked on 14 August 2009; criminal prosecutions followed
- **MCPE relevance:** Audit quality, financial statement fraud detection, regulatory compliance, ethics
- **Source:** [Brunel University Research](https://bura.brunel.ac.uk/bitstream/2438/18733/1/FullText.pdf)

### 4.3 ICAN Exam Impersonation Scandal (2025)
- **What happened:** Viral video circulated alleging impersonation during ICAN professional examinations
- **ICAN response:** Launched investigation; reaffirmed zero-tolerance policy for examination malpractice; committed to transparent communication of findings
- **MCPE relevance:** Professional ethics, institutional integrity, examination standards
- **Source:** [Legit.ng - ICAN Exam Scandal](https://www.legit.ng/education/1675288-ican-reaffirms-tolerance-policy-examination-malpractice-viral-impersonation-allegations/)

### 4.4 HEDA v. Chukwunyere FCA Money Laundering Case (2026)
- **What happened:** HEDA urged ICAN to sanction Mr Nwabuoke Anamekwe Chukwunyere, an FCA, after his conviction for fraud and money laundering
- **Details:** Federal High Court in Abuja found him guilty on 9 counts of money laundering and diversion of public funds totalling N868 million; sentenced to 72 years' imprisonment
- **Issue:** Tests ICAN's disciplinary mechanism and public accountability
- **MCPE relevance:** AML obligations, professional conduct, consequences of ethical violations
- **Source:** [The Guardian Nigeria](https://guardian.ng/news/heda-urges-ican-to-sanction-convicted-ex-govt-official-over-n868m-fraud/)

### 4.5 IFRS Adoption Challenges for Nigerian SMEs
- Many Nigerian SMEs unaware of IFRS benefits; excessive disclosure requirements burden small businesses
- Poor financial management and limited access to finance compound adoption challenges
- SMEs in Lagos case studies show varied implementation experiences
- Government, regulatory agencies, and professional bodies urged to fast-track IFRS education
- **MCPE relevance:** IFRS for SMEs application, practical implementation skills
- **Source:** [Academia.edu - IFRS Adoption Challenges](https://www.academia.edu/8052302/CHALLENGES_OF_IFRS_ADOPTION_AMONGST_SMES_IN_NIGERIA)

### 4.6 IPSAS Implementation Challenges in Nigerian Public Sector
- Despite 2016 deadline, full implementation remains elusive across federal, state, and local government levels
- Key barriers: political will, statutory adjustment, inadequate funding, poor IT infrastructure, change management
- Local government level shows weakest adoption; persistent financial mismanagement undermines expected benefits
- **MCPE relevance:** Public sector accounting, IPSAS practical application, reform advocacy
- **Source:** [EJAAFR 2024 Research](https://eajournals.org/ejaafr/vol12-issue-1-2024/international-public-sector-accounting-standards-adoption-and-challenges-of-implementation-in-nigeria/)

### 4.7 ICAN-ANAN Regulatory Rivalry
- ICAN opposed ANAN's establishment; filed suit in 2007 to have ANAN's enabling decree declared void (dismissed by Federal High Court)
- ANAN has rejected proposed ICAN Act amendments, warning of professional rivalry
- Rivalry creates confusion in standard-setting and certification overlap
- **MCPE relevance:** Professional regulation, institutional governance, collaborative frameworks
- **Source:** [Leadership.ng - ANAN Rejects ICAN Act Amendment](https://leadership.ng/anan-rejects-proposed-ican-act-amendment-warns-of-rivalry/)

### 4.8 Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) 2021 -- Accounting Implications
- Major overhaul of oil and gas sector governance and fiscal framework
- Changes to tax treatment, decommissioning fund deductibility, royalty payments
- Significant accounting and reporting implications for companies in Nigeria's largest revenue sector
- ICAN e-learning platform includes dedicated PIA course
- **MCPE relevance:** Sector-specific accounting standards, tax implications

## 6. Professional Body Requirements

### 6.1 ICAN Certification Levels

#### Associate Chartered Accountant (ACA)
- **Eligibility:** ATSWA certificate or university degree or equivalent
- **Examination:** Pass all 15 subjects across Foundation, Skills, and Professional levels
- **Practical experience:** 36 months for accounting graduates; 48 months for non-accounting graduates
- **Registration fee:** N11,400 (including bank charges)
- **Induction fee:** N150,000
- **Designation:** May use "ACA" after name
- **Practising certificate:** Separate application required to set up public practice

#### Fellow Chartered Accountant (FCA)
- **Eligibility:** ACA holders with minimum 10 years post-induction experience
- **Additional requirements:**
  - Continuous practice (for practitioners) or continuous professional activity (for non-practitioners)
  - Must be financial member of the Institute and a District Society
  - For practitioners: must be in continuous practice, using the Licence, on their own or in partnership
- **Application process:** Submit through ICAN portal with supporting documentation
- **Designation:** Highest status attainable in ICAN; may use "FCA" after name

### 6.2 MCPE Credit Structure (Post-January 2024)
- **3-year cycle:** 120 credit hours total
- **Annual minimum:** 40 credit hours
- **Categories:** Structured and unstructured learning activities
- **Structured activities:** Faculty training, certifications, conferences, webinars, e-learning courses
- **Unstructured activities:** Self-study, reading, mentoring, on-the-job learning
- **Reporting:** Through ICAN member portal (icanportal.org/members/mcpd)
- **Compliance:** Mandatory for all qualified members with current membership

### 6.3 Annual Membership Renewal
- **Annual subscription + licence (2025):** N17,500
- **District society dues:** Varies (~N5,000/year)
- **FRCN registration:** N10,000/year minimum (mandatory for practitioners)
- **Payment deadline:** 60 days from 1 January each year

### 6.4 Practising Certificate Requirements
- Only ICAN members with a practising certificate may serve as auditors of limited liability companies
- Separate licence application to ICAN Council required
- Must maintain current MCPE compliance
- Subject to ICAN/FRCN practice reviews (in place since 2009)

### 6.5 2026 Amnesty Programme
- First and only amnesty for documentation of audit training
- Members can complete and submit evidence of compulsory 36-month audit training
- Training documentation fee: N20,000

## 8. Proposed Module Structure

### Module 1: ICAN Regulatory Framework & Professional Standards (4 Lessons)
**Objective:** Ground learners in the legal and institutional foundations of the Nigerian accounting profession

| # | Lesson Title | Key Topics | Credit Hours |
|---|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| 1.1 | The ICAN Ecosystem | ICAN Act 1965, membership structure (ACA/FCA), ICAN faculties, ICAN-ANAN landscape, IFAC affiliation | 2 |
| 1.2 | MCPE Requirements & Compliance | 120-hour requirement, structured vs. unstructured credits, annual minimums, reporting through member portal, compliance consequences | 2 |
| 1.3 | FRCN Regulatory Framework | FRCN Act 2011 & 2023 Amendment, quality assurance, practice reviews, public interest entity requirements, digital financial repository | 2 |
| 1.4 | Professional Ethics & Code of Conduct | IESBA Code, ICAN Code of Ethics, fundamental principles, independence, confidentiality, AIP/ADT disciplinary framework, real-world Nigerian case studies | 2 |

### Module 2: Financial Reporting & IFRS Updates (4 Lessons)
**Objective:** Ensure practitioners maintain current IFRS knowledge and can apply standards in Nigerian context

| # | Lesson Title | Key Topics | Credit Hours |
|---|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| 2.1 | IFRS in Nigeria: Adoption & Application | IFRS adoption history, FRCN role, automatic adoption of new standards, IFRS for SMEs, practical challenges | 2 |
| 2.2 | Key IFRS Standards Deep Dive | IFRS 9 (Financial Instruments), IFRS 15 (Revenue), IFRS 16 (Leases) post-implementation issues; IFRS 17 (Insurance); fair value measurement | 3 |
| 2.3 | Public Sector Accounting (IPSAS) | IPSAS adoption in Nigeria, accrual vs. cash basis, implementation challenges, government financial reporting | 2 |
| 2.4 | Sustainability & ESG Reporting | ISSB standards (IFRS S1, S2), FRCN adoption roadmap, 2027-2030 timeline, Nigerian implementation considerations | 2 |

### Module 3: Taxation & Fiscal Policy (4 Lessons)
**Objective:** Equip practitioners with knowledge of Nigeria's transformed tax landscape

| # | Lesson Title | Key Topics | Credit Hours |
|---|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| 3.1 | Nigeria Tax Reform Acts 2025: Overview | NTA, NTAA, NRSA, JRBA; consolidation of 60+ taxes; Nigeria Revenue Service; Tax Ombud Office | 2 |
| 3.2 | Corporate & Personal Income Tax Changes | PIT exemption (N800,000 threshold), minimum ETR (15%), 4% unified development levy, small company redefinition, bad debt restrictions | 3 |
| 3.3 | VAT, Customs & Indirect Taxes | VAT at 7.5%, zero-rating changes, exempt items, mandatory fiscalisation, input VAT claims expansion, digital service provider obligations | 2 |
| 3.4 | Sector-Specific Tax Issues | Petroleum Industry Act implications, mining sector reforms, digital economy taxation, transfer pricing, startups and employment tax incentives | 2 |

### Module 4: Auditing, Governance & Compliance (4 Lessons)
**Objective:** Update practitioners on evolving audit standards, corporate governance, and compliance obligations

| # | Lesson Title | Key Topics | Credit Hours |
|---|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| 4.1 | Auditing Standards Update | ISA 240 (Revised - Fraud), ISA 570 (Revised - Going Concern), ISA for LCE adoption in Nigeria, Nigerian Standards on Auditing | 2 |
| 4.2 | Corporate Governance & CAMA 2020 | NCCG 2018, CAMA 2020 audit requirements, audit committee composition, audit exemptions for small companies, website disclosure requirements | 2 |
| 4.3 | Anti-Money Laundering & Financial Crime | ML(PP) Act 2022, DNFBP obligations, SCUML registration, AML programme requirements, suspicious transaction reporting, penalties, case studies | 3 |
| 4.4 | Forensic Accounting & Fraud Investigation | Forensic accounting methods, Nigerian banking crisis case study, Cadbury Nigeria case study, fraud detection techniques, whistleblower frameworks | 2 |

### Module 5: Digital Transformation & Future of the Profession (4 Lessons)
**Objective:** Prepare practitioners for technology-driven changes in accounting practice

| # | Lesson Title | Key Topics | Credit Hours |
|---|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| 5.1 | Digital Transformation in Accounting | ICAN Digital Institute, technology in audit/assurance, cloud accounting, automated bookkeeping, digital financial repositories (FRCN) | 2 |
| 5.2 | Data Analytics & AI for Accountants | AI impact on Nigerian accounting profession, data analytics in audit, automation of routine tasks, skills development, cybersecurity fundamentals | 3 |
| 5.3 | E-Governance & Digital Tax Compliance | Fiscalisation systems, e-procurement, TIN/bank account linking, digital filing, NRS digital platforms, blockchain in accounting | 2 |
| 5.4 | Building a Future-Ready Practice | Career development strategies, specialisation paths (ICAN's 8 faculties), soft skills for accountants, global mobility, ICAN vs. ACCA career positioning | 2 |

### Module Structure Summary
| Module | Lessons | Total Credit Hours | Assessment Type |
|--------|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| Module 1: Regulatory Framework | 4 | 8 | Quiz + Case Analysis |
| Module 2: Financial Reporting | 4 | 9 | Technical Problems + Quiz |
| Module 3: Taxation | 4 | 9 | Scenario-Based Assessment |
| Module 4: Auditing & Governance | 4 | 9 | Case Study + Quiz |
| Module 5: Digital Transformation | 4 | 9 | Project + Reflection |
| **Total** | **20** | **44** | |

**Design notes:**
- 44 credit hours covers a full year's MCPE requirement (40 minimum) with a 4-hour buffer
- Course can be completed in approximately 22 study sessions of 2 hours each
- Adaptive learning paths allow experienced practitioners to test out of foundational content
- Each lesson includes Nigerian-specific case studies and practical exercises
- Mobile-first design targets the 80%+ smartphone penetration among Nigerian professionals
- Microlearning format (10-15 minute segments within each lesson) suits the busy professional schedule
