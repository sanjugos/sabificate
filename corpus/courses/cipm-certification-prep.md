## 1. PRIMARY REGULATORY SOURCES

### 1.1 CIPM Establishment Act (Decree No. 58 of 1992)

- **Full Title:** Chartered Institute of Personnel Management of Nigeria Act, No. 58 of 1992
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Republic of Nigeria
- **Original Establishment:** 1968 (as Personnel Management Association of Nigeria)
- **Renamed:** 1973 (as Institute of Personnel Management of Nigeria)
- **Chartered:** 1992 (Act No. 58)
- **Official Source:** https://cipmnigeria.org/
- **Reference:** https://cipmikejachapter.org/cipm/overview-of-cipm/

**Key Provisions:**
- Establishes CIPM as the sole statutory regulatory body for the practice of Human Resource Management in Nigeria
- Mandates CIPM to regulate, certify, and discipline HR practitioners across Nigeria
- Confers on CIPM the authority to set standards for HR professional practice
- Establishes the membership grades of Associate (ACIPM), Member (MCIPM), and Fellow (FCIPM)
- Provides for the establishment of a Disciplinary Committee with powers to sanction erring practitioners
- Mandates the promotion of excellence in the acquisition and application of HR knowledge and skills, contributing to sustainable national development

**Historical Context:**
- 1968: Founded as Personnel Management Association of Nigeria
- 1973: Renamed Institute of Personnel Management of Nigeria
- 1992: Chartered by Act No. 58 (later Decree No. 58)
- The Act positions CIPM as the authoritative body responsible for setting standards, certifying practitioners, and ensuring discipline within the HR profession
- Within Nigeria, CIPM remains the only Institute with the legal mandate to regulate the practice of Human Resource Management

**Source:** https://cipmnigeria.org/ | https://cipmikejachapter.org/cipm/overview-of-cipm/

### 1.2 Federal Government MDA Circular on HR Professionalization (May 2026)

- **Reference Number:** HCSF/3065/Vol.1/230
- **Date:** May 14, 2026
- **Signed By:** Mrs Didi Esther Walson-Jack, Head of the Civil Service of the Federation
- **Issuing Office:** Office of the Head of the Civil Service of the Federation (OHCSF)
- **Coverage:** All Federal Ministries, Departments, and Agencies (MDAs)
- **Official Source:** https://cipmnigeria.org/fg-reaffirms-cipm-as-nigerias-only-chartered-hr-regulatory-body/
- **Press Coverage:** https://dailypost.ng/2026/05/18/federal-government-reaffirms-cipm-as-nigerias-only-chartered-hr-regulatory-body/

**Key Provisions:**
- Establishes mandatory professional certification requirements for officers occupying Human Resource roles within MDAs
- Twelve-month moratorium granted to affected officers to obtain requisite certification
- After moratorium expiry, only certified professionals eligible for deployment into designated HR roles
- Preference in HR postings to go to certified or actively certifying professionals by January 2026 (initial directive)
- Recognised certifications accepted from six bodies:
  1. Chartered Institute of Personnel Management of Nigeria (CIPM)
  2. Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development (CIPD), United Kingdom
  3. Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM), United States
  4. Human Resource Certification Institute (HRCI), United States
  5. Chartered Professionals in Human Resources (CPHR), Canada
  6. Any other body subsequently approved by the OHCSF

**Significance:**
- Reaffirms CIPM as Nigeria's foremost HR regulatory body
- Creates immediate demand for CIPM certification among thousands of federal civil servants
- Establishes a compliance deadline creating urgency for certification preparation
- Validates the market for CIPM exam preparation content

### 1.3 HR Practitioners' Licence (HRPL) Requirements

- **Issuing Body:** CIPM Nigeria
- **Official Page:** https://cipmnigeria.org/membership/hr-practitioners-licence/
- **Contact:** hrplicence@cipmnigeria.org | 07025709873

**Licence Structure:**
- The HRPL is a permit to operate -- an endorsement by CIPM of the holder's worthiness to practice the profession
- Validity period: 3 years from activation date
- Serves as proof of continued professional competence

**Initial Licence Requirements:**
- Must be a CIPM member in good standing (Associate, Member, or Fellow)
- Completion of HRPL Competency Assessment (online assessment)
- Payment of applicable fees
- Attendance at scheduled assessments (e.g., Saturday, March 14, 2026 at 9AM WAT)

**Renewal Requirements:**
- Minimum of 60 credit-points required for renewal
- Credit points accumulated through the HR Work Activities Route
- Qualifying activities include:
  - HR-related work activities
  - Professional training attended
  - Contributions to the Institute
  - Other approved CPD activities
- Evidence of activities must be submitted online via the CIPM portal
- Each activity type carries unique credit point values (per the CIPM Activity Table)
- Renewal fee: N15,000 (payable via portal or direct bank transfer)

**HRPL Competency Assessment Curriculum:**
- Designed by Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)
- Covers core HR functional and behavioral competencies
- Downloadable curriculum available at: https://cipmnigeria.org/hrpl-competency-assessment-curriculum/
- Full curriculum PDF: https://cipmnigeria.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/HRPL-COMPETENCY-ASSESSMENT-CURRICULUM.pdf

**Source:** https://cipmnigeria.org/membership/hr-practitioners-licence/

### 1.4 CPD Credit Point Structure

- **Total Required:** 60 credit-points per 3-year licence cycle
- **Averaging:** Approximately 20 credit-points per year
- **Submission Method:** Online upload of evidence via CIPM portal

**Credit-Earning Activities (HR Work Activities Route):**
- HR-related work activities performed during validity period
- Professional training courses completed
- CIPM-approved conferences and seminars attended
- Contributions to CIPM (committee work, mentoring, publications)
- Academic research and publications in HR
- Teaching or facilitating HR courses

**Renewal Process:**
1. Log into CIPM portal
2. Upload evidence of qualifying activities
3. System calculates accumulated credit points
4. Once minimum 60 points verified, pay N15,000 renewal fee
5. Renewed licence issued for another 3 years

**Source:** https://cipmnigeria.org/membership/hr-practitioners-licence/

### 1.5 CIPM-CIPD Mutual Recognition Agreement (September 2024)

- **Signed:** September 17, 2024
- **Parties:** CIPM Nigeria and CIPD United Kingdom
- **Preceded By:** Memorandum of Understanding signed in 2023
- **Official Page:** https://cipmnigeria.org/cipm-cipd/

**Agreement Structure:**

*CIPD to CIPM Conversion:*
- Associate Members of CIPD may apply to become Associate Members of CIPM
- Chartered Members of CIPD may apply to become Full Members of CIPM
- Chartered Fellows of CIPD may apply to become Full Members of CIPM

*Conversion Process:*
1. Application submission
2. 60-minute competency-based interview covering:
   - Functional competencies (HR technical knowledge)
   - Behavioral competencies (professional conduct and leadership)
3. Mandatory training on Nigerian Employment Laws
4. Attendance at CIPM Induction Ceremony

*Benefits:*
- Dual recognition from both CIPM and CIPD
- International career mobility and recognition
- Access to professional communities of both institutes
- CPD resources from both bodies
- Enhanced career prospects in both local and international senior roles

### 3.5 Competing HR Certifications in Nigeria

**1. SHRM Certifications (Society for Human Resource Management, USA)**
- SHRM-CP (Certified Professional)
- SHRM-SCP (Senior Certified Professional)
- Global perspective; valued by multinationals
- Salary premium in Nigeria: N200,000-N500,000 per annum
- Source: https://www.jobsregion.com/human-resources-hr-certifications/

**2. HRCI Certifications (Human Resource Certification Institute, USA)**
- PHRi (Professional in Human Resources - International)
- SPHRi (Senior Professional in Human Resources - International)
- Salary premium in Nigeria: N150,000-N400,000 per annum
- Valued at multinational corporations operating in Lagos and Abuja

**3. CIPD Certifications (Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development, UK)**
- Foundation Certificate (Level 3)
- Associate Diploma (Level 5)
- Advanced Diploma (Level 7)
- Strong recognition in UK, Europe, and parts of Africa and Asia

**4. IPMA (International Personnel Management Association)**
- HR certification with international focus

**5. IHRMP (Institute of Human Resource Management Practitioners)**
- Alternative professional body

**Comparative Position:**
- CIPM is the only Nigerian statutorily recognised HR certification body
- CIPM membership is the "gold standard" for HR practice in Nigeria and is recognised by the National Industrial Court
- Other certifications are recognised for the FG MDA mandate but CIPM has primary statutory authority
- CIPM certification provides a salary premium of N100,000-N300,000 per annum

**Source:** https://professions.ng/hr-certifications-in-nigeria/ | https://www.profolio.ng/human-resources-officer/best-certifications

## 6. PROFESSIONAL BODY REQUIREMENTS (DETAILED)

### 6.1 Complete CIPM Certification Pathway

```
                   CIPM CERTIFICATION PATHWAY
                   ==========================

[Entry] -----> STUDENT MEMBER
                    |
          +---------+---------+
          |                   |
   EXAMINATION          PRACTITIONERS'
     ROUTE                 ROUTE
          |                   |
   Foundation              (Direct
   Intermediate I          entry based
   Intermediate II         on experience
   Professional I          + credentials)
   Professional II              |
          |                     |
          +--------+------------+
                   |
            ASSOCIATE (ACIPM)
                   |
            + Work Experience
                   |
           FULL MEMBER (MCIPM)
                   |
            + Distinguished Service
            + Years of Membership
                   |
            FELLOW (FCIPM)
                   |
          HR PRACTITIONERS' LICENCE (HRPL)
          (3-year renewable; 60 CPD credits)
```

### 6.2 Examination Route Detail

**Stage 1: Foundation**
- Subjects: 6
- Entry: WASSCE/NECO/GCE with 5 credits including English and Mathematics
- Focus: Foundational business and HR knowledge

**Stage 2: Intermediate I**
- Subjects: 5
- Entry: Completion of Foundation OR OND/NCE holder direct entry
- Focus: Business administration, economics, finance, statistics, communication

**Stage 3: Intermediate II**
- Subjects: 4
- Entry: Completion of Intermediate I OR BSc/HND holder direct entry
- Focus: Learning & development, public administration, workforce planning, comparative HRM

**Stage 4: Professional I**
- Subjects: Multiple (per official syllabus)
- Entry: Completion of Intermediate II
- Focus: Advanced HR management, industrial relations, organisational behaviour, compensation

**Stage 5: Professional II**
- Subjects: Multiple (per official syllabus)
- Entry: Completion of Professional I
- Focus: Strategic HRM, Nigerian labour law, HR digitization, career management

### 6.3 Practitioners' Route to Membership

- For experienced HR practitioners who have not taken CIPM examinations
- Requires demonstration of significant HR work experience
- Application process includes documentation of credentials and experience
- 474 practitioners inducted via this route at the 68th Induction Ceremony

### 6.4 Executive Route to Membership

- For senior executives in HR leadership positions
- Abbreviated assessment process
- 34 full members inducted via this route at the 68th Induction Ceremony

### 6.5 CIPM-CIPD Dual Pathway

**From CIPD to CIPM:**
1. Hold valid CIPD membership (Associate, Chartered Member, or Chartered Fellow)
2. Apply to CIPM with documentation
3. Pass 60-minute competency-based interview (functional + behavioral)
4. Complete training on Nigerian Employment Laws
5. Attend CIPM Induction Ceremony
6. Receive corresponding CIPM membership grade

**From CIPM to CIPD:**
- Details managed through the Mutual Recognition Agreement
- CIPM members can apply for corresponding CIPD membership level
- International recognition and career mobility benefits
