### 1.2 Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023 -- Automated Decision-Making Provisions

- **Full Title:** Nigeria Data Protection Act, 2023
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Government of Nigeria (signed by President Bola Tinubu)
- **Effective Date:** June 12, 2023
- **Replaces:** Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019
- **Regulatory Authority:** Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC)
- **Official PDF:** https://ndpc.gov.ng/download/nigeria-data-protection-act-2023

**Section 37 -- Right Not to Be Subject to Automated Decision-Making:**

- **Core Prohibition (Section 37(1)):** A data subject has the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing of personal data, including profiling, which produces legal or similarly significant effects concerning the data subject
- **Three Exceptions (where automated decisions ARE permitted):**
  1. Necessary for entering into or performing a contract between data subject and data controller
  2. Authorized by written law which establishes suitable measures to safeguard fundamental rights and freedoms
  3. Based on the data subject's explicit consent
- **Mandatory Safeguards (when exceptions apply):**
  - Right to obtain human intervention or oversight
  - Right to express the data subject's point of view
  - Right to contest the decision
  - Data controller must implement appropriate measures to safeguard fundamental rights and freedoms
  - Required transparency about the automated decision-making process
  - Mechanisms for human review must be in place
- **Section 38:** Data subject may request restriction of processing in certain circumstances

**Business Relevance:** Any company using AI for credit scoring, hiring decisions, loan approvals, insurance underwriting, or other automated decisions affecting individuals must comply with Section 37. Failure to provide human oversight and contestability mechanisms creates legal liability.

**Penalties:** Standard maximum N2,000,000 or 2% of gross revenue; Higher maximum N10,000,000 or 2% annual gross revenue (for entities of major importance)

### 1.4 CBN Guidelines on AI in Banking: AML/CFT AI Mandate (2026)

- **Full Title:** CBN Anti-Money Laundering/Counter-Financing of Terrorism (AML/CFT) Guidelines incorporating AI and Machine Learning requirements
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN)
- **Date:** Circular issued March 10, 2026
- **URL:** https://techcabal.com/2026/03/12/cbn-wants-ai-to-fight-money-laundering/

**Key Provisions:**
- **Mandatory AI/ML Integration:** All financial institutions (banks, fintechs, payment companies) must deploy automated AML systems using AI, ML, predictive analytics, and behavioral monitoring
- **Compliance Timelines:**
  - Deposit Money Banks: 18 months to full compliance
  - Other Financial Institutions (OFIs): 24 months to full compliance
  - Submit detailed implementation roadmap within 90 days of circular issuance
- **Governance Requirements:**
  - Establish governance structures to oversee automated system performance
  - Validate AI models regularly
  - Ensure data protection safeguards comply with NDPA
  - Human oversight remains essential -- institutions must validate AI-generated outputs
  - Conduct regular audits for accuracy and accountability
- **Risk-Based Supervision:** Tiered, outcome-based obligations with risk-adjusted supervision

**Significance:** Historic first for the CBN -- marks the shift from manual compliance processes to technology-driven financial crime monitoring.
