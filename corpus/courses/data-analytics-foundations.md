### 1.4 Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023 -- Data Analytics Provisions

- **Full Title:** Nigeria Data Protection Act, 2023
- **Date Signed:** June 12, 2023
- **Enforcing Body:** Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC)
- **Key Provisions Relevant to Data Analytics:**
  - **Section 37 -- Automated Decision-Making:** Restricts exclusive use of automated decision-making processes for processing personal data with legal/similar impact, except with consent, legal obligation, or contractual necessity
  - **Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs):** Required for AI-driven analytics and significant data processing operations
  - **Record of Processing Activities:** Organizations must maintain processing records -- a core compliance artifact that data analysts must understand
  - **Lawful Bases for Processing (Section 25):** Data analysts must understand the six lawful bases: consent, contractual necessity, legal obligation, vital interests, public interest, legitimate interests
  - **7 Data Protection Principles (Section 24):** Lawfulness/fairness/transparency, purpose limitation, data minimisation, storage limitation, accuracy, security, accountability
- **Penalty Structure:** Up to N10M or 2% annual gross revenue (DCPMI entities); criminal penalties up to 1 year imprisonment
- **Relevance to Course:** Every data analyst in Nigeria must understand NDPA obligations; course teaches ethical data handling alongside technical skills

**Sources:** [CookieYes -- NDPA 2023 Guide](https://www.cookieyes.com/blog/nigeria-data-protection-act-ndpa/) | [Adeola Oyinlade -- NDPA AI Restrictions](https://www.adeolaoyinlade.com/en/nigeria-data-protection-act-2023-ndpa-restrictions-on-automated-data-processing-and-artificial-intelligence/) | [Securiti -- NDPA Overview](https://securiti.ai/overview-of-nigeria-data-protection-act/)

- **Gap:** While open datasets exist (NBS, NIBSS, Hugging Face synthetic banking data), the course needs curated, cleaned, and pedagogically structured datasets that map to specific lesson objectives
- **SME Need:** Data engineer or experienced Nigerian data analyst to curate and validate datasets for each module, ensuring they contain realistic Nigerian business patterns (naira denominations, Nigerian bank codes, state-level breakdowns, real industry ratios)
- **Impact if Unfilled:** Exercises will feel generic rather than authentically Nigerian

### 6.2 Nigerian Employer Expectations for Data Analyst Portfolios

- **Gap:** What specific deliverables do Nigerian hiring managers (banking, telecom, consulting, FMCG) expect to see in a data analyst's portfolio?
- **SME Need:** Interviews with 3--5 Nigerian data analytics hiring managers across key sectors to validate the portfolio artifact specification
- **Impact if Unfilled:** Capstone project may not align with actual employer evaluation criteria

### 6.3 Tool Version and Infrastructure Realities

- **Gap:** What versions of Excel, SQL environments, and Power BI are actually available to Nigerian learners? Many learners may use pirated Office copies, free alternatives (Google Sheets, LibreOffice), or mobile-only access
- **SME Need:** Survey of Nigerian early-career professionals' actual technology access: device types (laptop vs. phone), Excel version, internet reliability, data costs
- **Impact if Unfilled:** Course may assume tool access that learners do not have; exercises may fail on older software versions

### 6.4 SQL Environment Setup for Nigerian Learners

- **Gap:** Best recommended free SQL environment for Nigerian learners with limited bandwidth -- PostgreSQL local install vs. cloud-based options (BigQuery free tier, SQLite) vs. browser-based SQL practice platforms
- **SME Need:** Technical validation of setup instructions given typical Nigerian internet speeds (2--15 Mbps average) and data costs
- **Impact if Unfilled:** Learners may abandon the SQL modules due to setup frustration

### 6.5 Sector-Specific Analytics Workflows

- **Gap:** What do actual day-in-the-life workflows look like for data analysts at Nigerian banks, telecom companies, and fintech firms?
- **SME Need:** Practicing Nigerian data analysts to contribute anonymized workflow descriptions, sample reporting cadences, and common analytics requests they receive
- **Impact if Unfilled:** Course teaches tools but not the professional context in which they're used

### 6.6 Competitive Pricing Intelligence

- **Gap:** What do Nigerian learners actually pay for comparable data analytics training? Most providers do not publish prices online
- **SME Need:** Price benchmarks from Utiva, Data Science Nigeria, AltSchool, Lagos Data School, NiveDigital, Semicolon; comparison with 3MTT free training and ALX scholarship model
- **Impact if Unfilled:** Cannot validate SABIficate's pricing against actual market rates

### 6.7 Power BI Service vs. Desktop Availability

- **Gap:** Power BI Desktop is free but Power BI Service (cloud publishing, sharing dashboards) requires Microsoft 365 subscriptions. What access do Nigerian SMEs and their employees typically have? How do Nigerian data analysts share dashboards in practice?
- **SME Need:** Survey of Power BI usage patterns in Nigerian organisations; workarounds for learners without Power BI Service access (export to PDF, publish-to-web for public dashboards)
- **Impact if Unfilled:** Capstone dashboard project may require cloud features learners cannot access

### 6.8 NDPA Compliance Practical Application for Data Analysts

- **Gap:** How do Section 37 (automated decision-making restrictions) and DPIA requirements practically affect data analysts' daily work in Nigerian organizations? What compliance steps must a junior analyst follow when building a dashboard that processes personal data?
- **SME Need:** Legal/compliance SME or DPO from a Nigerian organization to provide practical guidance translatable into course content
- **Impact if Unfilled:** Data protection module will be theoretical rather than practical
