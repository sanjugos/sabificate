## 1. PRIMARY REGULATORY SOURCES

### 1.1 Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA)

- **Full Title:** Nigeria Data Protection Act, 2023
- **Date Signed:** June 12, 2023 by President Bola Ahmed Tinubu
- **Structure:** 66 sections organized into 12 Parts plus a Schedule
- **Official PDF:** https://ndpc.gov.ng/download/nigeria-data-protection-act-2023
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Government of Nigeria / National Assembly
- **Replaces:** Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019 and NDPR Implementation Framework 2020

**12-Part Structure of the NDPA:**

| Part | Title | Coverage |
|------|-------|----------|
| I | Objectives & Application | Purpose, scope, territorial application |
| II | Establishment of the Commission | Creates NDPC, Governing Council |
| III | Functions & Powers of the Commission | Regulatory authority, enforcement |
| IV | Commission Financing | Funding mechanisms |
| V | Data Processing Principles | 7 core principles, lawful bases |
| VI | Rights of Data Subjects | 8 data subject rights |
| VII | Data Security | Technical/organizational measures, breach notification |
| VIII | Cross-Border Data Transfer | Adequacy, transfer mechanisms |
| IX | Registration of Data Controllers/Processors | DCPMI classification, registration |
| X | Enforcement | Compliance orders, investigations |
| XI | Legal Proceedings | Jurisdiction, limitation periods (3 months), warrants |
| XII | Miscellaneous Provisions | Transitional, supplementary matters |

**7 Data Protection Principles (Section 24):**
1. Lawfulness, Fairness & Transparency
2. Purpose Limitation
3. Data Minimization
4. Storage Limitation
5. Accuracy
6. Security (Integrity & Confidentiality)
7. Accountability

**6 Lawful Bases for Processing (Section 25):**
1. Consent -- freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous
2. Contractual Necessity -- performance of contract or pre-contractual steps
3. Legal Obligation -- required by applicable law
4. Vital Interests -- protect welfare of data subject or another person
5. Public Interest / Official Authority -- task in public interest
6. Legitimate Interests -- NEW in NDPA (absent from NDPR 2019); controller's interests unless overridden by data subject rights

**8 Data Subject Rights:**
1. Right to Information (informed before processing)
2. Right of Access (copy in electronic format)
3. Right to Rectification (correct inaccurate/outdated data)
4. Right to Erasure / Right to be Forgotten
5. Right to Restrict Processing
6. Right to Object (including absolute right to object to direct marketing)
7. Right to Data Portability (structured, machine-readable format)
8. Right Against Automated Decision-Making (Section 37 -- right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing including profiling)

**Penalty Structure (Section 48):**
- DCPMI entities: N10,000,000 or 2% of annual gross revenue (whichever higher)
- Non-DCPMI entities: N2,000,000 or 2% of annual gross revenue (whichever higher)
- Criminal: up to 1 year imprisonment for non-compliance with NDPC orders
- Civil: compensation orders to data subjects for harm

**Breach Notification (Section 40):**
- Notify NDPC within 72 hours of becoming aware
- Notify affected data subjects immediately if high risk to rights/freedoms
- Must include: nature of breach, categories/numbers affected, consequences, mitigation measures
- Processors must promptly notify the controller

**Children's Data:**
- Under 18: parental/guardian consent required
- Age verification mechanisms mandatory (government-approved ID)
- Child-friendly privacy policies required
- Exceptions: vital interests, social/health/education services, court proceedings

**Sensitive Personal Data (defined):**
- Genetic and biometric data for unique identification
- Race or ethnic origin
- Religious or similar beliefs
- Health status
- Sex life
- Political opinions or affiliations
- Trade union memberships
- Other categories prescribed by NDPC

**Automated Decision-Making (Section 37):**
- Right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing
- Exceptions: consent, legal requirement, contractual necessity
- Must provide: human intervention/oversight, right to express view, right to contest
- Controller must inform data subject of existence of automated decision-making before collecting data

**Cross-Border Transfer (Section 43):**
- Requires adequate level of protection through: applicable law, binding corporate rules, contractual clauses, codes of conduct, certification mechanisms
- Derogations: explicit consent (with risk warning), contractual necessity, third-party contracts in data subject's interest, public interest, legal claims, vital interests, sole benefit of data subject
- NDPC solely determines adequacy -- NO adequacy list published yet as of 2026
- Must record basis for transfer
- National Assembly must approve adoption of international cross-border codes/standards

### 1.2 General Application and Implementation Directive (GAID) 2025

- **Full Title:** Nigeria Data Protection Act -- General Application and Implementation Directive (GAID) 2025
- **Date Issued:** March 20, 2025
- **Effective Date:** September 19, 2025 (fees from January 2026)
- **Issuing Body:** Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC)
- **PDF:** https://ndpc.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/NDP-ACT-GAID-2025-MARCH-20TH.pdf
- **Key Reference:** https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/privacy-protection/1669814/

**Significance:** The GAID replaces the NDPR 2019 and NDPR Implementation Framework 2020 as the primary implementation guidance. The two governing instruments for data protection in Nigeria are now the NDPA 2023 and the GAID 2025.

**DCPMI Categories and Filing Fees:**

| Category | Data Subjects (6 months) | Filing Fee |
|----------|--------------------------|------------|
| Ultra-High Level (UHL) -- 50,000+ | 50,000+ | N1,000,000 |
| Ultra-High Level (UHL) -- 25,000-49,999 | 25,000-49,999 | N750,000 |
| Ultra-High Level (UHL) -- under 25,000 | <25,000 | N500,000 |
| Extra-High Level (EHL) -- 10,000+ | 10,000+ | N250,000 |
| Extra-High Level (EHL) -- 5,000-9,999 | 5,000-9,999 | N200,000 |
| Extra-High Level (EHL) -- under 5,000 | <5,000 | N100,000 |
| Ordinary High Level (OHL) | 200-999 | N10,000-N100,000 |

**Compliance Audit Return (CAR) Requirements:**
- Entities established before June 12, 2023: file by March 31 annually
- Entities established after June 12, 2023: file within 15 months, then March 31 annually
- Late filing penalty: 50% of applicable fee
- UHL and EHL: register once, file CAR annually
- OHL: renew registration annually, no mandatory CAR

**Standard Notice to Address Grievance (SNAG) -- NEW:**
- Allows data subjects to formally notify organizations of privacy violations before escalating to NDPC
- Can be issued by data subjects, their representatives, or civil society organizations
- Served through physical address, telephone, email, courier, or other reasonable methods
- Data controller/processor must communicate decision on SNAG to NDPC through electronic platform

**Records of Processing Activities (ROPA):**
- Mandatory for all data controllers/processors
- Must detail processing activities within preceding 6 months
- Semi-annual internal data protection reports submitted by DPOs integrated into ROPA

**Data Processing Agreements:**
- GAID specifies 20 mandatory clauses for written contracts with processors
- Must include: purposes, lawful bases, technical/organizational measures, DPIA outcomes, dispute resolution

**Emerging Technology Requirements (AI, IoT, Blockchain):**
- NDPA compliance required
- Data protection by design and default
- DPIA completion mandatory
- Continuous post-deployment monitoring required

**Data Retention:** Maximum 6 months under GAID guidance (general default)

### 1.3 NDPC Registration Requirements

**DCPMI Registration:**
- Data Controllers/Processors of Major Importance must register within 6 months of becoming DCPMI
- Original deadline: October 31, 2024 (late registration still ongoing with penalty)
- Required information: name, address, DPO details, data categories/volume, processing purposes, cross-border transfer details, risk overview, security measures
- Must have .ng domain website

**DPO Registration Fees (by organizational type):**

| Category | Fee |
|----------|-----|
| Major Data Controller/Processor | N250,000 |
| Regular Data Controller/Processor | N100,000 |
| Small Business (<40 staff, <N50M turnover) | N25,000 |
| Government/Public Entity | Free |
| Minor Processing (MDP-OHL) | N10,000 |

**Required Documentation:**
- CAC registration certificate
- DPO appointment letter and credentials
- Valid identification for director
- Qualifications for two staff members
- Data processing overview
- Description of security safeguards
- Evidence of .ng domain website

### 1.4 Data Protection Officer (DPO) Requirements

**Mandatory Appointment:**
- Required for all DCPMIs
- Required for organizations processing data of 10,000+ subjects annually
- Required for organizations handling sensitive data
- Required for organizations managing critical national infrastructure

**Qualifications:**
- Expert knowledge of data protection law and practices
- Minimum Bachelor's degree in Law, Computer Science, IT, Cybersecurity, Data Science, or Business Administration (or relevant certifications: CIPP, CIPM, ISO 27001)
- Must undergo NDPC Annual Credential Assessment
- NDPC verification of DPO certification is the metric for fitness determination

**Responsibilities:**
- Advise on NDPA compliance
- Monitor compliance with Act and organizational policies
- Serve as NDPC contact point
- Compile semi-annual compliance reports for ROPA integration
- Can serve multiple entities if no conflicts

**Notification to NDPC:** Within 60 days of appointment or change

### 1.5 Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

**When Required:**
- Processing likely to result in high risk to rights and freedoms of data subjects
- AI, automated decision-making, or large-scale profiling
- Large-scale processing of sensitive data
- Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas
- AI, IoT, or blockchain deployments

**DPIA Must Include:**
- Systematic description of envisaged processing and its purpose
- Assessment of necessity and proportionality
- Evaluation of risks to data subject rights
- Identification of mitigating measures
- Legitimate interest pursued by controller/third party

**Process:**
- Must be completed BEFORE processing begins
- High-risk assessments require NDPC consultation before proceeding
- Completed DPIA must be retained and made available to NDPC on request

---

### 1.6 Data Protection Compliance Organisation (DPCO)

**Definition:** Entity licensed by NDPC for training, auditing, consulting, and rendering compliance services under the NDP Act 2023.

**Requirements:**
- At least two staff with professional/academic data protection certifications
- N2,000,000 licensing fee (one-time)
- CAC registration
- 317 DPCOs licensed as of early 2026

**Role:** Organizations file their CAR through a licensed DPCO. DPCOs conduct compliance audits on behalf of DCPMIs.

## 3. COMPARISON: NDPR 2019 vs NDPA 2023

| Feature | NDPR 2019 | NDPA 2023 |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Legal Status | Regulation (subsidiary legislation under NITDA Act) | Primary legislation (Act of National Assembly) |
| Supervisory Authority | NITDA | Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) |
| Lawful Bases | 5 (no legitimate interest) | 6 (adds legitimate interest) |
| DPO Requirement | All data controllers | Only DCPMIs and qualifying organizations |
| Cross-Border Transfer | HAGF/NITDA determined adequacy | NDPC solely determines adequacy |
| Enforcement Authority | Limited (breach = breach of NITDA Act) | Comprehensive (dedicated NDPC enforcement powers) |
| Penalties | Up to 2% of annual gross turnover or N10M | Tiered: N10M/2% for DCPMI; N2M/2% for others + imprisonment |
| DPIA | Required but less structured | Required with NDPC consultation for high-risk |
| Automated Decision-Making | Limited provisions | Comprehensive Section 37 protections |
| Grievance Mechanism | Not structured | SNAG mechanism introduced in GAID |
| Implementation Instrument | NDPR Implementation Framework 2020 | GAID 2025 |

**Key Sources:**
- https://home.walla.my/blog/ndpr-vs-ndpa-how-nigeria-s-data-privacy-law-is-evolving
- https://www.hoganlovells.com/en/publications/key-changes-brought-by-the-nigerian-data-protection-act-2023

## 4. INTERNATIONAL COMPARISON

### 4.1 NDPA vs GDPR

| Feature | GDPR | NDPA |
|---------|------|------|
| Enacted | May 25, 2018 | June 12, 2023 |
| DPA Structure | Multiple per EU member state | Single NDPC |
| Maximum Fines | EUR 20M or 4% global revenue | N10M (~EUR6K) or 2% revenue |
| Breach Notification | 72 hours | 72 hours |
| Cross-Border Transfers | Strict (adequacy/SCCs/BCRs) | Similar but no adequacy list published |
| DPO Requirement | Large-scale/sensitive processing | DCPMIs and qualifying organizations |
| Legitimate Interest | Yes | Yes (new in NDPA) |
| Data Protection by Design | Explicit Article 25 | Not explicit section but embedded in guidance |
| Pseudonymised Data | Yes, defined and treated distinctly | Not specifically addressed |
| Right to be Forgotten | Yes | Yes |
| Children's Age Threshold | Varies 13-16 across member states | Under 18 |

### 4.2 NDPA vs Kenya Data Protection Act 2019

- Kenya passed DPA in 2019; established Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)
- Kenya requires ODPC registration, DPO appointment, DPIAs for high-risk processing
- Nigeria's NDPA has more nuanced penalty structure distinguishing enterprise size
- Kenya has been enforcing since 2021; Nigeria since 2023 with GAID from September 2025
- Source: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13600869.2025.2506918

### 4.3 NDPA vs South Africa POPIA

- POPIA in force since 2021; most mature GDPR-aligned African regime
- POPIA generally does not apply extraterritorially; NDPA does
- Both draw from GDPR principles; both require consent, purpose limitation, data minimization
- POPIA enforced by Information Regulator; NDPA by NDPC
- POPIA does not distinguish enterprise size in penalties; NDPA does (DCPMI vs non-DCPMI)
- Source: https://gdprlocal.com/gdpr-and-data-protection-laws-in-africa-a-comparison/

### 4.4 Malabo Convention

- African Union Convention on Cyber Security and Personal Data Protection adopted 2014
- Entered into force June 8, 2023 with 15 ratifications
- Nigeria has NOT ratified the Malabo Convention
- IIM CDPO certification aligns with Malabo Convention standards
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malabo_Convention
