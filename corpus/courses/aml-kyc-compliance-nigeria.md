## Corpus Summary

This corpus covers the full AML/KYC regulatory landscape for Nigerian financial institutions, anchored in the 2022 legislative overhaul (Money Laundering Prevention and Prohibition Act, Terrorism Prevention and Prohibition Act, Proceeds of Crime Act), the CBN AML/CFT/CPF Regulations 2022, the landmark CBN 2026 Baseline Standards for Automated AML Solutions (12 standards, 100 requirements), the NFIU Act 2018, FATF Recommendations and Nigeria's mutual evaluation journey (grey-listed February 2023, delisted October 2025 after completing 19-item action plan), CBN Customer Due Diligence Regulations 2023, CBN PEP Guidance Notes 2022, SCUML guidelines for DNFBPs, CBN tiered KYC framework, and the ISA 2025 for virtual asset regulation. The corpus includes 6 major Nigerian enforcement case studies, 5 existing training providers with course details, professional body requirements from CIBN, ACAMS and ICA, 6 reference textbooks, and a proposed 5-module/25-lesson structure mapping to all sources.

## Primary Regulatory Sources

### 1. Money Laundering (Prevention and Prohibition) Act, 2022
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Republic of Nigeria (signed by President Buhari)
- **Date:** 2022-05-13
- **Relevance:** Primary AML legislation for Nigeria. Repeals 2011 Act. Establishes cash payment limits (N5M individuals, N10M corporates), 24-hour STR filing requirement, CDD obligations for FIs and DNFBPs, penalties of 4-14 years imprisonment for individuals. Classifies lawyers as DNFBPs.
- **Key Sections:** Cash payment restrictions (S.2), Customer identification (S.5), STR obligations (S.6), International transfer reporting above US$10,000 (S.4), Penalties (S.15-18), DNFBP coverage including legal practitioners
- **Reference:** https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/money-laundering/1233474/key-provisions-in-the-money-laundering-prevention-and-prohibition-act-2022

### 2. CBN AML/CFT/CPF Regulations, 2022
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2022-06-20
- **Relevance:** Sectoral AML regulations for all CBN-regulated financial institutions. Covers commercial banks, merchant banks, MFBs, PMBs, PSBs, BDCs, DFIs, finance companies, PSPs. Requires designation of Chief Compliance Officers, comprehensive AML/CFT/CPF compliance programmes, prohibition of anonymous accounts, and risk-based approach to customer identification.
- **Key Sections:** CCO designation requirements, compliance programme structure, anonymous account prohibition, risk-based CDD, record keeping (minimum 5 years), ongoing monitoring obligations
- **Reference:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/Out/2022/FPRD/AML%20CIRCULAR%20AND%20REGULATIONS%20MERGED.pdf

### 3. CBN Baseline Standards for Automated AML Solutions, 2026
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2026-03-10
- **Relevance:** Landmark directive requiring all FIs to implement automated AML/CFT/CPF systems. 12 standards and 100 requirements covering customer identification, risk profiling, sanctions screening, transaction monitoring, case management, regulatory reporting, and audit logging. Requires behavioral pattern recognition, anomaly detection, predictive analytics. DMBs have 18 months compliance timeline, other FIs 24 months.
- **Key Sections:** 12 functional standards, proportionality principle, governance and audit requirements, enforcement mechanisms
- **Reference:** https://www.acelera.law/publications/cbn-issues-baseline-standards-for-automated-aml-solutions-a-new-compliance-paradigm-for-financial-institutions

### 4. Nigerian Financial Intelligence Unit (Establishment) Act, 2018
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Republic of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2018-07-12
- **Relevance:** Established NFIU as autonomous agency domiciled with CBN. Empowers NFIU to impose penalties for non-compliance with reporting requirements under Section 25(2).
- **Key Sections:** Establishment and autonomy provisions, penalty powers (S.25(2)), reporting obligations, intelligence dissemination framework
- **Reference:** https://www.nfiu.gov.ng/index.php/nfiu

### 5. Terrorism (Prevention and Prohibition) Act, 2022
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Republic of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2022-05-12
- **Relevance:** Companion legislation to the ML Act 2022. Provides framework for combating terrorism financing and implementing international instruments on terrorism prevention.
- **Key Sections:** Terrorism financing offenses, targeted financial sanctions, asset freezing provisions, international cooperation mechanisms
- **Reference:** https://thenigerialawyer.com/download-money-laundering-prevention-and-prohibition-act-2022-terrorism-prevention-and-prohibition-act-2022-and-proceeds-of-crime-recovery-and-management-act-2022/

### 6. Proceeds of Crime (Recovery and Management) Act, 2022
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Republic of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2022-05-12
- **Relevance:** Third pillar of the 2022 legislative overhaul. Comprehensive provisions for seizure, confiscation, forfeiture, and management of properties derived from unlawful activity. Section 74 controversially shifts burden of proof to defendants.
- **Key Sections:** Seizure and confiscation powers, forfeiture provisions, asset management framework, burden of proof (S.74)
- **Reference:** https://lawnigeria.com/2024/02/06/proceeds-of-crime-recovery-and-management-act-2022/

### 7. CBN Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Regulations, 2023
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2023-06-20
- **Relevance:** Detailed CDD requirements for all CBN-regulated FIs. Mandates collection and verification of social media handles as KYC requirement.
- **Key Sections:** Standard CDD measures, simplified CDD for low-risk, enhanced CDD for high-risk customers, social media handle verification requirement, beneficial ownership identification
- **Reference:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/Out/2023/CCD/CBN%20Customer%20Due%20diligence%20Reg.%202023-combined.pdf

### 8. CBN Guidance Notes on Politically Exposed Persons, 2022
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2022
- **Relevance:** Regulatory framework for PEP management. Categorizes PEPs as domestic, foreign, or international organizations PEPs.
- **Key Sections:** PEP categorization, EDD requirements, senior management approval, PEP register requirements, source of wealth documentation
- **Reference:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/Out/2023/CCD/Approved%20PEP%20Guidance%20May%202023-combined.pdf

### 9. CBN Three-Tiered KYC Requirements
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2013 (updated December 2023)
- **Relevance:** Foundational KYC framework for financial inclusion. Tier 1 (low-value, minimal documentation), Tier 2 (medium-value), Tier 3 (high-value, full documentation). December 2023 update added BVN and NIN requirements for Tier 1 accounts.
- **Key Sections:** Tier 1/2/3 account requirements and transaction limits, December 2023 BVN/NIN update
- **Reference:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/out/2013/ccd/3%20tiered%20kyc%20requirements.pdf

### 10. FATF Recommendations (40 Recommendations, updated October 2025)
- **Issuing Body:** Financial Action Task Force (FATF)
- **Date:** 2012 (updated October 2025)
- **Relevance:** International AML/CFT standards. Nigeria achieved Compliant/Largely Compliant on 37 of 40 by October 2025.
- **Key Sections:** R.1 Risk Assessment, R.10 CDD, R.12 PEPs, R.13 Correspondent Banking, R.15 New Technologies, R.20 STR Filing, R.24-25 Beneficial Ownership Transparency
- **Reference:** https://www.fatf-gafi.org/content/dam/fatf-gafi/recommendations/FATF%20Recommendations%202012.pdf.coredownload.inline.pdf

### 11. GIABA Mutual Evaluation Report of Nigeria (2nd Round)
- **Issuing Body:** GIABA / FATF
- **Date:** 2021 (with follow-up reports through 2024)
- **Relevance:** Comprehensive assessment of Nigeria's AML/CFT framework. Initial 2021 report rated Nigeria Compliant/Largely Compliant on only 13 of 40 Recommendations. Led to 2023 grey-listing.
- **Key Sections:** Technical compliance ratings, effectiveness ratings, 19-item action plan
- **Reference:** https://www.fatf-gafi.org/content/dam/fatf-gafi/fsrb-mer/GIABA-Mutual%20Evaluation%20Report%20of%20the%20Federal%20Republic%20of%20Nigeria.pdf

### 12. SCUML AML/CFT Compliance Guidelines for DNFIs
- **Issuing Body:** Special Control Unit Against Money Laundering (SCUML) / EFCC
- **Date:** 2019
- **Relevance:** AML/CFT compliance guidelines for Designated Non-Financial Institutions and Businesses (DNFBPs).
- **Key Sections:** Registration requirements, CDD obligations, STR filing requirements, record keeping, internal controls
- **Reference:** https://www.scuml.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/AML-CFT-COMPLIANCE-GUIDELINES-FOR-DNFIS-1.pdf

### 13. NFIU Guidelines for Identification, Verification and Reporting of Suspicious Transactions (December 2024)
- **Issuing Body:** Nigerian Financial Intelligence Unit (NFIU)
- **Date:** 2024-12-13
- **Relevance:** Latest STR filing guidelines. Defines 72-hour investigation timeline and 24-hour reporting deadline.
- **Key Sections:** STR identification process, 72-hour investigation window, 24-hour filing deadline, required documentation checklist
- **Reference:** https://www.mondaq.com/nigeria/money-laundering/1563230/anti-money-laundering-regulation-in-nigeria-recent-updates

### 14. CBN Guidelines on International Money Transfer Services (Revised), 2024
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2024-01-31
- **Relevance:** Revised guidelines for cross-border remittances incorporating AML/CFT requirements from CDD Regulations 2023 and PEP Guidance Notes 2022.
- **Key Sections:** Inbound-only IMTO restrictions, CDD/KYC requirements, reporting obligations, suspicious transaction reporting timeline
- **Reference:** https://businessday.ng/news/legal-business/article/key-changes-to-the-revised-international-money-transfer-services-guidelines/

### 15. Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA) 2020 -- Beneficial Ownership Provisions
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Republic of Nigeria
- **Date:** 2020-08
- **Relevance:** Sections 119-123 require companies to disclose persons with significant control (PSC) holding over 25% shares, voting rights, or significant influence.
- **Key Sections:** S.119-123 Disclosure of PSC, Persons with Significant Control Regulations 2022, CAC Beneficial Ownership Register
- **Reference:** https://www.afriwise.com/blog/the-beneficial-ownership-register-implementing-the-financial-action-task-forces-recommendations-on-money-laundering-and-terrorism-financing-in-nigeria

### 16. Investments and Securities Act (ISA) 2025 -- Virtual Asset Provisions
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Republic of Nigeria (signed by President Tinubu)
- **Date:** 2025-03
- **Relevance:** Section 357 recognizes digital assets as securities. All VASPs must obtain SEC license. Mandatory KYC/AML compliance for crypto exchanges.
- **Key Sections:** S.357 virtual asset definitions, VASP licensing requirements, KYC/AML obligations
- **Reference:** https://www.ibanet.org/Updated-overview-of-Nigerian-cryptocurrency-landscape

### 17. National Inherent Risk Assessment of Money Laundering in Nigeria, 2022
- **Issuing Body:** Nigerian Inter-Agency Committee (NIGSAC) / NFIU
- **Date:** 2022-10
- **Relevance:** Updated national ML risk assessment identifying key vulnerabilities.
- **Key Sections:** ML risk ratings by sector, terrorism financing methods, financial inclusion risks, DNFBP vulnerability assessment
- **Reference:** https://nigsac.gov.ng/assets/niradocs/nira%20ml%20report%20-%20october2022.pdf

## Nigerian Case Studies

### 1. CBN Fines 29 Banks N15 Billion for AML/CT Violations (2024)
- **Organizations:** CBN, 29 Nigerian commercial banks, CIBN
- **Summary:** CBN Governor Cardoso announced at the 2024 CIBN Bankers' Night that 29 banks were penalized a combined N15 billion for AML/CTF violations. Zenith Bank shareholders blamed fines for lower dividend payouts.
- **Source:** https://businessday.ng/banking-finance/article/29-banks-fined-n15bn-for-anti-money-laundering-counter-terrorism-violations/
- **Teaching Points:** Systemic nature of AML failures; financial impact on profitability; board accountability; escalating enforcement

### 2. CBN Revokes 4,173 BDC Licenses (March 2024)
- **Organizations:** CBN, 4,173 Bureau de Change operators, CAC
- **Summary:** Largest mass license revocation in Nigerian banking history. BDCs failed to comply with AML/CFT/CPF regulations. CAC gave deadlines to change names or face dissolution.
- **Source:** https://www.channelstv.com/2024/03/01/cbn-revokes-operational-licenses-of-4173-bdcs/
- **Teaching Points:** Scale of non-compliance in informal sector; license revocation as ultimate sanction; regulatory reforms following enforcement

### 3. Binance/Tigran Gambaryan Money Laundering Case (2024)
- **Organizations:** Binance, EFCC, Tigran Gambaryan, Nadeem Anjarwalla
- **Summary:** Nigerian authorities arrested Binance executives on ML charges, accusing Binance of facilitating $23 billion in untraceable funds. Gambaryan detained 8 months before charges dropped.
- **Source:** https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2024/12/10/tigran-gambaryan-the-star-crypto-investigator-kidnapped-by-nigeria/
- **Teaching Points:** VASPs face severe enforcement risk; corporate vs individual liability; crypto-naira nexus

### 4. Former CBN Governor Emefiele Prosecution
- **Organizations:** EFCC, Former CBN Governor Godwin Emefiele
- **Summary:** Arraigned on 22 counts including $4.5 billion fraud, criminal breach of trust, forgery. Court ordered forfeiture of $1.4 million. Wife declared wanted for ML.
- **Source:** https://nairametrics.com/2024/04/30/five-former-nigerian-officials-embroiled-in-n6-03-trillion-efccs-probe-cases/
- **Teaching Points:** Even the highest regulator is not immune; PEP risk is real; asset forfeiture under POCA 2022

### 5. EFCC Record 4,111 Convictions (2024)
- **Organizations:** EFCC, various cybercrime syndicates
- **Summary:** Historic 4,111 convictions with recoveries exceeding N365.4 billion. Major raid arrested 792 suspects in Victoria Island for crypto fraud. EFCC uncovered PEPs using yahoo boys for ML.
- **Source:** https://www.vanguardngr.com/2025/03/crackdown-on-economic-financial-crimes-efccs-landmark-cases-recoveries/
- **Teaching Points:** Cybercrime-ML nexus is dominant typology; PEP exploitation of informal channels; scale of enforcement

### 6. Nigeria's FATF Grey-Listing and Delisting Journey (2023-2025)
- **Organizations:** FATF, GIABA, CBN, NFIU, EFCC, NIGSAC, CAC
- **Summary:** Grey-listed Q1 2023. IMF estimates 7.6% GDP capital inflow reduction. Completed all 19 action items. Achieved 37/40 C/LC ratings. Delisted October 24, 2025.
- **Source:** https://technext24.com/2025/10/25/nigeria-off-fatf-grey-list/
- **Teaching Points:** Concrete economic consequences; grey-listing as systemic risk; reforms that worked; ongoing obligations

## Professional Body Requirements

### CIBN (Chartered Institute of Bankers of Nigeria)
- **Requirement:** Professional certification and CPD
- **Mandatory Topics:** Banking Law, Regulation and Ethics; Corporate Governance and Professionalism; Bank Audit and Compliance
- **CPD Hours:** MCPD programme for all members; estimated 30 hours annually
- **Assessment:** ACIB examinations: Diploma (4 subjects), Intermediate Professional (4 core + 1 elective), Chartered Banker level

### ACAMS (Association of Certified Anti-Money Laundering Specialists)
- **Requirement:** CAMS certification
- **Topics:** ML/TF risks, programme design, compliance standards, investigations
- **CPD Hours:** Continuing education credits required; 40+ hours initial training
- **Assessment:** 120 MCQs, 3.5 hours

### ICA (International Compliance Association)
- **Requirement:** Tiered AML certification (Certificate to International Diploma)
- **Topics:** AML regulations, risk management, CDD, SAR, FATF standards
- **Assessment:** Tiered exams at Certificate, Advanced Certificate, and International Diploma levels

### CBN Regulatory Training Requirements
- **Requirement:** Mandatory AML/CFT training for all bank staff
- **Topics:** Legal requirements, CBN guidelines, suspicious activity identification, CDD, record keeping
- **Assessment:** No standardized CBN assessment; FIs must demonstrate coverage during examinations

## Recommended Module Structure

### Module 1: The Nigerian AML/CFT Framework: Why It Matters (5 lessons)
**Lessons:**
1. What Is Money Laundering? The Three Stages with Nigerian Examples
2. Nigeria's AML Legislative Framework -- The 2022 Trilogy
3. The Institutional Architecture -- CBN, NFIU, EFCC, SCUML, NDLEA and Their Roles
4. Nigeria's FATF Journey -- From Grey-Listing to Delisting (2023-2025)
5. The Cost of Non-Compliance -- N15B Fines, 4,173 BDC Revocations, and Personal Criminal Liability

**Learning Objectives:**
- Define money laundering and terrorism financing with Nigeria-specific typologies
- Identify key Nigerian AML legislation enacted in 2022
- Map roles of each Nigerian AML regulatory and enforcement body
- Explain why Nigeria was grey-listed and the 19-item action plan
- Calculate financial and reputational cost using real 2024 enforcement data

**Source Basis:** ML Act 2022, Terrorism Act 2022, POCA 2022, NFIU Act 2018, FATF MER, CBN AML/CFT Regulations 2022, CAMS Study Guide

### Module 2: Know Your Customer: CDD, KYC, and PEP Management (5 lessons)
**Lessons:**
1. CBN Tiered KYC Framework -- Tier 1, 2, 3 Requirements (Including BVN, NIN, Social Media)
2. Customer Due Diligence Under CBN CDD Regulations 2023
3. Beneficial Ownership Identification -- CAMA 2020, UBO Methodologies, PSC Register
4. Politically Exposed Persons -- CBN PEP Guidance Notes
5. Correspondent Banking and Cross-Border Transfer CDD (CBN IMTS Guidelines 2024)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply CBN's three-tiered KYC requirements correctly
- Conduct standard, simplified, and enhanced CDD
- Identify beneficial owners using CAMA 2020 PSC requirements
- Apply CBN PEP Guidance Notes to classify and monitor PEPs
- Implement CDD for correspondent banking and international transfers

**Source Basis:** CBN Three-Tiered KYC, CBN CDD Regulations 2023, CBN PEP Guidance 2022, CAMA 2020, CBN IMTS Guidelines 2024, FATF R.10/12/13

### Module 3: Suspicious Transaction Recognition, Reporting, and Record Keeping (5 lessons)
**Lessons:**
1. Red Flags and Suspicious Indicators -- Nigerian Typologies
2. STR Filing on the NFIU goAML Platform
3. Currency Transaction Reports and Other Mandatory Filings
4. Tipping-Off Prohibition and Safe Harbor Protections
5. Record Keeping Requirements -- 5-Year Retention and CBN Examination Readiness

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify suspicious indicators specific to Nigerian banking
- File a complete STR within regulatory timelines
- Prepare and file CTRs and mandatory reports
- Explain tipping-off prohibitions and legal protections
- Implement compliant record-keeping systems

**Source Basis:** NFIU December 2024 STR Guidelines, ML Act 2022, GIABA Typologies, National Risk Assessment 2022, CAMS Study Guide

### Module 4: Transaction Monitoring, Sanctions Screening, and the CBN 2026 Standards (5 lessons)
**Lessons:**
1. Risk-Based Transaction Monitoring -- From Manual to Automated
2. CBN 2026 Baseline Standards -- The 12 Standards and Implementation Roadmap
3. Sanctions and Watchlist Screening -- Domestic, UN, OFAC, Real-Time Matching
4. Alert Management, False Positive Tuning, and Case Management
5. The AML Compliance Programme -- Governance, CCO Role, Board Reporting, Independent Testing

**Learning Objectives:**
- Design a risk-based transaction monitoring framework
- Explain the 12 CBN Baseline Standards and 100 requirements
- Implement sanctions screening with fuzzy logic matching
- Manage alerts effectively including false positive documentation
- Structure an AML compliance programme meeting CBN requirements

**Source Basis:** CBN 2026 Baseline Standards, CBN AML/CFT Regulations 2022, FATF R.1, CAMS Study Guide

### Module 5: Emerging Threats, Enforcement, and the Future of AML Compliance (5 lessons)
**Lessons:**
1. Virtual Assets and Cryptocurrency AML -- ISA 2025, SEC VASP Rules, Binance Case
2. Cybercrime-Money Laundering Nexus -- Romance Scams, Yahoo Boys, PEP Exploitation
3. Trade-Based Money Laundering and Real Estate ML Typologies
4. EFCC Enforcement Landscape -- Prosecution Trends, Asset Forfeiture, Emefiele Case
5. Preparing for the Future -- AI in AML, RegTech, Career Development (CAMS, ICA, CIBN)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply AML requirements to VASPs under ISA 2025
- Identify red flags for cybercrime-enabled ML
- Recognize TBML and real estate ML schemes
- Analyze EFCC enforcement trends and institutional exposure
- Evaluate professional development pathways

**Source Basis:** ISA 2025, Binance case, EFCC data, Emefiele prosecution, GIABA typologies, ACAMS/ICA/CIBN requirements
