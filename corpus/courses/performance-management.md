## Corpus Summary

This corpus covers the complete performance management landscape for Nigerian organizations, anchored in the CBN Corporate Governance Guidelines 2023 (mandatory board evaluation with 360-degree peer review), PenCom Revised Corporate Governance Guidelines 2025 (board and staff performance standards for pension fund operators), the NCCG 2018 Principle 14 (board evaluation framework for all sectors), CAMA 2020 director duties and removal provisions (Sections 285, 288, 305), Nigerian Labour Act requirements for fair process in performance-related termination, and National Industrial Court jurisprudence establishing documentation and due-process obligations for PIPs and dismissals. The corpus includes 11 primary regulatory sources, 10 reference textbooks (including Armstrong 7th ed. 2022, Aguinis 5th ed. 2024, and Nigerian-authored HR texts), 8 existing course/training providers with detailed offerings, 19 Nigerian case studies spanning banking (Zenith Bank 4,165 promotions, Access Bank warrior culture, FirstBank Century 2 BSC implementation), fintech (Moniepoint 4M framework, Interswitch KPI-driven culture, Hello Tractor OKR adoption), telecoms (MTN IiP Platinum -- only Nigerian company), public sector (APER-to-PMS civil service transition, Edo State EdoBEST), and conglomerates (Dangote PM Framework), 28 key statistics, CIPM certification pathway details (5 levels, 24 subjects, HRPL licence with 60-credit CPD cycle), and a proposed 3-module/10-lesson structure aligned to CIPM competency areas. Credential partner: CIPM Nigeria.

## Primary Regulatory Sources

### 1. CBN Corporate Governance Guidelines for Banks (2023)
- **Full Title:** Corporate Governance Guidelines for Commercial, Merchant, Non-Interest, Payment Services Banks and Financial Holding Companies in Nigeria
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria, Financial Policy and Regulation Department (FPRD)
- **Date:** Issued 13 July 2023; Effective 1 August 2023
- **Key Provisions on Performance Management:**
  - Board evaluation must be conducted annually by an independent external consultant with expertise in corporate governance and performance management
  - Evaluation includes 360-degree peer review of individual Directors' performance and contributions, plus 360-degree peer review of Chairman's leadership
  - Banks must forward evaluation report to Director, FPRD, CBN, by 31 May following every financial year or before AGM (whichever earlier)
  - Poor director performance may lead to non-renewal of tenure; continuous unsatisfactory performance is basis for disciplinary action
  - MD/CEO/ED tenure capped at 12 years maximum; NEDs also 12-year maximum
  - Supersedes all previous codes including the 2014 Code
- **Reference:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/Out/2023/FPRD/Circular%20and%20Guidelines%20for%20Corporate%20Governance.pdf

### 2. CBN Fit and Proper Persons' Regime (Revised 2015)
- **Full Title:** Assessment Criteria for Approved Person's Regime for Financial Institutions
- **Issuing Body:** CBN, Financial Policy Regulation Department
- **Date:** October 2015 (revised)
- **Key Provisions:**
  - "Fitness tests" assess competence of candidates for board, top management, and critical operational positions
  - "Propriety tests" assess integrity and suitability of persons in key positions
  - Shareholders with 5%+ stake must attend CBN oral interviews and complete "Approved Persons Regime" questionnaire
  - Strictly enforced for all prospective and significant shareholders, directors, and senior management staff
- **Reference:** https://www.cbn.gov.ng/out/2015/fprd/revised%20fit%20and%20proper%20-%20combined-final%20oct%202015.pdf

### 3. Nigerian Code of Corporate Governance (NCCG) 2018
- **Full Title:** Nigerian Code of Corporate Governance 2018
- **Issuing Body:** Financial Reporting Council of Nigeria (FRCN)
- **Date:** 2018
- **Key Provisions (Principle 14 -- Board Evaluation):**
  - "Annual Board evaluation assesses how each Director, the Committees of the Board and the Board are committed to their roles, work together and continue to contribute effectively"
  - Board performance evaluation must be externally facilitated by independent consultant at least once every three years
  - Includes peer review surveys, Chairman leadership assessment, and individual Director interviews
  - Criteria and KPIs must be established for Board, committees, Chairman, and each Board member
  - Unsatisfactory director performance requires appropriate training to address gaps
  - Principle 16 establishes remuneration governance for board and senior management
  - Serves as base code for all sector-specific guidelines (CBN, PenCom, NAICOM, SEC)
- **Reference:** https://businessday.ng/columnist/article/nigerian-code-of-corporate-governance-2018-principle-14-board-evaluation/

### 4. PenCom Revised Guideline on Corporate Governance for Licensed Pension Fund Operators (2025)
- **Full Title:** Revised Guideline on Corporate Governance for Licensed Pension Fund Operators
- **Issuing Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **Date:** 22 September 2025
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Board evaluation requirements aligned with NCCG 2018 Principle 14
  - Staff appointment approval: under PRA 2014, CEO, Directors, and Management Staff cannot assume roles without prior written PenCom approval
  - Mandatory succession planning for critical positions
  - Staff competency requirements with ongoing professional development
  - Measurable objectives and policies promoting board diversity (gender, age, experience, competency)
  - Non-compliance attracts administrative penalties
- **Reference:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/Revised-Guideline-on-Corporate-Governance-for-LPFOs-22-September-2025.pdf

### 5. Companies and Allied Matters Act (CAMA) 2020
- **Full Title:** Companies and Allied Matters Act, 2020
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Government of Nigeria / National Assembly
- **Date:** 2020
- **Key Provisions on Director Performance:**
  - Section 285 (Rotation): All directors retire at first AGM; at subsequent years, one-third retire from office
  - Section 288 (Removal): Shareholders can remove a director by ordinary resolution with special notice (28 days). Removed directors disqualified from other company boards
  - Section 305 (Fiduciary Duties): Directors owe fiduciary relationship to the company; must observe utmost good faith and act in best interests of the company as a whole
  - Board performance evaluation expected regularly with assessment of overall performance, individual directors, and Chairman
  - Director attendance record is a factor for re-election consideration
  - Chairman of public company prohibited from also acting as CEO
- **Reference:** https://www.cac.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2020/12/CAMA-NOTE-BOOK-FULL-VERSION.pdf

### 6. Labour Act, Cap L1 LFN 2004
- **Full Title:** Labour Act, Cap L1, Laws of the Federation of Nigeria 2004
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Government of Nigeria
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Principal legislation overseeing employment of workers in Nigeria
  - Employer must give justifiable reason connected with employee's work performance before termination
  - Notice requirements: reasonable notice or payment in lieu (unless gross misconduct justifies summary dismissal)
  - Employees must be advised of KPIs for their role with regular appraisals conducted
  - Documentation obligations: detailed records of performance reviews, warnings, and disciplinary actions required
  - Termination letters must state a valid, lawful reason
- **Reference:** https://omaplex.com.ng/termination-of-employment-in-nigeria-an-overview-of-the-labour-law/

### 7. National Industrial Court Jurisprudence on Performance-Related Termination
- **Issuing Body:** National Industrial Court of Nigeria (NICN), under National Industrial Court Act, 2006
- **Key Principles Established:**
  - Fair process required: employees must receive query, "seat-up" letter, or warning before termination for poor performance
  - PIP must state specific performance deficiencies, set clear expectations, and give reasonable timeline
  - Employee must sign acknowledgement confirming understanding of PIP
  - Failure to follow fair procedure renders dismissal procedurally unfair and potentially wrongful even with valid grounds
  - Remedies: general damages, exemplary damages (S.19 NICN Act), outstanding entitlements, or reinstatement
  - **Linda Jokanola v. Nigeria LNG Ltd** (NICN/LA/261/2013): N10M damages for wrongful termination violating constitutional rights
  - **NSPM Employee Case**: N96M damages for unconstitutional termination at age 57
- **Reference:** https://www.nicnadr.gov.ng/judgment/

### 8. SEC Code of Corporate Governance for Public Companies
- **Full Title:** Code of Corporate Governance for Public Companies in Nigeria
- **Issuing Body:** Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
- **Key Provisions on Performance Evaluation:**
  - Board must ensure evaluation of individual and collective performance of board and committees
  - Chairman oversees annual evaluation of CEO/MD performance
  - CEO/MD performs annual evaluation of Executive Directors based on agreed criteria/performance indicators
  - External facilitation by independent consultant required at least once every three years
  - Summary evaluation report must be included in annual report and investors' portal
  - Board minimum 5 members; no more than 2 members of same family on board
- **Reference:** https://www.sec.gov.ng/files/CODE%20OF%20CORPORATE%20GOVERNANCE%20FOR%20PUBLIC%20COMPANIES.pdf

### 9. NAICOM Corporate Governance Guidelines (CGGIRC 2021)
- **Full Title:** Corporate Governance Guidelines for Insurance and Reinsurance Companies in Nigeria
- **Issuing Body:** National Insurance Commission (NAICOM)
- **Date:** Issued 17 March 2021; Effective 1 June 2021
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Mandatory board training at least once annually with evaluation reported to NAICOM before end of Q1 of succeeding year
  - Each board member must attend minimum 75% of board meetings annually
  - Board: 7-15 members; no more than 40% in executive capacity; at least one Independent NED
  - Six mandatory committees including Nomination and Governance, Remuneration
  - Executive Director Technical required on each board, with same qualifications as MD, serving as succession line
  - Emphasis on transparency and fairness in HR policies, merit-based promotions, performance-based advancement
- **Reference:** https://www.corpgovnigeria.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/NAICOM-CORPORATE-GOVERNANCE-GUIDELINES.pdf

### 10. CIPM Act (Cap I15, LFN 2004) and National HR Competency Framework
- **Full Title:** Chartered Institute of Personnel Management of Nigeria Act (Act 58 of 1992, codified as Cap I15 LFN 2004)
- **Issuing Body:** Federal Government of Nigeria / National Assembly
- **Key Provisions:**
  - CIPM is the sole regulatory body for Human Resource Management in Nigeria
  - Mandated to determine standards of knowledge and skills for persons seeking registration as HR professionals
  - Required to develop and maintain high standards of professional competence
  - 2026 Development: Mandatory professional certification for officers in HR roles within MDAs (circular from Head of Civil Service, 14 May 2026), with 12-month moratorium for compliance
  - National HR Competency Framework introduced with CIPM accreditation
- **Reference:** https://cipmnigeria.org/about-us/who-we-are/

### 11. CBN Competency Framework for the Banking Industry
- **Issuing Body:** Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Developed as part of banking sector reforms to redirect industry toward sequenced competency development
  - Establishes standard competency requirements for each job role as guide for talent recruitment and development
  - Defines knowledge, skills, and behaviour attributes for effective performance in each role
  - Ensures practitioners continually update knowledge and skills in line with role requirements
  - Research confirms adoption by top Nigerian banks with positive impact on bank performance
- **Reference:** https://cibng.org/introduction-cf

## Nigerian Case Studies

### 1. Zenith Bank: 4,165 Promotions and Performance-Linked Restructuring (2025)
- **Organization:** Zenith Bank Plc (8,000+ employees)
- **Industry:** Banking / Financial Services
- **Summary:** Executed comprehensive performance-based workforce restructuring in January 2025 with three simultaneous components: (a) 4,165 employees promoted (~50% of workforce) between January 13-17, 2025, based on "demonstrated exceptional performance" -- over a two-year period under CEO Dr. Adaora Umeoji, 80% of total staff have been promoted; (b) salary increases of 20-30% across the board, with lower-level staff receiving proportionally larger increases (first adjustment in two years); (c) 66 senior staff exits based on age requirements and failure to meet internal performance standards
- **PM System:** Uses formal performance appraisal including 360-degree feedback, Management by Objectives (MBO), and structured performance reviews. KPIs include task completion rates, error reduction, goal achievement, customer satisfaction, and loan disbursement efficiency. Adopted "balanced scorecard philosophy" extending beyond financial metrics to include ESG and sustainability. Africa's first bank to publish a standalone GRI Standards sustainability report (2016)
- **Results:** 2024 pre-tax profit N1.3 trillion (67% YoY increase) -- first Nigerian bank to exceed N1 trillion PAT. First to cross N5 trillion market cap (April 2026). 80% of employees receiving structured reviews showed 10-15% productivity improvement. However, 40% cited favouritism and 30% reported unclear evaluation criteria
- **Teaching Points:** Performance as dual-edged tool (reward and exit); mass promotion as engagement strategy; BSC philosophy in banking; tension between merit and favouritism
- **Sources:** https://punchng.com/zenith-bank-promotes-over-4000-employees-more-expected/ | https://www.academia.edu/129641818/

### 2. FirstBank Nigeria: Century 2 Enterprise Transformation and Senior Restructuring
- **Organization:** First Bank of Nigeria Plc (Nigeria's oldest bank, founded 1894)
- **Industry:** Banking / Financial Services
- **Summary:** Two-phase transformation story. Phase 1 (1996 onward): Launched "Century 2" Enterprise Transformation Project (partnered with Accenture), anchored on four strategic themes -- growth, service excellence, talent management, and performance management. Bisi Onasanya (later CEO 2009-2015) built PM function from scratch as Group Head of Finance and Performance Management. Implemented BSC across all four perspectives at individual, departmental, and corporate levels. Operates FirstAcademy and Leadership Acceleration Programme (LAP) using job shadowing, coaching, mentoring, succession planning. Phase 2 (2024-2025): Exited ~100 senior executives under Chairman Femi Otedola for repositioning. Additional March 2026 terminations targeting long-serving contract staff
- **Results:** Academic study (222 staff): all four BSC perspectives positively impacted organizational performance. Separate study (141 employees): PA components predicted 72.7% of variance in organizational commitment (R-squared 0.727). Customer accounts grew from 10M (2015) to 36M+. Ranked No. 1 in Nigeria by The Banker Magazine
- **Teaching Points:** Long-term PM transformation journey; BSC implementation across organizational levels; academy-based talent development; performance management at senior levels
- **Sources:** https://www.researchgate.net/publication/258126901 | https://nairametrics.com/2024/12/30/first-bank-exits-100-senior-staff-in-major-reorganizational-shakeup/ | http://gojehms.com/index.php/GOJEHMS/article/view/35

### 3. Access Bank: Warrior Culture and Results-Driven Performance (2002-present)
- **Organization:** Access Bank Plc / Access Holdings
- **Industry:** Banking / Financial Services
- **Summary:** Under Aigboje Aig-Imoukhuede (MD/CEO) and Herbert Wigwe (Deputy MD), introduced rolling five-year corporate plan strategy from 2002. Built aggressive, results-driven "Warrior" culture that filters for high-agency individuals thriving under pressure. Other Nigerian companies actively recruit Access Bank alumni. PM relies on strategic retreats for goal recalibration and accountability, with visible leadership engagement at all levels. KPIs tracked: deposit/loan portfolio expansion, cost-to-income ratio, Capital Adequacy Ratio, share price appreciation, market cap, dividend payouts
- **Results:** Rose from 114th largest Nigerian bank to 1st position over ~20 years. First year under new leadership: balance sheet grew 100%, posted N1B PBT (exceeding cumulative profits of previous 12 years). Customer base grew from 90,000 (2002) to 60M+. 2024 gross earnings N4.8 trillion (86% YoY), PBT of N893.7B. Completed 20+ acquisitions (2002-2025)
- **Teaching Points:** Performance culture as competitive advantage; strategy-led PM; leadership accountability; growth through aggressive KPIs
- **Sources:** https://medium.com/@drola/a-legacy-of-leadership-how-access-bank-redefined-banking-in-nigeria-fabb8f83bf71 | https://www.thecable.ng/from-nigeria-to-africa-the-incredible-journey-of-aig-imoukhuede-and-herbert-wigwe-in-growing-access-bank/

### 4. Moniepoint: 4M Culture Framework and Scaling Performance (2022-2026)

### 5. MTN Nigeria: 6Bs Framework and IiP Platinum (Only Nigerian Company)
- **Organization:** MTN Nigeria (largest telecoms operator)
- **Industry:** Telecommunications
- **Summary:** Achieved Investors in People (IiP) Platinum certification in November 2019 -- highest level, held by only 2% globally. First and only Nigerian company to achieve this. Implements 6Bs framework: Building (training), Buying (recruitment), Borrowing (partnerships), Binding (retention), Boosting (leadership development), Bouncing (performance management). Partnered with FEES Limited for BI and Analytics-enhanced PM: customized dashboards/scorecards integrated with data warehouse, SAP BusinessObjects for reporting and KPI tracking. Employee Value Proposition: Work with meaning, Grow with purpose, Thrive on positivity, Connected to develop. Uses 70:20:10 learning philosophy

### 8. Federal Civil Service: APER to PMS Transition (2020-present)
- **Organization:** Federal Civil Service of Nigeria (via OHCSF)
- **Industry:** Government / Public Sector
- **Summary:** Formally scrapped Annual Performance Evaluation Report (APER) in November 2020, replacing with Performance Management System (PMS). APER -- inherited from British colonial administration, adopted via 1974 Udoji Report -- condemned as "unreliable as a means of assessment" by 2004 Public Service Review Commission. Known problems: subjectivity (trait-based not output-based), inflated assessments ("no one scores less than 95%"), favouritism/nepotism, paper-based, annual-only, no KPIs, no reward linkage. New PMS features: quarterly appraisals, digital platform, defined KPIs, competency-based assessments, performance contracts, 360-degree peer reviews, BSC methodology. FCSSIP25 houses PMS reform as one of six priority pillars. President Tinubu (2023) required all ministers to sign performance bonds with KPIs, reviewed quarterly
- **Results:** 71.1% of surveyed federal employees willing to adopt PMS. IPPIS eliminated ~70,000 ghost workers, saving N220 billion. Implementation remains uneven due to bureaucratic rigidity, patronage culture, ICT gaps, insufficient funding
- **Teaching Points:** Failure of subjective systems; grade inflation as PM risk; transitioning from colonial-era to modern PM; ministerial performance bonds; digital transformation of public sector PM

| Level | Subjects | PM-Relevant Content | Exam Fee |

| Professional I (5 papers) | HR Metrics & Analytics; Org Behaviour & Performance; Insurance/Pensions/Risk; Advanced HRM I; Presentation Skills | AHRM I: dedicated PM module; HRMA: performance metrics; OBP: motivation, MBO | ~N63,450 |
| Professional II (4 papers) | Nigerian Labour Law; Employment Relations; Advanced HRM II; Labour Market Analysis | AHRM II: Advanced PM (BSC, goal setting, automation). No exemptions | N70,050 |

**Total estimated cost:** N500,000-600,000 including registration, subscriptions, study packs, and tech fees

**Membership Grades (ascending):**
- Student Member -- Currently in CIPM examinations
- Associate Member (ACIPM) -- Completed all exams + 2-day mandatory induction (N160,000)
- Full Member (MCIPM) -- ACIPM + 6 years minimum + CPD + upgrade assessment
- Fellow (FCIPM) -- MCIPM + 7 years minimum + CPD + upgrade assessment (highest)

**Six Entry Routes:** Examination, HR Practitioners' (7+ years experience), Executive (12+ years), Academic, Military/Paramilitary, Corporate

**HRPL (HR Practitioners' Licence) -- CPD Mechanism:**
- Valid for 3 years
- Renewal requires minimum 60 credit points accumulated during validity period
- Credits earned through HR work activities, training programmes, and professional contributions
- Renewal fee: N15,000
- Two renewal pathways: HR Work Activities Route or Competency Assessment Route (online)
- Mandatory for membership upgrade and CIPM-to-CIPD conversion

**Performance Management as Core Competency:** Performance Management System is one of 9 recognized core HR competency areas in CIPM's framework:
1. Human Resource Management
2. Resourcing and Talent Planning
3. **Performance Management System**
4. Rewards and Remuneration Management
5. Learning and Talent Development
6. Change Management
7. Industrial and Employee Relations
8. Employee Engagement
9. Diversity and Inclusiveness

**CIPM-CIPD Mutual Recognition Agreement** (signed 17 September 2024):
- ACIPM maps to CIPD Associate Member
- MCIPM maps to CIPD Chartered Member
- FCIPM maps to CIPD Chartered Fellow
- Conversion via 60-minute professional discussion + valid HRPL + financial status confirmed

**Annual Subscription (2025):**
- Student: N35,000 + N10,000 tech fee
- Associate: N60,000 + N10,000 tech fee
- Member: N75,000 + N10,000 tech fee
- Fellow: N90,000 + N10,000 tech fee

## Recommended Module Structure

### Module 1: Foundations of Performance Management in Nigeria (3 lessons)
**Learning Outcomes:** Understand why PM matters in Nigerian organizations; distinguish PM from performance appraisal; navigate the regulatory landscape; build the business case for modern PM

**Lesson 1: Why Performance Management Matters for Nigerian Organizations**
- Hook: Zenith Bank 4,165 promotions vs. CBN 1,000-employee restructuring -- same tool, different outcomes
- PM definition and evolution (from APER to modern PM)
- Business case: turnover costs (12.5% to 28.1% in banking), engagement crisis (20% Sub-Saharan Africa)
- Nigerian regulatory drivers: CBN Corporate Governance, NCCG 2018, CAMA 2020
- CIPM's role as apex HR body and PM as core competency
- Assessment: Scenario -- identify PM vs. PA in workplace situations

**Lesson 2: The Regulatory Framework -- What the Law Requires**
- CBN Corporate Governance Guidelines 2023: board evaluation, 360-degree review, external consultants
- NCCG 2018 Principle 14: board evaluation requirements, external facilitation, KPI setting
- PenCom guidelines: pension operator staff performance and governance
- NAICOM guidelines: insurance sector board training and attendance requirements
- SEC Code: CEO/MD evaluation, disclosure requirements
- CAMA 2020: director duties (S.305), removal (S.288), fiduciary obligations
- Assessment: Match regulatory requirements to responsible bodies

**Lesson 3: Performance Management vs. Performance Appraisal -- Getting the Distinction Right**
- Continuous cycle vs. annual event
- The APER cautionary tale: grade inflation, subjectivity, lack of accountability
- Armstrong's performance management cycle (plan, act, track, review)
- From subjective to objective evaluation: the Nigerian transition imperative
- Assessment: Diagnose a case study organization's PM maturity level

### Module 2: Designing and Implementing Performance Management Systems (4 lessons)
**Learning Outcomes:** Set SMART objectives and cascade KPIs; select and implement appropriate PM frameworks; build competency frameworks; design effective feedback and review processes

**Lesson 4: Goal Setting, KPI Cascading, and Alignment**
- Strategic planning to individual objectives: the cascade process
- SMART goals in Nigerian organizational context
- KPIs vs. KRAs: definitions and practical examples (banking, fintech, pension)
- The Balanced Scorecard four perspectives adapted for Nigerian organizations
- OKRs as alternative framework: John Doerr's model and ONE Campaign Africa case
- Assessment: Cascade organizational strategy into department and individual KPIs for a Nigerian bank scenario

**Lesson 5: Competency Frameworks -- Building the Foundation**
- What competency frameworks are and why Nigerian regulators require them
- CBN/CIBN competency framework for banking: structure and application
- Building frameworks: knowledge, skills, behaviors; proficiency levels; assessment criteria
- CIPM 9 core HR competency areas as meta-framework
- Integrating technical and behavioral competencies
- Assessment: Design a competency framework for a role in a Nigerian fintech

**Lesson 6: Continuous Feedback and Performance Conversations**
- Moving beyond annual reviews to continuous feedback
- The manager's role: coaching vs. evaluating
- Structuring performance conversations: SBI model (Situation, Behavior, Impact)
- 360-degree feedback: CBN requirements and practical implementation
- Cross-cultural feedback in Nigeria: navigating hierarchy, seniority, and cultural diversity
- Technology enablers: SeamlessHR, performance management software landscape in Nigeria
- Assessment: Role-play a performance feedback conversation using the SBI model

**Lesson 7: Calibration Sessions and Performance Ratings**
- Purpose and process of calibration: ensuring fairness and consistency
- Bell curve and forced ranking: pros, cons, and when to use
- Managing rater bias: halo effect, recency bias, leniency bias
- Addressing the Nigerian APER syndrome: grade inflation and how to prevent it
- Calibration meeting facilitation: practical guide for Nigerian managers
- Assessment: Facilitate a mock calibration session with conflicting ratings

### Module 3: Performance Outcomes -- Rewards, Development, and Improvement (3 lessons)
**Learning Outcomes:** Link performance to compensation and promotion; design legally compliant PIPs; manage underperformance within Nigerian legal frameworks; build a performance-driven organizational culture

**Lesson 8: Linking Performance to Reward and Recognition**
- Performance-related pay: Nigerian banking sector practices
- Total reward components: base salary, variable pay, bonuses, 13th month, benefits
- Designing performance-based promotion criteria: moving beyond "godfatherism"
- Zenith Bank case study: mass promotion as engagement strategy
- Stanbic IBTC case study: appraisal rating linked to guaranteed promotion
- Non-financial recognition: impact on engagement and retention
- Assessment: Design a performance-linked reward matrix for a Nigerian organization

**Lesson 9: Performance Improvement Plans and Managing Underperformance**
- When performance falls short: early intervention strategies
- PIP structure under Nigerian labour law: legal requirements from NICN jurisprudence
- Documentation requirements: query letters, warning letters, PIP documents
- Timeline and escalation: reasonable periods, progressive discipline
- Employee rights: fair hearing, representation, right to challenge
- Exit management: legally defensible termination for poor performance
- Linda Jokanola v. NLNG case study: N10M cost of getting it wrong
- Assessment: Draft a PIP for a scenario with documentation checklist

**Lesson 10: Building a Performance-Driven Culture**
- From compliance to culture: embedding PM in organizational DNA
- MTN Nigeria 6Bs framework: building, buying, borrowing, binding, boosting, bouncing talent
- Digital transformation of PM: HRIS adoption (68% of Nigerian organizations), analytics, AI
- Change management for PM transformation: FirstBank and CBN restructuring lessons
- The future of PM in Nigeria: continuous feedback, agile PM, Gen Z workforce expectations
- CIPM CPD pathway: continuing professional development through HRPL
- Assessment: Develop a 90-day PM transformation roadmap for a Nigerian organization
