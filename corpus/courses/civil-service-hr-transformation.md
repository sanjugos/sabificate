## 1. Primary Regulatory Sources

### 1.1 Public Service Rules (PSR)
- **Current Edition:** Revised 2008; new edition unveiled July 2023 by the Office of the Head of Civil Service of the Federation (OHCSF)
- **Publisher:** Federal Government of Nigeria / OHCSF
- **Scope:** Comprehensive rules governing appointments, promotions, discipline, leave, retirement, and conditions of service for all federal civil servants
- **Key Provisions for HR Transformation:**
  - Chapter 2: Appointments (qualifications, federal character, probation)
  - Chapter 4: Promotions (criteria, examination, seniority-merit balance)
  - Chapter 5: Discipline (misconduct categories, due process, appeals)
  - Chapter 12: Training (study leave, bonding, mandatory development)
- **Download:** https://clients.rvomedia.com/ohcsf1/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/PUBLIC-SERVICE-RULES.pdf
- **Teaching Relevance:** Foundation document for any HR framework redesign; must be reconciled with competency-based approaches
- **Source:** Federation Civil Service Commission (https://fedcivilservice.gov.ng/resource-public_service_rules)

### 1.2 Civil Service Handbook
- **Current Edition:** Updated periodically by FCSC
- **Scope:** Procedural guide complementing the PSR; covers departmental structures, filing systems, correspondence protocols, and administrative practice
- **Key Content:** Organizational hierarchies (GL 01-17), cadre structures, posting procedures, retirement processes
- **Publisher:** Federation Civil Service Commission
- **Source:** https://fedcivilservice.gov.ng/resource-civil_service_handbook

### 1.3 Bureau of Public Procurement (BPP) Act 2007
- **Citation:** Public Procurement Act 2007, No. 14
- **Regulatory Body:** Bureau of Public Procurement (BPP)
- **Key Compliance Requirements for HR:**
  - All procurement above threshold must follow open competitive bidding
  - Accounting Officers in each MDA are responsible for legal and regulatory compliance
  - Conflict of interest provisions bar procurement organizers from bidding
  - All contract values must be stated in Naira (or CBN exchange rate conversion)
  - Government contracts must contain arbitration clauses
  - P-COMS (Procurement Compliance Monitoring Services) enables real-time monitoring of procurement activities
- **Digital Platform:** MDAs submit procurement plans, initiate procurement processes, and request No-Objection approvals through P-COMS (https://pcoms.bpp.gov.ng/)
- **HR Relevance:** Governs procurement of consulting services, training programs, and HR technology platforms; any outsourced HR function (recruitment testing, payroll systems, training delivery) must comply
- **Source:** https://www.bpp.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/Public-Procurement-Act-2007pdf.pdf

### 1.4 Integrated Payroll and Personnel Information System (IPPIS)
- **Launched:** Pilot April 2007 (7 MDAs); managed by Office of the Accountant General of the Federation (OAGF)
- **Initial Funding:** World Bank facility of ~USD 4.9 million
- **Current Coverage:** 1,121,031 public servants enrolled (as of July 2020); 359 MDAs
- **Impact on Ghost Workers:** Eliminated 60,450 ghost workers, saving N185 billion (by December 2014); more recent BVN integration uncovered 45,000 additional ghost workers
- **Total Savings:** Over $1 billion saved through ghost worker elimination and payroll rationalization
- **HR Transformation Role:**
  - Centralized payroll and personnel data across all federal MDAs
  - Biometric verification (BVN integration) to validate workforce identity
  - Personnel records digitization enabling workforce analytics
  - Resistance from some agencies (notably universities and ASUU) over autonomy concerns
- **Teaching Points:** IPPIS is both a technological solution and a change management challenge; illustrates tension between centralized control and institutional autonomy
- **Sources:** Academia.edu IPPIS research papers; ResearchGate publications; publicservice.wangonet.org

### 1.6 SERVICOM (Service Compact with All Nigerians)
- **Established:** 2004, outcome of Presidential Retreat on Service Delivery under President Obasanjo
- **Influenced By:** UK DFID study identifying systemic inefficiencies in government service delivery
- **Structure:**
  - Presidential Steering Committee (PSC) provides policy direction
  - Each MDA has a SERVICOM Unit headed by a Nodal Officer
  - Service charters outline commitments, delivery standards, and feedback mechanisms
- **Performance Framework:** Charter-based approach where each MDA outlines service commitments; these form the basis for performance measurement
- **Recent Activity:** Inauguration of Parastatal SERVICOM Units/Committees (PSU/PSC) for NAQS and CPN; repositioning under acting National Coordinator with focus on citizen-centric reforms at NRS and Federal Ministry of Livestock Development
- **HR Relevance:** SERVICOM charters create measurable service standards that HR performance management systems must align with; nodal officers need HR training
- **Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SERVICOM_(Nigeria); https://fmino.gov.ng/servicom-repositions-for-excellence/

### 1.7 Treasury Single Account (TSA)
- **Introduction:** Policy introduced 2012; full implementation mandated 2015 by President Buhari
- **Mechanism:** Consolidation of all government revenues into a single account at the Central Bank of Nigeria
- **Impact on MDAs:**
  - Positive: Enhanced financial accountability, transparency, and revenue monitoring; reduced corruption and leakages
  - Challenges: Delayed fund disbursement, reduced financial autonomy for agencies, bureaucratic inefficiencies, inadequate ICT infrastructure
- **HR Relevance:** TSA eliminated agencies' ability to maintain independent revenue pools that sometimes funded off-book staff positions; forced alignment of HR budgets with centralized fiscal planning
- **Source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treasury_single_account; ResearchGate publications

### 1.8 Federal Civil Service Strategy and Implementation Plan 2021-2025 (FCSSIP25)
- **Approved:** Federal Executive Council (FEC), December 2021
- **Publisher:** Office of the Head of Civil Service of the Federation
- **Vision:** Transform the federal civil service into a world-class institution that is efficient and digitally empowered
- **Six Pillars:** Including Staff Welfare and Enhanced Value Proposition of Civil Servants
- **Key Achievement:** As of December 30, 2025, the Federal Civil Service completed implementation of presidential directives to operate an entirely paperless system
- **Digital Milestones:**
  - Migration to 1Gov Cloud Enterprise Content Management System
  - Electronic processing of all official correspondence
  - Workflow automation across all MDAs
- **HR Relevance:** The strategic framework provides the policy context for all HR transformation; Module 1 of the course should anchor in FCSSIP25 as the overarching reform mandate
- **Sources:** https://seamlesshr.com/blog/fcssip25-nigerias-federal-civil-service-strategy-explained/; https://ohcsf.gov.ng/

### 1.9 Personnel Audit and Skills Gap Analysis (PASGA) / Project Phoenix
- **Launched:** October 2025 by Office of the Head of Civil Service of the Federation
- **Lead Consultant:** Phillips Consulting Limited (with 15 cluster consulting firms)
- **Technology Partner:** MuchSkills (Swedish skills intelligence platform)
- **Implementation Period:** October 20 -- November 14, 2025 (verification phase)
- **Scope:** Comprehensive workforce profile across all federal MDAs covering ICT, project management, public finance, data analysis, and other critical skill areas
- **Workforce Covered:** ~70,000 core federal civil servants; broader public sector of ~1 million
- **Enforcement:** Salaries of civil servants who fail to complete verification and skills assessment suspended from March 2026
- **Expected Outcomes:** Guide future decisions on recruitment, training, deployment, and career progression; eliminate redundancies; enable strategic redeployment
- **HR Relevance:** PASGA represents the most comprehensive competency mapping exercise in Nigerian civil service history; directly feeds into competency-based recruitment and digital fluency assessment modules
- **Sources:** https://phillipsconsulting.net/articles_post/personnel-audit-and-skills-gap-analysis-pasga-initiative/; https://www.newswire.com/news/swedish-startup-muchskills-powers-phillips-consulting-22684613

### 1.11 Pension Reform Act 2004 (as amended 2014)
- **Regulatory Body:** National Pension Commission (PenCom)
- **System:** Contributory Pension Scheme (CPS) replacing unfunded Defined Benefits Scheme
- **Coverage:** Over 10 million Nigerians (civil servants, private sector, artisans, self-employed)
- **Recent Reforms (Pension Revolution 2.0):**
  - Reintroduction of gratuity for civil servants
  - N758 billion Federal Government bond to offset pension arrears
  - Free health insurance for low-income retirees
- **HR Relevance:** Pension administration is a core HR function affected by transformation; CPS compliance is mandatory for all MDAs; pension data integration with IPPIS is an ongoing challenge
- **Source:** https://www.pencom.gov.ng/pension-reform-act/

### 1.12 Bureau of Public Service Reforms (BPSR)
- **Established:** February 4, 2004
- **Mandate:** Coordinate, manage, and integrate reform programs across the public service
- **Governance Structure:** National Council on Reforms, Steering Committee on Reforms, and the Bureau itself
- **10-Year Strategy -- Four Pillars:**
  1. Improving the Governance and Institutional Environment
  2. Improving the Social-Economic Environment
  3. Improving Public Finance Management
  4. Improving Civil Service Administration
- **Reform Track Record:** FG initiated 43 reforms in civil service over a 5-year period
- **HR Relevance:** BPSR is the institutional home for civil service reform policy; any HR transformation must align with BPSR's strategic framework
- **Source:** https://bpsr.gov.ng/

### 3.2 CIPM (Chartered Institute of Personnel Management of Nigeria)

**Regulatory Status:**
- Confirmed by Federal Government (May 2026) as Nigeria's only chartered HR regulatory body
- OHCSF circular mandates CIPM certification for officers occupying HR roles in MDAs
- 12-month moratorium granted to affected officers to obtain requisite certification; after which only certified professionals eligible for deployment into HR roles

**Examination Structure (5 Stages):**
1. **Foundation:** Entry-level HR fundamentals
2. **Intermediate I:** Business Administration, Advanced Economics, Accounting & Finance for Managers, Business Statistics & Social Research Methodology
3. **Intermediate II:** Training and Development, Public Administration, Manpower Planning, Comparative Management & Administration
4. **Professional I:** Operational Research, Organisation Behaviour & Performance
5. **Professional II:** Nigerian Labour Law, Advanced Industrial Relations

**Examination Administration:** Online exams 4 times per year

**HR Transformation Relevance:**
- CIPM certification is now mandatory for public sector HR professionals -- this creates both a compliance requirement and a learning pathway
- The Public Administration module in Intermediate II directly addresses civil service context
- Course should prepare learners to understand CIPM framework even if not seeking full certification

## 4. Nigerian Case Studies

### 4.1 NNPC Corporatization under PIA (2021-Present)

**Background:**
- Petroleum Industry Act (PIA) signed August 16, 2021 by President Buhari
- Section 53(1) mandated incorporation of NNPC Limited under CAMA within 6 months
- Transition from government-owned parastatal to commercially driven, profit-focused limited liability company
- In-house transition committee supported by McKinsey, KPMG, PWC, Wood Mackenzie, and Olaniwun Ajayi LP

**HR Transformation Dimensions:**
- Staff mindset shift from civil service culture to commercial performance orientation
- Self-funding requirement -- no more government subventions
- New corporate governance framework: Board of Directors, Audit Committee, Finance and Investment Committee, Governance and Remuneration Committee
- EVP Corporate Services oversees HR, legal, and administration
- Leadership transition: Mele Kyari replaced by Bayo Ojulari (appointed April 2025)

**Recent Executive Orders (2024-2025):**
- Targeted tax incentives for gas and oil projects
- Streamlined contracting processes to reduce timelines and costs
- Performance-based incentives to reward cost savings in upstream operations

**Proposed PIA Amendments (2025):**
- Restructuring NNPC Limited's ownership
- Ministry of Finance Incorporated (MOFI) to set strategic direction
- NUPRC potentially replacing NNPC as concessionaire

**Teaching Points:**
- Legislative mandate creates urgency but doesn't guarantee cultural change
- World-class consulting support does not substitute for internal change champions
- Dual governance (PIA + CAMA) creates compliance complexity
- "A paradox of reform and stagnation" -- structural changes outpacing cultural transformation
- **Gbitse SME Input Needed:** Real consulting experience with NNPC HR challenges

### 4.2 FIRS to NRS Institutional Transformation (2025-2026)

**Background:**
- Four landmark laws signed June 26, 2025: Nigeria Tax Bill, Nigeria Tax Administration Bill, NRS Establishment Act 2025, Joint Revenue Board Establishment Bill
- FIRS formally rebranded as Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS) December 31, 2025
- Chairman: Zacch Adedeji

**Institutional Changes:**
- Expanded responsibilities to include petroleum royalties and mineral revenues
- "One-Stop-Shop" approach eliminating multiple taxations
- Digital tools: National e-Invoicing system, *829# USSD service
- Unified, technology-driven authority

**Revenue Performance Demonstrating Transformation:**
- 2024: N21.66 trillion (111.6% of target); non-oil taxes exceeded projections by 28%
- 2025: N28.3 trillion (against N25.2 trillion target)
- 2026 target: N40.7 trillion

**Teaching Points:**
- Demonstrates that institutional renaming must be accompanied by genuine structural transformation
- Revenue performance improvements provide measurable transformation KPIs
- Digital modernization (e-invoicing, USSD) as enablers of institutional change
- **Gbitse SME Input Needed:** Direct experience with NRS consulting engagement; organizational design insights

### 4.3 CBN Restructuring (2024-Present)

**Background:**
- Governor Olayemi Cardoso initiated restructuring focused on core mandate alignment
- CBN named Global Central Bank of the Year (March 2026)

**HR Restructuring:**
- December 2024: Voluntary early exit package for long-serving staff
- Plans to retire ~1,000 employees with N50 billion payout
- Internal restructuring: staff reduction, misconduct addressing, redeployment to areas of need
- Establishment of dedicated compliance function
- Deployment of advanced analytics tools for policy formulation

**Broader Reform Context:**
- Aggressive monetary tightening: interest rates from 18.75% (2023) to 27.5% (late 2024)
- Improved communication and stakeholder engagement to rebuild trust
- Governance, transparency, and accountability prioritized

**Controversy:**
- The early exit program generated regional outcry and public debate
- "From Policy Shift to Regional Outcry" -- illustrates change resistance dynamics

**Teaching Points:**
- Voluntary exit programs as HR transformation tool -- benefits and pitfalls
- Compliance function establishment as governance reform indicator
- Communication strategy critical when restructuring affects livelihoods
- **Gbitse SME Input Needed:** CBN consulting experience; organizational change dynamics

### 4.4 PENCOM Pension Reform

**Pre-Reform State:** Unfunded Defined Benefits Scheme; budgetary allocation for pension among most vulnerable budget items; delays and accumulated arrears

**Reform Impact:**
- Contributory Pension Scheme (CPS) covering 10+ million Nigerians
- Pension Revolution 2.0 -- "Stronger Pensions, Stronger Nigeria"
- Reintroduction of gratuity for civil servants
- N758 billion Federal Government bond for pension arrears
- Free health insurance for low-income retirees

**Teaching Points:**
- Pension reform as driver of HR modernization -- forced digitization of personnel records
- Transition from defined benefits to contributory scheme as culture change case study
- Government bond solution to legacy obligations demonstrates fiscal creativity

### 4.5 BPE Privatization -- Successes and Failures

**Track Record:**
- Over 149 transactions completed from 2000 to present
- Sectors: telecommunications, pensions, seaports, solid minerals, power sector

**Successes:**
- Power sector: Unbundling and privatization of PHCN successor companies
- Telecommunications liberalization (enabling MTN, Glo, Airtel entry)
- Seaport concessions improving efficiency

**Failures:**
- Ajaokuta Steel -- controversial transaction, incomplete privatization
- ALSCON (Aluminium Smelting Company) -- disputed outcomes
- NITEL -- failed privatization of national telecoms
- Nigeria Airways -- liquidation rather than successful privatization

**Current Status:**
- Pushing for new Public Enterprises Reform Bill to modernize legal framework
- Labor concerns, institutional overlaps, and public skepticism remain challenges

**Teaching Points:**
- Privatization creates massive HR dislocation -- downsizing, culture shock, skills mismatch
- Failed privatizations often trace to inadequate HR transition planning
- Successful privatizations (telecoms) show what's possible with proper institutional design

### 4.6 Kaduna State Civil Service Reform (El-Rufai Administration)

**What Happened:**
- Competency test administered to 30,000+ primary school teachers
- 21,780 teachers who failed to achieve 70% pass mark were sacked
- 25,000 new teachers recruited as replacements
- 4,042 local government workers sacked as part of restructuring

**Opposition and Controversy:**
- Nigeria Labour Congress (NLC) declared sackings "illegal," citing labour law violations
- NLC alleged competency test was "shrouded in arrant fraud" -- some who "passed" were deceased or retired
- Southern Kaduna communities decried mass sack of civil servants
- Teachers' union called indefinite strike
- Governor subsequently pledged to give sacked teachers a second chance

**Teaching Points:**
- Competency testing as reform tool -- powerful but politically explosive
- Methodological rigor in assessment is critical to legitimacy
- Stakeholder engagement (unions, communities) cannot be an afterthought
- Federal character and regional sensitivities complicate merit-based reforms
- Communication strategy failures can undermine valid reform objectives

### 4.7 Edo State Civil Service Transformation (EDOSTEP)

**Program:** Edo State Civil/Public Service Transformation and Enhancement Programme (EDOSTEP)
- **Goal:** Make Edo a model for sub-nationals in Africa

**Key Reforms:**
- Performance as sole determinant of promotion (replacing nepotism/favouritism)
- Balanced Scorecard as performance measurement tool against set benchmarks
- Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) measuring how well public servants meet tasks
- Merit-based employment with 5,000+ persons recruited through Civil Service Commission
- E-governance (digitization) to eliminate paper in administrative processes
- Competency-based framework for recruitment, promotions, deployment, and training

**Teaching Points:**
- State-level success story demonstrating that Nigerian civil service reform is achievable
- Balanced Scorecard adaptation for public sector context
- KPI-based promotion as alternative to seniority-only advancement
- Digital transformation as enabler of performance transparency

### 4.8 Lagos State Civil Service Modernization

**Key Reforms:**
- Digital literacy training for 1,000+ workers with commitment to global competitiveness
- 4,000 service providers registered online (May 2024-April 2025) for procurement transparency
- "Deepening Technology for a Smart and Agile Public Service" (October 2025)
- Competency-based framework as primary instrument for recruitment, promotions, deployment, and training
- Flexible work-from-home system for eligible officers
- Pension adjustments aligned with revised national minimum wage

**Teaching Points:**
- Lagos demonstrates a comprehensive, multi-dimensional reform approach
- Competency-based framework as cornerstone of modern HR
- Technology adoption combined with human development
- Flexible work arrangements as evidence of genuine culture change

### 4.9 UK-Nigeria Civil Service Partnership

**Partnership:** Agreement between Head of Civil Service of Nigeria and UK Cabinet Secretary
- Bilateral collaboration on reforms to make the civil service "very robust"
- Knowledge exchange on best practices in public service delivery
- Teaching Relevance: International benchmarking model; shows openness to external learning

### 4.10 APER to Performance Management System Transition

**Old System:** Annual Performance Evaluation Report (APER) -- based on Udoji report of 1979
- Annual evaluation of work ethics, skills, capabilities for promotion/training
- Known problems: subjectivity, inflated ratings, political interference, nepotism in promotions

**New System:** Performance Management System (PMS) announced by Head of Civil Service Dr. Folashade Yemi-Esan
- Directed by President Muhammadu Buhari
- Aligned with global best practices
- Designed to reward actual performance
- Digital replacement for paper-based assessment

**Teaching Points:**
- The APER-to-PMS transition illustrates the challenge of modernizing entrenched evaluation systems
- Paper-based systems enable manipulation; digital systems create transparency
- System change must be accompanied by assessor training and culture change

## 6. Professional Body Requirements

### 6.1 CIPM (Chartered Institute of Personnel Management of Nigeria)

**Statutory Authority:**
- Federal Government (May 2026) reaffirmed CIPM as Nigeria's only chartered HR regulatory body
- Head of Civil Service declared: "Only CIPM has the statutory mandate to professionalise HR in Nigeria"
- OHCSF circular mandates professional certification for HR officers in all MDAs

**Public Sector Certification Requirements:**
- Officers in HR roles must possess approved HR certifications
- 12-month moratorium granted for compliance
- After moratorium: only CIPM-certified professionals eligible for HR deployment across the service

**Examination Pathway:**
| Stage | Modules |
|-------|---------|
| Foundation | HR fundamentals |
| Intermediate I | Business Administration, Advanced Economics, Accounting & Finance for Managers, Business Statistics & Social Research |
| Intermediate II | Training and Development, **Public Administration**, Manpower Planning, Comparative Management |
| Professional I | Operational Research, Organisation Behaviour & Performance |
| Professional II | **Nigerian Labour Law**, Advanced Industrial Relations |

**Examination Schedule:** 4 times per year (online)

**Membership Routes:**
- Student Member, Associate Member, Full Member, Fellow
- Alternative routes available for experienced practitioners

**Course Alignment:**
- SABIficate course should prepare learners to meet CIPM public sector requirements
- Modules on Public Administration and Nigerian Labour Law directly relevant
- Course can serve as CPD for existing CIPM members

### 6.2 ASCON Certification

**Mandatory Training Programme:**
- Required for Chief Executive Officers of Federal Government Parastatals and Agencies
- 13 cohorts completed as of October 2025
- 135+ federal Chief Executives trained
- Format: 2-week intensive residential programs at ASCON, Badagry

**Training Coverage:**
- Organizational development and leadership development
- Administrative procedures and project management
- Procurement practices (BPP compliance)
- ICT and entrepreneurship
- Human resource management
- Train-the-trainers programs

**State Government Usage:**
- States regularly send senior staff batches (e.g., Kaduna: 500 senior staff for 2-week programs)
